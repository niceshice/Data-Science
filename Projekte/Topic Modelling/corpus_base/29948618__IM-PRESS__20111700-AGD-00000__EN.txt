The Week Ahead 18-24 April 2011
2011-04-15 - 10:18
Committee meetings – Brussels
Economic governance. Six pieces of legislation on fiscal issues, the Stability and Growth Pact and macroeconomic imbalances in the EU and eurozone will come to a vote in the Economics Committee, with some 1700 amendments on the table (Tuesday).
Food labelling. The Environment Committee will vote at second reading on a law updating EU food information rules. The aim is to ensure both that customers have clear information on what they are buying and that the rules are straightforward for producers to follow (Tuesday).
EP electoral reform. A package of reforms to the EU laws governing elections to the European Parliament will be put to the vote in the Constitutional Affairs Committee. The ideas under discussion include the introduction of transnational party lists and the introduction of a mathematical formula to distribute EP seats among the Member States (Tuesday).
Transparency register/codes of conduct. The same committee will vote on an agreement with the European Commission to create a common “transparency register” for lobbyists and others aiming to influence EU decisions. Separately, proposals to tighten Parliament’s own codes of conduct for lobbyists and for MEPs are being drawn up by a working group led by EP President Jerzy Buzek which holds its first formal meeting (both events Tuesday).
Women in business leadership. The Women’s Rights and Gender Equality Committee will vote on a series of ideas aiming to increase the proportion of women in management-level positions in companies (Wednesday).
Easter recess. Parliament’s offices will be closed for the Easter holidays from 21 to 25 April inclusive. Urgent press questions between these dates should be addressed to Michaela Findeis on +32 498 983 332.
