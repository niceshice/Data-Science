The Week Ahead – 3-9 December 2007
Institutions
2007-11-30 - 10:18
Political groups week – Brussels
Polish Prime Minister. Donald Tusk, the new Polish Prime Minister, will join a meeting of the political group leaders. He will also have a bilateral meeting with EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering, followed by an informal press briefing. (Tuesday) Future of Europe. For the fourth time, national MPs will join MEPs in Brussels for a two-day series of debates on the future of Europe, this time focussing on the Lisbon Treaty. (Monday-Tuesday) Europa – Quo Vadis? The future of Europe will also be the subject of a televised debate organised by the European Parliament and the Charlemagne Prize Foundation with President Pöttering, 2007 Charlemagne Prize Winner Javier Solana and former winners Bronisław Geremek and Pat Cox. (Wednesday) President’s diary. The President of Croatia, Stjepan Mesic, the Prime Minister of Georgia, Lado Gurgenidze, (both Wednesday) and the Foreign Minister of Egypt, Ahmed Aboul Gheit, (Thursday) will all be meeting President Pöttering. Informal press briefings are scheduled after each meeting. EU-Africa Summits. President Pöttering will join 25 other MEPs in Lisbon for a Parliamentary summit with the Pan-African Parliament. He and his PAP counterpart, Gertrude Mongella, will both then address the summit of EU and African Heads of State or Government. (Thursday-Sunday) Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 10-13 December. The agenda includes the proclamation of the Charter of Fundamental Rights, the presentation of the Sakharov Prize to Sudanese lawyer Salih Mahmoud Osman and an address from the King of Jordan. Legislation due for vote includes the reform of the wine market and the EU budget for 2008, plus the air quality rules and the remaining part of the rail interoperability package. Pre-session press briefing. Changes to the agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be presented at the press briefing at 11am on Friday.
