This week in the EP: food prices, unbundling, sport, Afghanistan
Institutions
2008-05-05 - 14:22
Watch the plenary on Wednesday and Thursday
The first week of May is a busy one for MEPs, with committees meeting to discuss issues ranging from Tibet to time share holidays and from rising food prices to the separation of electricity production and supply. On Wednesday and Thursday the plenary session will look at human rights, lobbying, 10 years of economic and monetary union and sport. Parliament delegations were in the US and Afghanistan last week - catch up with news from them on Tuesday.
Members of the Development Committee will discuss rising food prices in the world and the impact on developing countries with UN Special Advisor Professor Jeffrey Sachs . Watch live from 1500 CET. On Tuesday the Development and Women's Rights Committees hold a hearing on how women are involved in and affected by war.
Among the important votes in committee this week, the industry committee will vote on the electricity directive Tuesday. A key issue is "ownership unbundling" - the separation of supply and production. Also Tuesday the Internal Market Committee votes on time share protection and the Environment Committee votes on food additives and water quality standards.
MEPs will debate and vote on the annual report on human rights, lobbying, 10 years of economic and monetary union and a report on sports policy during the plenary . Watch live from Wednesday at 1500 CET.
Afghanistan, Climate change talks in the US
Two EP delegations are just back from a fact finding mission to Afghanistan and from climate change talks in the US, read more about their experiences on Tuesday.
What are the objectives and implications of the directive on the return of immigrants? Find out more on Thursday in our report from the exchange of views with MEPs and journalists, participating in a seminar for journalists on immigration . Parliament is set to vote on legislation in Strasbourg in June.
Europe Day
Friday is Europe Day, when the EU commemorates the declaration by French Foreign Minister Robert Schuman on 9 May 1950 calling on France, Germany and other European countries to pool their coal and steel production.
