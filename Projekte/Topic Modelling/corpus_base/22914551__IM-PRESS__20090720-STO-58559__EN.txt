Former Lithuanian Head of State Landsbergis is just one MEP who has his own pigeon hole for mail The entrance to the Parliament's hemicycle Chamber in Strasbourg. Where's my seat? The newly elected MEPs are arranged so that they sit in blocs with other Members of the same beliefs - not of the same nationality. 14 July: The newly elected MEPs assemble e in the Chamber in Strasbourg for the first time. Roughly half have been elected for the first time. 14 July: The votes for Parliament's new President are taken away and counted, the winner was elected with 555 votes out of the 736. Born in 1940, Poland's Jerzy Buzek will be the first holder of the post to come from Central or Eastern Europe. An MEP listens to the acceptance speech of Parliament's new President Jerzy Buzek where he pledged to make the work of the Institution more interesting for the public. Wednesday 15 July, the debates start in earnest. Between now and the next election there will be an estimated 300 days of debates in Strasbourg and Brussels. After a long election campaign newly elected MEPs assembled for the first time in Strasbourg on 13-16 July. As well as finding out where they will be sitting in the new Chamber, Members had to elect a new President or Speaker and other senior office holders. The session also saw political blocs being officially formed - bringing together MEPs from different countries but with broadly similar beliefs. Some of this can be seen here in this slideshow.
