Former Irish MEP Gene Fitzgerald
On 14 December 2007, former Irish MEP Gene Fitzgerald passed away at the age of 75 in his native Cork. During his career, Gene had been involved in politics at local, national and European level. He was Minister for Labour and Minister for Finance in two separate Irish Governments during the early 1980s and he was of course an active member of the European Parliament from 1984 until 1994.
During his time in the European Parliament, Gene was a member of the European Democratic Alliance Group. He was Vice-Chairman of the Committee on Social Affairs and Employment, and a member of the Committee on Institutional Affairs, the Committee on Petitions and the Committee on Regional Policy, Regional Planning and Relations with Regional and Local Authorities. He was also Vice-Chairman of the Delegation for relations with Canada and a member of the Delegation for relations with the countries of South America, the Delegation for relations with the Member States of ASEAN and the ASEAN Interparliamentary Organisation and the Republic of Korea, the Delegation for relations with Australia and New Zealand and the Delegation for relations with the United States.

He subsequently became Minister for Labour again in 1982.
Gene is survived by his wife Noreen and five children.
