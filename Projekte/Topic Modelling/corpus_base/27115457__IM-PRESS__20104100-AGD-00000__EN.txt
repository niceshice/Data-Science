The Week Ahead 4-10 October 2010
2010-11-22 - 16:59
Plenary session and committee meetings – Brussels
Brussels session:
Biodiversity. MEPs debate the loss of animal and plant species as well as EU strategy for the forthcoming Nagoya meeting (debate Wednesday, vote on resolution Thursday).
20 years since German reunification. To mark the anniversary, MEPs hear from two speakers: Lothar de Maizière, the only democratically-elected Prime Minister of East Germany, and former Commission President Jacques Delors (Thursday).
Visa-free travel. Parliament will decide if Albanian and Bosnian citizens will be allowed to travel to the EU without visas from January (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday).
Timor Leste. President José Ramos Horta will address MEPs. He will also have a meeting and give a joint press conference with EP President Jerzy Buzek (Wednesday).
Other parliamentary business:
EU Budget 2011. The Conference of Presidents meets to consider Parliament’s approach to negotiations with the Council on the 2011 EU Budget (Tuesday).
Committee chairs meet Commission. The 23 chairs of Parliament’s committees will have debate priorities with the 27 Commissioners (Thursday).
Maternity Leave. The Women’s Rights and Employment Committees will discuss an impact assessment on the costs and benefits of extending maternity leave rights (Tuesday).
Late payments. The Internal Market Committee will vote on new rules to ensure large companies and public bodies pay their bills on time (Tuesday).
Sakharov Prize. The nominees for the 2010 Prize for Freedom of Thought will be discussed at a meeting of the Foreign Affairs Committee and the Subcommittee on Human Rights (Tuesday).
Transparency register. EP Vice-President Diana Wallis and Commission Vice-President Maroš Šefčovic join a seminar on plans for a common register for lobbyists (Wednesday).
Jerzy Buzek’s diary. The EP President will meet the UK Deputy Prime Minister Nick Clegg (Tuesday) and will take part in a ceremony in memory of Russian journalist Anna Politkovskaya, who was murdered in 2006 (Wednesday).
