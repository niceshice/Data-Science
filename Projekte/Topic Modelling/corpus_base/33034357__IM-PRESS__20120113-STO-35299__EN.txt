Orbán in EP Wednesday to discuss latest developments in Hungary
European Council
2012-01-18 - 12:23
Photo of computer showing streaming As Hungary faces legal action by the European Commission over laws concerning the central bank, judiciary and data protection authority, Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán will be in the Parliament Wednesday afternoon to discuss the situation with MEPs.
European Commission President Jose Manuel Barroso and Nicolai Wammen for the Danish Presidency will also take part in the debate. You can watch live by clicking on the link to the right and you will find a story about the debate online later this afternoon.
The Commission announced Tuesday that it would launch infringement proceedings against Hungary for failing to guarantee the respect of EU law on three issues: the indepedence of the central bank, the retirement age of judges and the independence of the data protection authority and would ask for more information about the independence of the Hungarian judiciary.
