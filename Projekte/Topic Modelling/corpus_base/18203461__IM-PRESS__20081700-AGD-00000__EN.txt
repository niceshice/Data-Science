The Week Ahead 21-27 April 2008
2008-04-18 - 09:06
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Transatlantic relations. MEPs will debate, with Commission and Council, May’s EU-Latin America/Caribbean summit and the US visa-waiver programme for EU citizens (Wednesday).
Slovenian President. Danilo Türk, President of the Republic of Slovenia, addresses MEPs in a formal sitting. He will join EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering for a press conference (Wednesday).
Galileo. After reaching agreement with Council, Parliament looks set to give the final go-ahead for the EU’s satellite navigation system (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Discharge 2006. Parliament will decide whether to approve the EU institutions’ financial management in 2006 (debate and vote Tuesday).
Organ transplants. Parliament will debate ways to improve cooperation on organ transplantation in the EU – and to tackle trafficking and ‘transplant tourism’ (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Ombudsman. MEPs will consider changes to the powers of the EU Ombudsman (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Foreign Affairs. Parliament will debate the situation in Burma with representatives of Council and Commission (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) and consider a report on the effect of Chinese policies in Africa (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Food prices. Unrest caused by rising food prices will be the subject of a debate with Commission and Council (Tuesday).
EU/FYROM, EU/Turkey. MEPs will vote on the progress made by the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia towards EU membership (Wednesday). Away from the plenary, the Foreign Affairs Committee is set to vote on Turkey’s progress (Monday).
ECB/Papademos. ECB Vice-President Lucas Papademos will present his institution’s annual report to the Economics Committee (Monday).
EP/Lithuania. President Pöttering will make an official visit to Lithuania, including meetings with the President and Prime Minister plus a speech to the Seimas (Thursday-Friday).
