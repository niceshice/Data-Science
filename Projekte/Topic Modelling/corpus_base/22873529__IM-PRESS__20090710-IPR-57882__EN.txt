The Week Ahead 13-19 July 2009
Institutions
2009-07-10 - 10:33
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Election of the President. The inaugural plenary session of the 7th directly elected European Parliament will open on Tuesday morning with an election to choose the Parliament’s President for the next two and a half years. Outgoing President Hans-Gert Pöttering will be in the chair until this election is complete.
Election of the Vice-presidents and Quaestors. MEPs will also elect 14 Vice-Presidents (Tuesday) and 5 Quaestors (Wednesday).
Debates with the Czech and Swedish Presidencies. Czech Prime Minister Jan Fischer will debate the results of his country’s six month presidency of the Council with MEPs, then a second debate will see Swedish Prime Minister Fredrik Reinfeldt discuss his priorities for the next six months (Wednesday).
Iran, China and Honduras. MEPs will debate the situation following the contested elections in Iran, the ethnic unrest in China and the political crisis in Honduras with the Council and Commission (Wednesday).
Inaugural committee meetings. The plenary will decide on the size and then the precise membership of the 20 committees and 2 sub-committees (Wednesday and Thursday). A number of committees will hold their inaugural meetings, electing their chairs and vice-chairs, on Thursday in Strasbourg. The remaining committees will hold their inaugural meetings the following Monday in Brussels.
30th anniversary of direct EP elections. Before the session begins, a ceremony will take place in front of the Louise Weiss building marking 30 years since the first directly elected European Parliament took office in 1979 (Monday).
