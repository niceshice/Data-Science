14-18 July in parliament: terrorism, Zimbabwe, budget
Institutions
2008-07-14 - 11:52
Environment MEPs vote on tighter vehicle emissions Tuesday Incitement to commit terrorism and the situation in Zimbabwe are just two things on the agenda this week in parliament. Also being discussed by MEPs in committee meetings will be air pollution and the effectiveness of the EU's structural funds. The week also sees various French Ministers appear before parliaments committees as they set out the policies of France's presidency of the European Union.
Terrorism/personal data: MEPs on the Civil Liberties Committee are considering a report which looks at whether to make incitement to commit terrorist acts a criminal offence. Another report being discussed and put to the vote Tuesday will be on the protection of personal data when the police and judiciary from different countries cooperate with one another.
Environment/Rules on vehicles: Tuesday sees Members of the Environment Committee vote on a proposed new regulation that will impose tighter environmental standards on vehicles. Here on parliament's website we will be looking at efforts by EU members to try and cut emissions from vehicles.
Structural funds: A hearing on Thursday by the Regional Development Committee will examine how effective the EUÂ´s structural funds are.
Zimbabwe: As turmoil continues to engulf Zimbabwe the spokesman in Europe for the main opposition group - the Movement for Democratic Change (MDC) - will be in parliament. Mr Hebson Makuvise will meet MEPs on the Development Committee Tuesday.
Budget: A delegation of MEPs will hold talks with representatives of the Council on Thursday view to reaching a preliminary agreement on the 2009 budget.
