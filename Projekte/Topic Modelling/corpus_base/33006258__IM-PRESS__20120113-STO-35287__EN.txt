This week in Parliament: election of president, Hungary, Draghi
Institutions
2012-01-16 - 11:08
Outside of one of the buildings in Strasbourg The first plenary sitting of the year marks the mid-point of the current parliamentary term and means the election of a new president and vice-presidents. Other major issues on the agenda this week are recent political developments in Hungary and the presentation of the Danish presidency program for the first half of this year.
Three candidates have declared themselves so far in the presidential race - German Socialist Martin Schulz , British Liberal Diana Wallis and British Conservative Nirj Deva . Five quaestors and 14 vice-presidents will also be elected this week.

The EP will get its first chance to have a say on the planned fiscal stability union Wednesday when it sets down its demands and concerns about the agreement for a reinforced economic union to tackle the crisis.
Key legislation on the agenda includes rules to improve the collection and recycling of electric and electrical waste , stricter checks on pest control products and proposals to cut the EU's massive food wastage .
Outside plenary
ECB chief Mario Draghi will meet MEPs on the Economics Committee Monday evening in his capacity as chair of the European Systemic Risk Board, which monitors the build up of potential economic imbalances. The focus will be on competitiveness disparities among eurozone economies. The meeting comes just after Standard and Poor's downgraded the credit ratings of nine European countries including France and Austria, which lost their AAA rating.
