The Week Ahead 11-17 October 2010
2010-10-14 - 12:33
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Death Penalty and the case of Sakineh Mohammadi Ashtiani . The Human-Rights Subcommittee holds a hearing on the occasion of the World Day against the Death Penalty. The subcommittee will also hear from the lawyer representing Sakineh Mohammadi Ashtiani, sentenced to death in Iran (Thursday).
EP President visits Cyprus. Jerzy Buzek makes an official visit to Cyprus, including meetings with the President, Demetris Christofias, leading political figures and an address to the Cypriot Parliament. He will also meet leaders of the Turkish Cypriot community (Monday-Tuesday).
EP-Pakistan. The Foreign Minister of Pakistan, Shah Mehmood Qureshi, will have a discussion with the Foreign Affairs Committee (Thursday).
EP-Ukraine. Mr Buzek’s diary also includes a meeting in Brussels with the Prime Minister of Ukraine, Mykola Azarov (Wednesday, with press point).
Journalism Prize. The EP President will present the awards at this year’s European Parliament Prize for Journalism (Wednesday).
Plenary preparations. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 18-21 October plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda includes the main vote on the 2011 EU Budget, legislation on the staffing and budget of the External Action Service, maternity leave, hedge funds and late payment of invoices. President Buzek will give a mid-term address, and Parliament will also hold a formal sitting to hear from UN Secretary General Ban Ki-moon.
Pre-session press briefing. Parliament’s press service and the main political group spokespeople will be on hand to comment on the agenda and any changes adopted by the Conference of Presidents at the regular briefing at 11am on Friday (Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).
