The Week Ahead 28 September - 4 October 2009
2009-09-25 - 11:12
Committee meetings - Brussels
Economic crisis. ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet and Eurogroup President Jean-Claude Juncker will debate euro zone economic developments and financial regulation plans in the light of the G20 meeting (Monday and Tuesday).
Lorry drivers' working time. The Employment Committee could adopt a report proposing that Parliament reject the Commission's proposed directive on lorry drivers' working hours. Parliament wants self-employed drivers included in its scope (Tuesday).
Fuel efficiency of tyres . A deal could be struck with the Council under which tyres would be classified and labelled according to their fuel efficiency (Thursday).
Discharge for the Council. The Budgetary Control Committee is to vote again on whether to grant the Council a discharge for the 2007accounts (Thursday).
2010 budget. The Budgets Committee will vote on first-reading amendments, with a view to a vote by Parliament as a whole at the October plenary session (Monday to Wednesday).
Telecoms conciliation. Parliament will choose its negotiators among members of its conciliation delegation for the telecoms package.
Moldova . EP President Jerzy Buzek will meet the Republic of Moldova's Prime Minister Vlad Filat. A press point will take place after this meeting (Tuesday, at about 16.45h). Mr. Filat will also meet MEPs from the Foreign Affairs Committee.
EP President visits Latvia and Lithuania. President Buzek will make official visits to Latvia (Thursday ) and Lithuania (Friday) . Human rights in Russia. The Human Rights Sub-Committee will hear Stanislav Dmitrievsky, editor-in-chief of the Russian human rights journal Pravo-zashchita (Wednesday).
Sakharov prize. MEPs and political groups will present 10 candidates for the 2009 prize, at a joint meeting of the development and foreign Affairs committees (Wednesday).
Inter-parliamentary delegations. The delegations to the new multilateral Assembly with the Eastern Partnership countries (Euronest) and the Euro-Mediterranean (Euromed) Assembly are to elect their chairs, as are the delegations for relations with China, India, Belarus, Ukraine, Iran and Iraq (Tuesday and Wednesday).

Like the European energy label, the tyre label would use fuel-efficiency classes ranging from best-performance (green “A” class) to worst (red “G” class). It would also show the tyre's wet grip and noise performance.
