Constituency Week
Josep Borrell in Greece. The President of the European Parliament will make an official visit to Greece, during which he will meet the President, Prime Minister, Foreign Minister and the Speaker of the Greek Parliament. He will also address a meeting of three parliamentary committees and hold a press conference . (Monday and Tuesday)
Electoral observation - Congo and Nicaragua. An EP delegation will be in the Democratic Republic of Congo to observe the second round of the presidential elections, which takes place on 29 October. Another delegation will travel to Nicaragua to observe the presidential and legislative elections of 5 November.
MEPs in their constituencies. The week beginning 30 October is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies, so there are no official meetings of parliamentary bodies in Brussels. Parliament's offices will be closed from Wednesday to Friday for the All Saints holiday.

Luis HERRER-TEJEDOR (EPP-ED, ES) 09:00 18:30 Athens, Greece Observation delegation - MEPs to monitor run-off in Congo presidential elections The European Parliament is sending a delegation to Congo to witness the second round of presidential elections there on 29 October, as it did for the previous round. The nine-strong delegation will be part of the overall EU Election Observation Mission. The MEPs will be travelling to the Democratic Republic of Congo on 26 October and will hold a press conference on 1 November in the capital, Kinshasa. The first round of presidential elections on 30 July resulted in a run-off between incumbent president Joseph Kabila and current vice-president Jean-Pierre Bemba. Jürgen Schröder (EPP-ED, DE), who is leading the delegation, called on all parties concerned to ensure that the elections would be fair and peaceful. The other members of the European Parliament delegation are Filip Kaczmarek (EPP-ED, PL), Richard Howitt (PES, UK), Robert Evans (PES, UK), Ulrich Stockmann (PES, DE), Javier Pomes Ruiz (PES, ES), Fiona Hall (ALDE, UK), Marie-Hélène Aubert (Greens/EFA, FR) and Koen Dillen (NI, BE). The overall EU Mission is also led by an MEP, Philippe Morillon (ALDE, FR).

Luis HERRER-TEJEDOR (EPP-ED, ES) 09:00 18:30 Athens, Greece 2006-10-29 The President's diary Official visit to Greece . 09:30 18:30 Greece Delegations Observation delegation MEPs to monitor run-off in Congo presidential elections
The European Parliament is sending a delegation to Congo to witness the second round of presidential elections there on 29 October, as it did for the previous round. The nine-strong delegation will be part of the overall EU Election Observation Mission.
The MEPs will be travelling to the Democratic Republic of Congo on 26 October and will hold a press conference on 1 November in the capital, Kinshasa. The first round of presidential elections on 30 July resulted in a run-off between incumbent president Joseph Kabila and current vice-president Jean-Pierre Bemba.
Jürgen Schröder (EPP-ED, DE), who is leading the delegation, called on all parties concerned to ensure that the elections would be fair and peaceful, "in order to do justice to the dignity and the determination the Congolese people showed in July when they seized the opportunity to participate in democratic elections for the first time in forty years."
The other members of the European Parliament delegation are Filip Kaczmarek (EPP-ED, PL), Richard Howitt (PES, UK), Robert Evans (PES, UK), Ulrich Stockmann (PES, DE), Javier Pomes Ruiz (PES, ES), Fiona Hall (ALDE, UK), Marie-Hélène Aubert (Greens/EFA, FR) and Koen Dillen (NI, BE). The overall EU Mission is also led by an MEP, Philippe Morillon (ALDE, FR). 09:00 23:59 The Congo
