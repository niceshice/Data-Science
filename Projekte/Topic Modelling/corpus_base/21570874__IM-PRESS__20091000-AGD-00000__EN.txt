The Week Ahead 2-8 March 2009
2009-02-27 - 09:39
Committee meetings and political groups week
Women/European elections. Ahead of International Women’s Day on Sunday, EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and other MEPs will join national MPs for a debate on women and the European elections (Thursday). The EP Press Service is organising a debate with journalists and MEPs (Wednesday).
Hillary Rodham Clinton. The US Secretary of State will join President Pöttering for a "town hall" style meeting with young Europeans (Friday).
Seal products. The Internal Market Committee considers legislation which could ban or more closely regulate trade in seal products (Monday) . The proposal originates from a Parliament resolution in 2006.
Financial reforms. A discussion with Peter Praet of the ECB Banking Supervision Committee gives the Economics Committee a first chance to debate the de Larosière report on financial market supervision (Monday).
President’s activities. President Pöttering will be meet both Nika Gilauri, Prime Minister of Georgia, and Sergei Stanishev, Prime Minister of Bulgaria (Thursday).
Altiero Spinelli anniversary. MEPs, including President Pöttering, will hold an event to celebrate the life and work of Altiero Spinelli. (Thursday).
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 9-12 March plenary session in Strasbourg. The main items on the agenda include a debate on preparations for the EU Spring Summit, including a vote on Parliament’s reaction to the Commission’s economic recovery plan. MEPs will hold the final vote on the maritime package and will decide on the ‘eurovignette’ proposal to include charge lorries for road use partly on the basis of the pollution.
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda will be announced at the regular press briefing. (Friday midday, Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).

Members of the Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection will hear from a number of experts about the issues surrounding the consumer rights directive. The proposal made last year by the European Commission is currently being examined by MEPs, with conclusions to be included in a report drawn up by Arlene McCarthy (PSE, UK). The hearing will be divided into two thematic sessions, both followed by a questions and answers time. In the first part it will focus on harmonisation of law related to cross-border buying and selling transactions, whereas the latter will deal with European contract law. Among the invited experts there will be representatives of the European Consumer Centre, consumer organisations from various Member States and SMEs. 16:00 18:30 Brussels, JAN 2Q2 Watch webstreaming live 2009-03-03 Press conferences Today's press conferences 11:00 - Intellectual Property Rights - Manuel MEDINA ORTEGA (PES, ES), Jacques TOUBON (EPP-ED, FR) and a delegation of European artists

MEPs and researchers will assess the challenges to scientific cooperation between the EU and developing countries at a conference organised by Parliament's scientific research panel STOA and the EP's Development Committee. Participants will discuss issues like the sustainable management of natural resources, the use of genetically modified organisms in agriculture, and the development of new or improved drugs and vaccines against HIV/AIDS, malaria and tuberculosis.
Speakers include:
- Mosibudi Mangena, Minister of Science and Technology of the Republic of South Africa

- Dr Leena Srivastava of the Energy and Resources Institute in New Delhi, India
- Dr Pedro Castanera of the Spanish National Institute for Agricultural and Food Research and Technology 10:30 17:00 PHS 3C50 Programme of the event Equal opportunities at the heart of the European elections? In parallel with the European Parliament’s meeting with national parliaments to mark International Women’s Day, the EP Press Service is organising a seminar with journalists on gender issues surrounding the elections.
Chaired by EP Vice-President Rodi Kratsa-Tsaragopoulou (EPP-ED, EL) and moderated by BBC TV presenter Shirin Wheeler, the event focuses on women’s perception of the European elections, future perspectives on action in the field of equal opportunities and the role of the European Parliament in this area. It will also see the presentation of the results of Eurobarometer research on women’s participation in European election.
Commission Vice-President Margot Wallström and Women’s Rights Committee Chair Anna Záborská (EPP-ED, SK) will also be taking part along with writers, broadcasters, academics and other MEPs.

Hillary Rodham Clinton, Secretary of State of the United States of America, will join European Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering at Parliament in Brussels this Friday, 6 March, for a discussion with an audience of young Europeans.
The “town hall” style event with questions and answers will be preceded by a bilateral meeting with President Pöttering, who will then introduce Secretary Clinton to the audience.
Access to the event is strictly by invitation only. Due to space restrictions, a pooling arrangement will be in place for audiovisual media wishing to cover the event. Europe by Satellite (EbS) will cover the Town Hall live and it will also be streamed on Parliament’s EPLive website and on EuroparlTV . Print journalists are advised to follow the discussion via EbS or in the EP Press Room.
Audiovisual media wishing to take part in the pool for coverage of arrivals/greetings and the Town Hall itself should contact Kirsten van Kampen in the EP Audiovisual Unit ( AVaccreditations@europarl.europa.eu , tel. +32 2 284 48 17 / +32 498 98 35 44 ) before 1800 on Wednesday. 10:00 11:45 Brussels, PHS building, Yehudi Menuhin hall
