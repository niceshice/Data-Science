2007-04-13 - 10:42
Political groups week - Brussels
Rights of the child. Seven EP committees are working together to hold a special public hearing on an EU strategy on the rights of the child. MEPs and experts from NGOs will be joined by children in the plenary chamber in Brussels to debate a range of issues from violence against children to child poverty (Tuesday). Energy policy and candidate countries. MEPs will join national MPs from Croatia, Turkey and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia to debate energy policy (Monday and Tuesday). He will meet President Georgi Parvanov, Prime Minister Sergey Stanishev and the Parliamentary Speaker, Georgi Pirinski, among others, as well as addressing the Bulgarian Parliament. (Thursday and Friday) EP/United States. A delegation of MEPs from the Civil Liberties Committee will travel to Washington DC for meetings with the US administration and Congress on transatlantic data protection issues, including air passenger data and the SWIFT case. (Monday- Wednesday) Citizens Forums. The latest in Parliament's meetings enabling members of the public to join the debate on the future of Europe take place in Aachen (Monday) and Stockholm (Thursday). Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary of 23-26 April. The main items on the agenda include rules on advanced therapy medicinal products, a package of improvements of maritime safety regulations, a debate with Council and Commission on transatlantic relations and the annual report on human rights around the world. There will also be a formal address from the President of India, Dr A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the usual press briefing at 11am on Friday 20 April.
