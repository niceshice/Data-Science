The Week Ahead 28 February – 6 March 2011
2011-02-25 - 09:58
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
North Africa and the Middle East. The Foreign Affairs Committee will hear from Egyptian opposition figure Ayman Nour (Monday, with Human Rights Subcommittee) , and from Commissioner Stefan Füle and EEAS Secretary General Pierre Vimont about the EU’s response to the uprisings (Monday). The Civil Liberties Committee will hear from the Council Presidency and EU borders agency Frontex on migration issues (Tuesday).
Union for the Mediterranean Assembly. The protests will also feature on the agenda of the Rome plenary session of the assembly, which brings together MEPs, led by EP President Jerzy Buzek, with parliamentarians from the EU’s Mediterranean neighbours (Thursday-Friday).
Kyrgyz President. Rosa Otunbayeva, President of Kyrgyzstan, will meet the Foreign Affairs Committee and Jerzy Buzek (Tuesday).
GMOs – national restrictions. The Environment Committee discusses legislation which would allow Member States more scope to ban cultivation of genetically modified crops (Monday).
International Women’s Day. Ahead of 8 March, the Women’s Right’s Committee holds a debate on women in EU politics, with participants including President Buzek and former Irish President Mary Robinson (Thursday).
EP President – Vatican. Jerzy Buzek makes an official visit to Vatican City, including an audience with Pope Benedict. While in Rome, he will also meet Mario Draghi, governor of the Bank of Italy (Monday).
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote the week to plans for the 7-10 March session. The agenda includes consumer rights legislation, the proposed treaty change for the eurozone stability mechanism and assessments of the EU membership prospects for Turkey and Montenegro. Parliament will mark the 100th International Women’s Day and there will be a debates on the situation in the Middle East and on EU-Iran relations, plus a formal address by the President of Hungary.
Pre-session press briefing. The regular press briefing with the EP Press Service and the main political group spokespeople is on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).
