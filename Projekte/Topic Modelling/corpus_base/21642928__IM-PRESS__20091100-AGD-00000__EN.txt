The Week Ahead 9-15 March 2009
2009-03-06 - 09:46
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Economic crisis. MEPs debate and vote on reports on the Commission’s recovery plan, on employment policy guidelines and on cohesion policy, plus resolutions giving Parliament’s position ahead of the Spring European Council (debate and votes Wednesday). In addition, the Economics Committee votes at first reading on changes to the capital requirements directive to improve the supervision of the banking sector (Monday).
Maritime package. Votes on six conciliation agreements and two second-reading agreements with Council should bring into law a set of measures to further improve maritime safety (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Eurovignette. MEPs will decide whether to give Member States the option to base road charges for lorries partly on the basis of the air and noise pollution – and the congestion – they cause (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Industrial pollution. Rules to reduce pollution from industrial installations come to first reading (debate and vote Tuesday).
EU enlargement. The latest progress reports on Turkey, Croatia and the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia will be debated and put to the vote (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday).
Access to documents. Parliament will decide on new rules to widen public access to the EU institutions’ documents (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Asylum system. MEPs are set to debate and vote on the future of the European Common Asylum System (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Social situation of the Roma. A report considering how to improve access to the labour market for the Roma will be put to the vote (brief presentation Monday, vote Tuesday).
Roaming charges. The Industry Committee votes on plans to extend in time the EU-cap on excessive charges for roaming with mobile phones and to include a cap on text message charges (Monday).
Cross-border healthcare. The Environment Committee is due to vote at first reading on patients’ rights to seek medical treatment outside their own Member State ( Thursday).

13.30 Management Forum DZ Bank 10:00 14:00 Luxembourg 2009-03-15 The President's diary 18.00 Meeting of the Bureau of the Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly (EMPA) 18:00 18:30 Brussels Delegations Election observation mission to El Salvador
On Sunday 15 March 2009, a 7-strong delegation of MEPs will observe the presidential election. The EP is sending an election observation team headed by José Ignacio Salafranca (EPP-ED, ES) . On Election Day, three teams are expected to be deployed around the country.
Ahead of the ballot, MEPs will meet the candidates, the Supreme Electoral Tribunal President, Walter Araujo and civil society representatives.
MEPs are expected to give a Press conference jointly with the Chief Observer of the EU Electoral Observation mission on Tuesday 17 March.
The delegation is composed of Fernando Fernandez Martin (EPP-ED, ES), José Manuel García-Margallo Y Marfil (EPP-ED, ES), José Ignacio Salafranca (EPP-ED, ES), Armando França (PES, PT), Emilio Menéndez del Valle (PSE, ES), Mikel Irujo Amezaga (Greens/EFA) and Pedro Guerreiro (GUE/NGL, PT). 09:00 19:00 El Salvador, San Salvador Other events Plenary session of Euro-Med Assembly
The situation in the Middle East and the impact of the world financial crisis on the Mediterranean region will be the main points for debate at the fifth plenary session of the Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly (EMPA) taking place from 15 to 17 March at the European Parliament in Brussels, beginning with a meeting of the Bureau on Sunday.
Following the plenary session, Abdel Hadi Al-Majali, Speaker of the Jordanian Parliament, will take the reins of EMPA for a year (until March 2010), succeeding Hans-Gert Pöttering, President of the European Parliament and current president of EMPA.
EMPA may also decide to welcome, as new members of the Assembly, Albania, Mauritania and Monaco, which are also members of the Union for the Mediterranean.
EMPA brings together 260 parliamentarians. Of these, 130 represent the European Union (49 Members of the EP and 81 Members appointed by the national parliaments of the EU's 27 Member States) and 130 represent the national parliaments of the Mediterranean partner countries which are not members of the EU: Algeria, Egypt, Jordan, Israel, Lebanon, Morocco, the Palestinian Authority, Syria, Tunisia and Turkey. 15:00 20:00 Brussels, European Parliament
