2008-04-11 - 12:27
Committee and political group meetings - Brussels
Preparing the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparing for the 21-24 April plenary session in Strasbourg. Highlights will include a Commission statement on totalitarianism, combating organ shortages and "transplant tourism", and the 2006 discharge. Also important are the EU-Latin America and Caribbean summit , the follow-up to last month's EU-US ministerial troika, road haulage and the international carriage of passengers by coach and bus.

Women and science. The Women's Rights Committee is to adopt a report voicing concern about the under-representation of women in the scientific world (Monday).
EU and Muslim women. The situation of Muslim women and the role of the EU will be debated by experts and NGO representatives at a Women's Rights Committee hearing (Thursday).
Trade. Economic Partnership Agreements (EPAs), currently being negotiated to renew the partnership with African, Caribbean and Pacific states will be debated at an International Trade Committee workshop attended by Commissioner Mandelson (Thursday).
Kosovo. A delegation of six MEPs led by Doris Pack (EPP-ED, DE), will visit Pristina to meet the President, Prime Minister and Speaker of the Kosovo Parliament (Thursday to Saturday).
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda made by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular briefing at 11am on Friday.
