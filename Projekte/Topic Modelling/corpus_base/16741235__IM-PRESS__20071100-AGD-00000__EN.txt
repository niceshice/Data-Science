The Week Ahead – 12-18 March 2007
2007-03-09 - 10:29
Plenary Session, Strasbourg
Berlin Declaration and European Council. The German Foreign Minister will update MEPs on preparations for the Berlin Declaration to mark the 50th anniversary of the Treaty of Rome and join a second debate on the outcome of the EU summit in Brussels on 8-9 March. (Wednesday)
Aviation. MEPs will debate a Commission statement on the ‘open skies’ agreement with the USA (Tuesday) and a first-reading report on the European Aviation Safety Agency (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday). Amid concern over job losses at Airbus, there will also be a debate on the future of aircraft construction in Europe. (Wednesday)
Equal opportunities. In the week after International Women’s Day, MEPs will consider a report from the Women’s Rights Committee on the “road map” for equality between men and women over the coming years. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Online music. A report critical of the Commission’s plans to open the music copyright field to competition will be put to the vote. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Euromed. Ahead of the Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly meeting in Tunis on 16-17 March, MEPs will debate Euro-Mediterranean relations with the Commission and Council, and consider a report from the Trade Committee on a Euro-Med free-trade area. (debates Wednesday, vote on report Thursday). EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering is among those participating in the Tunis meeting, with intercultural dialogue high on the agenda. (Friday - Saturday)
Committee meetings. Away from the plenary, the Budgets Committee will vote on a report on the future of the own resources system for funding the EU budget. The Foreign Affairs Committee meanwhile is set to adopt a report on Kosovo. (both Monday)
European Day for Victims of Terrorism. Before the official start of the plenary, Parliament will mark the annual day in memory of victims of terrorism with 500 young people joining President Pöttering and Commissioner Vladimir Spidla among others for a debate in the plenary chamber. (Monday)

Fouad Mebazaa, President-in-Office of EMPA and Speaker of the Tunisian Chamber of Deputies, will open the proceedings.
Intercultural dialogue between Europe and the Islamic world will be on the agenda of the Assembly, which will also discuss and adopt a report on the implementation of the code of conduct on the fight against terrorism. European Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering, German Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs in charge of European Affairs, Gunter Gloser, and European Commission President José Manuel Barroso will speak at the opening session.
EMPA, which meets once a year in plenary session, is a joint assembly: it has 120 MPs representing the European Union (45 Members of the European Parliament and 75 Members appointed by the parliaments of the EU Member States) and 120 representing the national parliaments of the non-EU Mediterranean countries.
During its debates the Assembly uses French, English and Arabic as working languages. Texts to be adopted are also available in Hebrew, Turkish and all the official languages of the European Union. 09:00 20:00 Tunis, Tunisia Third ordinary session of the Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly Other events EP Citizen's Forum on the Future of Europe As part of its contribution to the debate on the future of Europe and the period of reflection following the 'no' votes on the constitution in France and the Netherlands, the European Parliament is organising a series of citizen's forums to enable members of the public directly to enter the debate on the main issues.
