The Week Ahead 21-27 July 2008
2008-07-18 - 11:03
External Parliamentary Activities
External activities. The week beginning 27 July is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies and for visits by Parliamentary delegations, so there are no official meetings in Brussels.
A delegation from the International Trade Committee will be in Geneva for the World Trade Organisation ministerial meeting.
A delegation of MEPs will be travelling to Cambodia to observe the parliamentary elections of 27 July.
Funeral of Bronisław Geremek. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering will attend the funeral service of Bronisław Geremek in Warsaw on Monday.
Belgian National Day. Please note that Parliament’s offices in Brussels will be closed for the public holiday on Monday.
Summer recess. The break in Parliamentary activities over the summer begins on 28 July. Business resumes on Monday 25 August with a week of meetings of committees and political groups ahead of the plenary session beginning in Strasbourg on 1 September.
2008-07-21 The President's diary
14.00 - Attendance at Mass and Funeral for Bronisław GEREMEK 14:00 16:30 Warsaw, Poland Delegations 15th EP-New Zealand Interparliamentary Meeting
A Delegation of 11 MEPs will be travelling to New Zealand from 19-26 July for the 15th EP-New Zealand Interparliamentary Meeting. The Delegation will discuss matters of mutual interest with its New Zealand counterparts in Wellington, including trade, CAP reform, Pacific Island affairs, Maori issues and environmental matters. Led by David Martin (PES, UK) the Delegation is composed of: Alessandro Battilocchio (PES, IT), Jean-Marie Beaupuy (ALDE, FR), Michael Cramer (Greens/EFA, DE), Zita Plestinská (EPP-ED, SK), Fernand Le Rachinel (NI, FR), Reinhard Rack (EPP-ED, AT), Margarita Starkeviciuté (ALDE, LT), Brian Simpson (PES, UK), Hannu Takkula (ALDE, FI), and Kyriacos Triantaphyllides (GUE/NGL, CY). 09:00 19:00 Wellington/Regions, New Zealand 5th EP-Sri Lanka Interparliamentary Meeting
A Delegation of 7 MEPs is travelling to Sri Lanka from 20-26 July for the 5th EP-Sri Lanka Interparliamentary Meeting. The Delegation, led by Robert Evans (PES, UK), will meet with its Sri Lankan counterparts in Colombo, before travelling to Trincomalee to visit local authorities and UN projects. The Delegation is composed of Edit Bauer (EPP-ED, SK), Nirj Deva (EPP-ED, UK), Jaromir Kohlicek (GUE/NGL, CZ), Jean Lambert (Greens/EFA, UK), Jo Leinen (PES, DE), James Nicholson (EPP-ED, UK) and Chair Robert Evans (PES, UK). 09:00 19:00 Sri Lanka 2008-07-22 Delegations 15th EP-New Zealand Interparliamentary Meeting

A seven-strong delegation of MEPs led by Helmuth Markov (GUE/NGL, DE), chair of the International Trade Committee, will be in Geneva from 22-25 July for the meeting of thirty trading powers who are attempting to reach an agreement on agriculture and industrial products – central issues in the Doha round.
The MEPs will follow the progress of the negotiations and will meet, among others, the European negotiators, Trade Commissioner Peter Mandelson and Agriculture Commissioner Mariann Fisher-Boel – plus the advisors to WTO Director General Pascal Lamy.
The members of the delegation are: Ignasi Guardans (ALDE, ES), Robert Sturdy (EPP-ED, UK), Erika Mann (PES, DE), Mairead McGuinness (EPP-ED, IE), Jörg Leichtfried (PES, AT) et Jorgo Chatzimarkakis (ALDE, DE). 09:00 19:00 Geneva 2008-07-23 Delegations 15th EP-New Zealand Interparliamentary Meeting

A 7-Member Delegation from the European Parliament will be travelling to Cambodia from 24-29 July to observe the Parliamentary Elections that will be held on the 27th. The Parliament's short-term election observation delegation, led by Glyn Ford (PES, UK), will be joining a long-term EU election observation mission in Phnom Penh, which is led by MEP Martin Callanan (EPP-ED, UK). The Parliament's delegation will meet with Foreign Minister Hor Namhong, as well as representatives of the political parties contesting the election. A joint press conference between the EU mission and the Parliament's delegation will be held on the afternoon of 29 July. The Parliament Delegation includes: Adam Bielan (UEN, PL), Mikel Irujo Amezaga (Greens/EFA, ES), Marianne Mikko (PES, ET), Alojz Peterle (EPP-ED, SL), José Javier Pomes Ruiz (EPP-ED, ES) and Csaba Sógor (EPP-ED, RO). 09:00 18:00 Cambodia 2008-07-25 Delegations 15th EP-New Zealand Interparliamentary Meeting
