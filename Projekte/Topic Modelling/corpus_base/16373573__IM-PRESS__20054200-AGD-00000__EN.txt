The week ahead 10-16 October 2005
2005-10-13 - 12:25
Plenary session / Committee activities
Brussels plenary session
Education. MEPs are expected to adopt at first reading a recommendation on improving standards in higher education in the EU. They also vote on measures to promote teaching of the mother tongue of immigrants in national education systems (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) .
Iran. Parliament will vote on EU-Iran relations in the light of the international negotiations on Iran's nuclear programme (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) .
Africa. Following statements from the Council and Commission, the House will vote on a resolution on Ethiopia (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) . This will be followed by a broader discussion of the situation in Africa, after the presentation of a strategy for the continent by Development Commissioner Louis Michel.
Committee activities
Future of Europe. How can the EU's constitutional process be revived after the "no" votes in the French and Dutch referenda? This question will be the subject of a symposium organised by the Constitutional Affairs Committee with academic specialists in European integration (Thursday and Friday) .
Transport. In the wake of this summer's aircraft accidents, the Transport Committee will give its view on whether the EU should set up a blacklist of airlines which fail to meet international safety standards. MEPs will also vote on measures to improve disabled access to air transport (Tuesday) .
Fighting fraud. The Budgetary Control Committee will grill candidates for the post of director of OLAF, the EU's anti-fraud office. The director is appointed by the Commission after consulting Parliament and the Council (Tuesday) .
Climate change and fluorinated gases. The Environment Committee votes on a report on climate change ahead of the meeting of signatories to the Kyoto protocol which is due to agree in December on a post-2012 strategy. MEPs will also vote on two legislative reports on measures to cut the use of fluorinated gases, especially in car air-conditioning systems (vote Tuesday).
Public hearings. MEPs and experts will discuss the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina ten years after the signing of the Dayton accords (Tuesday) , trade negotiations in the Doha round ahead of the ministerial meeting in Hong Kong (Tuesday) and access to micro-loans by women in Mediterranean countries and some developing countries (Tuesday) .

Parliament's delegation for relations with the Russian parliament is making its twice yearly visit to Russia, this time to Kaliningrad, from 8 to 11 October. As an enclave within EU territory, Kaliningrad is of special interest to Parliament. Among the delegation's activities will be meetings with members of the Kaliningrad Regional Duma and the Kaliningrad governor and mayor.
Issues of special interest to MEPs, especially after this week's EU-Russia summit, will be the transit of people and goods through Kaliningrad, border controls, cross-border projects with Poland and Lithuania and the situation of Russian minorities within the EU.
The EP delegation is headed by Camiel Eurlings (EPP-ED, NL). Other members participating in the visit are Laima Andrikiene (EPP-ED, LT), Sarunas Birutis (ALDE, LT), Joel Hasse Ferreira (PES, PT), Tunne Kelam (EPP-ED, ET), Barbara Kudrycka (EPP-ED, PL), Boguslaw Liberadzki (PES, PL), Justas Paleckis (PES, LT), Vladimir Remek (EUL/NGL, CZ), Boguslaw Sonik (EPP-ED, PL), Bart Staes (Greens/EFA, BE) and Tatjana Zdanoka (Greens/ELA, LV).
08:30 23:59 Kaliningrad, Russia Monitoring mission to elections in Liberia 9-12 October 2005
A delegation of MEPs will travel to Liberia to monitor presidential and parliamentary elections held there on 11 October. The European Parliament delegation, headed by Marie-Arlette Carlotti (PES, F), will arrive in Liberia on 9 October and have meetings with presidential candidates before election day. Afterwards, they will meet with other observer teams, such as the ECOWAS team, to compare notes. The delegation will return to Brussels on 13 October.
The other members of the EP delegation are Koen Dillen (NI, B), Péter Olajos (EPP-ED, H) and Pierre Schapira (PES, F). The EP delegation is part of the wider EU Election Observation Mission, which is led by another MEP, Max van den Berg (PES, NL).
