The Week Ahead 7-13 February 2011
2011-02-04 - 12:48
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Turkey and Montenegro. The Foreign Affairs Committee adopts its regular report on Turkey’s progress towards EU integration, as well as a similar assessment of developments in Montenegro (Thursday).
Summit debate. European Council President Herman Van Rompuy will debate the conclusions of the EU summit at a special meeting open to all MEPs (Tuesday).
Trichet/Systemic Risk. ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet will meet EP President Jerzy Buzek and then the political group leaders, as well has having a discussion with the Economics Committee in his capacity as chair of the new European Systemic Risk Board (Monday).
Prince of Wales/Low Carbon Prosperity Summit. Britain’s Prince Charles will meet Mr Buzek, Mr Van Rompuy and Commission President José Manuel Barroso, before joining them, and MEPs, business leaders and NGO representatives, for a conference on achieving economic progress while tackling climate change (Wednesday).
Korea/Free Trade. The International Trade Committee is now due to vote this week on the EU’s Free Trade Agreement with South Korea. An earlier committee vote has already backed the related Safeguard Clause (Monday).
President’s diary. Mr Buzek will meet Swiss President Micheline Calmy-Rey (Tuesday), former Russian Prime Minister Mikhail Kasyanov (Wednesday) and Moldovan Prime Minister Vlad Filat (Thursday).
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 14-17 February plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda includes legislative/binding votes on emission standards for vans, rights of bus and coach passengers, the Korea Free Trade Agreement and plans for some EU countries to cooperate on a European patent. Other debates deal with Croatia’s EU membership prospects, Hungary’s media law and the implementation of the services directive.
Pre-session press briefing. The EP Press Service and the spokespeople of the main political groups on hand to comment on the session agenda at the regular briefing (Friday 11am, Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).
