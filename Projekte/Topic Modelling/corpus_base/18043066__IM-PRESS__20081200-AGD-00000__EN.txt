The Week Ahead 17-23 March 2008
2008-03-14 - 09:20
External Parliamentary Activities
ACP-EU assembly. The Joint Parliamentary Assembly, bringing together MEPs with national parliamentarians from the African, Caribbean and Pacific States, holds a session in Ljubljana, with debates planned on food security, structural adjustment programmes and economic partnership agreements, among others (Monday-Thursday).
There are no official meetings in Brussels this week, as it is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies or to take part in delegations visits. On this occasion, a Trade Committee delegation will visit Bulgaria , while an Internal Market and Consumer Protection Committee delegation will be in China .
Easter recess. Parliament’s offices are closed from 20-24 March inclusive over Easter.
2008-03-17 Delegations MEPs' visit to Bulgaria will look at Black Sea region development A six-strong delegation of MEPs from the Committee on International Trade will visit Bulgaria from 16 to 20 March 2008. The development of the Black Sea region and ports will take centre stage. They will also meet the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs. MEPs will take part in a Round Table on “The Black Sea Bulgarian Municipalities and the EU Black Sea Regional Policy Approach”. This Round table is organised by the Bulgarian Black Sea Municipalities. In Sofia, MEPs will meet Ivailo Kalfin, the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs. At the National Assembly, they will meet Bulgarian MPs and Chairs of the Committee of European Affairs, the Foreign affairs Committee, the Economic Policy Committee and the Environment Committee of the Parliament. The meeting is hosted by Petar Kanev, Vice-Chair of the Economic Policy Committee. With the Bulgarian Industrial Association, MEPs will have a discussion on “International trade - Bulgarian and EU priorities”. A meeting with Bulgarian Trade Unions Representatives (Confederation of Independent Trade Unions in Bulgaria) is also scheduled. The delegation will be chaired by Helmuth Markov (GUE/NGL, DE), INTA chairman, Daniel Varela Suanzes-Carpegna (EPP-ED, ES), David Martin (PES, UK), Vural Öger (PES, DE), Marusya Ivanova Lyubcheva (PES, BG) and Ryszard Czarnecki (UEN, PL). 09:00 19:00 Bulgaria A delegation of the EP Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection will visit China on 16-21 March
Product safety and international trade: visit to China
A delegation of the EP Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection, led by Arlene McCarthy (P E S, UK) , will visit China on 16-21 March . Product safety, including the safety of toys, consumer protection and intellectual property will be the key topics for discussion between MEPs and the Chinese authorities .
09:00 19:00 Chine ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly meets in Ljubljana (Slovenia) from 15 to 20 March 2008
Negotiations on economic partnership agreements and developments in Chad and Kenya will take centre stage at the 15th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly (JPA), in Ljubljana (Slovenia) from 15 to 20 March 2008. The ACP-EU JPA brings together 78 MEPs and 78 parliamentarians from the African, Caribbean and Pacific (ACP) states. The formal opening on 17 March 2008 will be followed by a press conference at 12.30h.
The key speakers at the formal opening, which starts on Monday 17 March at 11.00h, are ACP-EU co-Presidents Glenys Kinnock and Wilkie Rasmussen (Cook Islands). European Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering will address the parliamentarians in a video message.
Mrs Kinnock and Mr Rasmussen will also give a press conference at around midday on Monday 17 March 2008.
Reports to be put to the vote
The Assembly will debate and vote on three reports on:
- Social and environmental consequences of structural adjustment programmes - Gay Mitchell (EPP-ED, IE) and Alma Oumarou (Niger),
- Experiences from the European regional integration process relevant to ACP countries - Filip Kaczmarek (EPP-ED, PL) and Mr De Sousa (Angola) and
-Food security issues in ACP countries and the role of ACP-EU co-operation - Alain Hutchinson (PES, BE) and Mohamed Ali (Ethiopia).
Economic partnership agreements
The ACP-EU Assembly will also debate economic partnership agreements, further to the Assembly's adoption of the "Kigali Declaration for development-friendly economic partnership agreements" (EPAs) in November 2007. The debate on EPAs may be attended by European Commission Director-General for trade David O'Sullivan (Monday 17 March).
