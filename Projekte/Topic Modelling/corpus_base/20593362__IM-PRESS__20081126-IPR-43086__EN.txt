President Pöttering’s official visit to Northern Ireland - 23-24 November
Institutions
United Kingdom
2008-11-28 - 11:07
President Pöttering with school pupils in Lisburn, County Antrim European Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering arrived in Belfast on Sunday 23 November at the start of a visit to the Northern Ireland Executive and Assembly. He came at the joint invitation of the First Minster, Rt Hon Peter Robinson and the Deputy First Minister Martin McGuinness, and was invited to address the Northern Ireland Assembly by the Speaker, William Hay.
In his address, Hans-Gert Pöttering spoke of shared European values and of the support offered by the EU to the peace process in Northern Ireland. He pledged that the EU would continue to be at their side as they built on political success to create a prosperous and socially-inclusive society.
During his visit the President met party leaders and members of the Assembly and Business Trust. He visited two cross-community projects supported by EU funding, the Educating for Diversity project involving three primary schools in the Lisburn area, and the Suffolk and Lenadoon Interface Group in West Belfast.
He had begun his visit by attending a dinner offered for faith leaders by the head of the Northern Ireland civil service, Bruce Robinson, where the President had a sustained exchange of views with wide variety of denominational representatives and cross-community groups.
