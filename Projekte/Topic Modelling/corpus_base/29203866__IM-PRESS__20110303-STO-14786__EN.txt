Reportage: President Jerzy Buzek meets Pope Benedict XVI
External relations
2011-03-04 - 09:28
8:45 AM - A quick breakfast before being briefed by a policy advisor. 9:30 AM - Buzek getting ready for his audience with HH Pope Benedict XVI. 10:20 AM - Leaving Montecitorio Square on the way to the Vatican. 11:30 AM - Jerzy Buzek, following his private audience with His Holiness Pope Benedict XVI, said: "I had a rich exchange of views with the Holy Father. It was very important for me to see today's Europe from a different perspective, not only through the lens of a politician." 11:55 AM - After meeting Cardinal Bertone, President Buzek visits St. Peter's Basilica and the tomb of John Paul II. 12:30 PM - President Buzek fields journalists' questions near St. Peter's Square. 13:00/15:30 PM - (L-R) Buzek greets Grand Master Festing of the Order of Malta; a guard outside Villa Magistrale; in the car with EU ambassador Yves Gazzo; meeting the 27 EU ambassadors at the Hungarian representation to the Holy See. 15:50 PM - The President's party climbs to the roof of the Hungarian Representation to the Holy See to take in the view over Rome 17:00 PM - Meeting Mario Draghi, the Governor of the Bank of Italy. The two Italian Vice-Presidents of the European Parliament, Gianni Pittella and Roberta Angelilli, look on. 23:45 PM - The end of a busy day: on the flight back to Brussels after a three hour delay. See the full-length photo reportage of President's day at the link below. On Monday, 28 February 2011, Jerzy Buzek was received by Pope Benedict XVI at a private audience at the Vatican, Rome. Our photographer spent a day travelling with President Buzek capturing the main highlights and details of this important encounter.
President Buzek also met Cardinal Bertone, dignitaries from the Order of Malta, Mario Draghi of the Bank of Italy as well as ambassadors from the 27 member states to the Holy See.See the full photo reportage on Flickr via the link below.
