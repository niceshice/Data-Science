This week online: MEPs, MP discuss future of Europe; demography; disability week interview
Institutions
2007-12-03 - 10:23
Read our latest article about the EU's demograhic deficit
This week MEPs meet in their political groups to prepare for next week's plenary session. On the website Monday and Tuesday you can watch the fourth meeting between MEPs and national MPs on the future of Europe and the new Lisbon Treaty.
The meeting is jointly organised by the European and Portuguese Parliaments and will be opened by EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and Portuguese Parliament President Jaime Gama . Watch from 1530 CET on Monday and 0930 on Tuesday.
On Wednesday you can watch another debate on the future of Europe, following the awarding of the Charlemagne Prize for services to European unity, to the EU High Representative for Foreign and Security Policy, Javier Solana. The focus of the debate, moderated by former MEP and 2004 Charlemagne Prize winner Pat Cox, will be foreign policy. Watch live from 1815 on Wednesday.
Catch up with our series on the demographic deficit in the EU on Thursday with an article on family policy and the birth rate. The Employment Committee is due to vote on Francoise Castex's report on "The demographic future of Europe" in December.
This week is the European week of people with disabilities - get Evangelia Tzampazi 's take on the issue in our interview on Friday. Also on Friday, find out about the EU-Africa Parliamentary Summit , which will take place in Lisbon 8-9 December. A delegation of 25 MEPs will attend and Mr Pöttering will address the meeting.
That's just a taste of some of the things going on in Parliament this week. Check the site throughout the week to stay up to date on major EP stories.
