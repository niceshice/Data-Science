Photo slideshow: Milestones of the 2004-2009 Parliamentary term
Institutions
2009-07-30 - 09:03
UK Prime Minister Tony Blair outlines his vision of Europe in June 2005 French President Nicolas Sarkozy makes his point to Parliament in October 2008 Commissioner-designate Rocco Buttiglione faces his parliamentary hearing in October 2004 Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering signs the Charter of Fundamental Rights alongside his counterparts from the European Council and Commission. Rapporteur Evelyne Gebhardt during the vote on the much-contested Services Directive, one of several major pieces of legislation on which Parliament found the political deals needed. The remains of the day: a projectile amid broken glass on the day Parliament bore the brunt of dockers' protests The Orange Revolution comes to the EU. Viktor Yushchenko's first foreign visit as Ukrainian President is to the European Parliament. Hans-Gert Pöttering ill-disguises his feelings amid the rubble of Gaza, February 2009 From Tony Blair's emphatic speech to the vote on the Services Directive to the dockers' angry protests: The five years of the last Parliamentary term saw some momentous events - some far reaching, others just dramatic. In this slideshow we look back at some incidents - both memorable and at times poignant, such as when the EP President led a delegation of MEPs to the Gaza strip.
With the new term underway we look forward to more incident, drama and debate from Strasbourg and Brussels from the current 736 MEPs, almost half of whom are newly elected.
