The Week Ahead 25 April – 1 May 2011
2011-04-20 - 15:03
External Parliamentary Activities
This week is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies and to take part in interparliamentary meetings outside the EU or other working visits. On this occasion, these include EP delegations meeting their counterparts in Pakistan, Jordan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan .
There are no official parliamentary meetings in Brussels this week.
Easter recess. Parliament’s offices are closed for the Easter holidays from 21 to 25 April inclusive. Urgent press questions between these dates should be addressed to Michaela Findeis on +32 498 983 332.
2011-04-26 Delegations 6th EP-Jordan Interparliamentary Meeting MEPs from the European Parliament's Delegation for Relations with the Mashreq countries will be attending the 6th EP-Jordan Interparliamentary Meeting in Jordan from 26-30 April. Led by Delegation Chair Mário DAVID (EPP, PT), the visit includes meetings with civil society organisations, university students and human rights groups as well as government members and legislative leaders. The delegation will also meet King Abdullah. 09:00 19:00 Amman, Jordan Delegation for relations with the Mashreq countries (DMAS) Delegation visit to Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan European Union-Republic of Turkmenistan
2nd Interparliamentary Meeting
The EP delegation for the relations with Central Asia, headed by Paolo BARTOLOZZI (EPP, IT) will visit Turkmenistan during 26-29 April. The MEPs will meet the Turkmen foreign minister, the Parliamentary Speaker and well as representatives of international organisations present on the ground: the OSCE, UNDP and UNICEF.
The MEPs taking part in the delegation are: Elisabeth JEGGLE (EPP, DE), Ana GOMES (S&D, PT), Teresa RIERA MADURELL (S&D, ES), Anneli JÄÄTTEENMÄKI (ALDE, FI), Nicole KIIL-NIELSEN (Greens/EFA, FR), and Heidi HAUTALA (Greens/EFA, FI)
Committee on Foreign Affairs Delegation visit to Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan
Preceding the visit to Turkmenistan a delegation from the Foreign Affairs Committee will visit Uzbekistan (Tashkent) where besides meeting the authorities, MEPs will also have the opportunity to meet representatives of civil society organisations.
Members of the delegation are: Heidi HAUTALA (Greens/EFA, FI), Inese VAIDERE (EPP, LV), Piotr BORYS (EPP, PL), Hannes SWOBODA (S&D, AT), Juan Fernando LÓPEZ AGUILAR (S&D, ES), Katarína NEVEĎALOVÁ (S&D, SK), Norica NICOLAI (ALDE, RO), Janusz WOJCIECHOWSKI (ECR, PL)
