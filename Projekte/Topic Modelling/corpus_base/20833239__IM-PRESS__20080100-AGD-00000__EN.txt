The Week Ahead: 29 December 2008 to 4 January 2009
2008-12-19 - 10:36
Christmas and New Year Recess
Parliament is in recess over the Christmas and New Year holidays. Normal business resumes on Monday 5 January with a week of meetings of Parliament’s political groups to prepare for the 12-15 January plenary session.
Parliament’s Press Service wishes all its readers a Merry Christmas and a Happy New Year.
2008-12-29 Delegations MEPs to observe upcoming parliamentary elections in Bangladesh A four-strong delegation of Members of the European Parliament, chaired by Conservative MEP Charles Tannock, will observe the general elections to take place Monday 29 December 2008, the first in seven years. The state of emergency in place since January 2007 has been lifted Wednesday 17 December 2008.
On top of the MEPs, some 150 EU Observers will be in place on Election Day. The 144 million-nation will choose its MPs among leading parties such as the Awami League and the Bangladesh Nationalist Party.
During a press conference to be held jointly with Alexander Graf Lambsdorff (ALDE, DE), leading the Election Observation Mission (EOM) of the European Union, MEPs will give a presentation of the preliminary statement following the polls. The press conference will take place Wednesday, 31 December 2008 at 15.30h. Venue: Sheraton Hotel, Marble room, Dhaka.
The delegation will be composed of Charles Tannock (EPP-ED, UK), Neil Parish (EPP-ED, UK), Nirj Deva (EPP-ED, UK) and Koenraad Dillen (NI, BE). 09:00 19:00 Bangladesh
