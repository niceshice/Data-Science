Committee week - Brussels
Open Day at the EP. The theme of the European Parliament's Open Day 2007 is the 50th anniversary of the Treaty of Rome. This symbolic milestone will be the backcloth to debates, events, concerts and exhibitions organised for the public (Tuesday in Strasbourg, Saturday in Brussels). European Institute of Technology. The EIT - famous even before it is launched - does not, in the format proposed by the Commission, have a clear role or mode of operation. A joint public hearing of the EP's Industry Committee and Culture Committee will discuss the options (Wednesday). Hans-Gert PÃ¶ttering in Warsaw. The President of Parliament will deliver a speech at the Polish Senate as part of the ceremonies to mark the anniversary of the constitution of the union between Poland and Lithuania in 1791 (Wednesday). Space and defence. The Subcommittee on Security and Defence is holding a public hearing on space and European security and defence policy: space observation, space weapons and telecommunications are among the main topics of the meeting (Wednesday). Mercury. The Environment Committee will vote on a report on the export and storage of mercury. The amended draft regulation could take greater account of environmental issues and consumer protection (Thursday). Internet. An own-initiative report on broadband internet access will be put to the vote by the Industry Committee. In addition to access in rural areas, the report will look at various aspects of the "digital divide" (Thursday). Louis Michel. The Development Commissioner will reply to questions from members of the EP Development Committee (Thursday). Ombudsman. Nikiforos Diamandouros, the European Ombudsman, will present his 2006 annual report to the Petitions Committee (Wednesday). Cartooning for peace. To mark World Press Freedom Day, Plantu (France), Dilem (Algeria) and other well-known political cartoonists will present their initiative "Cartooning for peace" to the EP Subcommittee on Human Rights (Thursday).
