The Week Ahead 7-13 September 2009
2009-09-04 - 10:20
Committee meetings and political groups week
Barroso in political group meetings. Ahead of a debate and a possible vote at the September plenary session on his candidacy for a renewed mandate as Commission President, José Manuel Barroso will be in Parliament for a series of meetings with individual political groups. Full details of the timing and format of each meeting are available from the political groups’ press teams. The Conference of Presidents will confirm on Thursday this week whether there will be a vote on the Commission presidency during the plenary.
Preparing for the plenary. The political groups will devote the rest of the week to preparations for the 14-17 September plenary session in Strasbourg. As well as the Commission presidency issue, the session includes the inaugural address of new EP President Jerzy Buzek and a vote on the three interim commissioners-designate.
President’s activities. President Buzek will have meetings in Brussels with EU Foreign Policy High Representative Javier Solana (Monday), ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet (Wednesday) and Bulgarian Prime Minister Boyko Borisov (Thursday – followed by a press point) . He will visit Dublin (Tuesday) ahead of the referendum in Ireland on the Lisbon Treaty, Warsaw (Thursday), to give a speech marking the 20th anniversary of the first non-communist government in Poland, and Rome (Thursday-Saturday) to take part in the regular meeting of Parliamentary Speakers from the G-8 countries.
Palestinian welfare. The Foreign Affairs and Development Committees hold a joint meeting with Karen Abu Zayd, Commissioner of UNWRA, the UN agency for Palestinian refugees (Thursday). She will also be meeting President Buzek.
Pre-session press briefing. The final draft agenda for the session, including changes adopted by the Conference of Presidents, will be presented at the regular press briefing on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room – PHS 0A050).
