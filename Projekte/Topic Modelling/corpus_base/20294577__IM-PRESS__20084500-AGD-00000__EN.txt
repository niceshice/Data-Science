The Week Ahead: 3 to 9 November 2008
2008-10-31 - 10:08
Parliamentary committee meetings - Brussels
Arab week. The Arab world will be the focus of a series of meetings and events organised by the EP as part of the year of intercultural dialogue. The Arab week will be inaugurated by EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering, who will receive inter alia Secretary-General of the League of Arab States Amr Moussa (Tuesday, press point at 18.00h). Among other guests, Iraqi MPs and Iraqi government spokesman Ali Dabbagh will be received by the delegation for relations with Iraq (Wednesday).
Hans-Gert Pöttering. The EP President will be received in the Hague by Queen Beatrix and Dutch Prime Minister Jan-Peter Balkenende (Tuesday).
Financial crisis. Eurogroup chair Jean-Claude Juncker will give the Economic Affairs Committee his assessment of the financial crisis and its impact on the real economy (Tuesday).
Working time. The Employment Committee takes its second-reading vote on the proposed revision of the EU Directive (Wednesday).
Immigration. The Civil Liberties Committee will vote on draft directives providing for sanctions against employers of illegally staying third-country nationals and introducing a European "blue card" for highly-qualified workers from third countries ( Tuesday).
Pesticides. The Environment Committee takes its second-reading vote on the framework directive on the sustainable use of pesticides and new marketing authorisation rules for plant protection products (Wednesday) .
Toy safety. The Internal Market Committee takes its first-reading vote on a draft directive designed to upgrade protection for children (Thursday).
Mobile roaming. The Industry Committee will hold a first exchange of views on a proposal to extend the "Roaming" regulation to SMS and other mobile data transmission services (Tuesday).
Enlargement. Commission Rehn will present the Commission's annual reports on accession candidate countries (Wednesday).

Parliamentary activities
European Parliament committees and delegations will focus on Arab issues during this week.
EP President Pöttering will meet Amr Mussa, Secretary General of the League of Arab States (Tuesday 4, 18.00h, TBC). They will have a press point following their meeting at 18.30 (PHS Building, 11th floor). The Foreign Affairs Committee will have a debate with Mr Mussa on Wednesday 5 (09.00h to 10.30h).

On Thursday, 4th November, 9:00-12:30 the three committees on Foreign affairs, Security and Defence and Development will hold to a public hearing on the theme "Security and Development". The purpose of the hearing will be to explore the vital link between security and development on a basis of acquired knowledge, experiences and needs on the ground with a view to formulating recommendations for better articulated EU action on the issue. The nexus between security and development was the subject of the Conclusions of the Council adopted in November 2007 and on the basis of which the European Commission is currently preparing an action plan. The hearing is an opportunity for the Parliament to contribute to the work carried out on the subject by the other institutions. The hearing would also provide a tribune for in-depth discussions together with EU's African partners on the progress made regarding the Peace and Security cluster of the Joint EU-Africa Strategy.
Keynote speakers: Mr João Gomes CRAVINHO, Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs and Cooperation, Portugal, Mr Koen VERVAEKE, EU Special Representative to the AU, H.E. Mr Mahamat Saleh ANNADIF, Permanent Representative of the African Union to the European Union, Mr Roger MIDDLETON, Royal Institute of International Affairs, Chatham House, London, Dr Damien HELLY, EU Institute for Security Studies, Paris, Prof. Michael BRZOSKA, Director of the Institute for Peace Research and Security Policy, Hamburg and Martin LACOURT, Senior Delegate to Armed Forces, International Committee of the Red Cross, Belgium. 09:00 12:30 Brussels, ASP 1G2 Programme of the Hearing Term of protection of copyright and related rights Committee on Legal Affairs
The Legal Affairs committee will hold a hearing with experts and stakeholders on the Commission proposal to extend the term of copyright protection for sound recordings and performers beyond the currently applicable 50 year term to 70 years after their death. According to the proposal, the extension will bring some added income to offset losses due to internet piracy. The added income will ensure record companies continue to spend money on new European acts.
