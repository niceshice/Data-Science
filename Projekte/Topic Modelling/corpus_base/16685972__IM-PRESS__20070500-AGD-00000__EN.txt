The Week Ahead 29 January - 4 February 2007
2007-01-30 - 09:27
Plenary session and committee meetings - Brussels
Official visits. At the opening of the plenary session on Wednesday Romania's President, Traian Basescu, will address a formal sitting of the House. The President of Bulgaria, Georgi Parvanov, addresses MEPs on Thursday morning. Both will have meetings with European Parliament President Hans-Gert Poettering.
Brussels plenary session. Passenger name records, SWIFT bank data transfer, climate change and the death penalty, along with a report on obesity are among the highlights of this week's plenary session on 31 January and 1 February.
Committees. The new composition of Parliament's standing committees will be approved by the House at 5.30pm, enabling the committees then to choose their chairs and vice-chairs (Wednesday and Thursday) .
Environment. Two key reports will be adopted by the Environment Committee: one on spirits (the "vodka" report), the other on advanced therapies. (Tuesday) .
Football. Excessive commercialisation, violence and racism are among the problems discussed in a report on professional football which will be put to the vote in the Culture Committee. MEPs are expected to call on the Commission to put forward an ad hoc action plan (Monday) .
Terrorism. The Civil Liberties Committee is also holding a seminar in conjunction with the NATO Parliamentary Assembly on the protection of Europe's critical infrastructure as part of the fight against terrorism (Wednesday) .
Nuclear safety. To mark the 50th anniversary of Euratom, a hearing takes place with representatives of the nuclear industry and NGOs to discuss the state of nuclear safety in Europe (Thursday) .
German presidency. Ministers representing the Presidency-in-Office of the Council will complete their series of briefings in the committees on their programme for the next 6 months (Monday and Tuesday).
