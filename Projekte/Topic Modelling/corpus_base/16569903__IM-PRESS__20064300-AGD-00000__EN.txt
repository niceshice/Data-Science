The Week Ahead 23-29 October 2006
2006-10-27 - 10:19
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
European Council. MEPs will debate the outcome of the informal meeting of EU leaders in Lahti, Finland, with the Finnish Prime Minister and the President of the Commission. (Wednesday)
EU Budget 2007. Parliament is due to vote at first reading on the EU’s budget for 2007. (Debate Tuesday, vote Thursday)
Schengen Information System II. MEPs will deal with a package of measures setting the rules for the new version of the SIS, which will include the new Member States in the electronic exchange of data on home affairs matters. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Hungary. Fifty years after the 1956 uprising in Hungary, President Josep Borrell and the leaders of Parliament’s political groups will make statements in plenary to mark the occasion. (Tuesday) The President of Hungary, László Sólyom , will be in Strasbourg to address MEPs in a formal sitting. (Wednesday)
ECB. Jean-Claude Trichet, ECB President, will take part in a debate on Parliament’s report on the Bank’s performance in 2005 . (debate and vote Thursday)
Spain/ETA. MEPs will debate the peace process in Spain with the Council and Commission. (debate and vote Wednesday)
Russia. Parliament will vote on a resolution on EU-Russia relations following the murder of journalist Anna Politkovskaya. (Wednesday)
Breast cancer. MEPs will be putting a series of oral questions to the Commission on the subject of breast cancer. (debate and vote Tuesday)
Sakharov Prize. Away from the plenary, the leaders of Parliament’s political groups will decide on the winner of this year’s Sakharov Prize for freedom of thought. (Thursday)
Services Directive. The Internal Market Committee will hold its second reading vote on the Services Directive. (Monday)
