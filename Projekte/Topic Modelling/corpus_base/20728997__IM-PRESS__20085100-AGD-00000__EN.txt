The Week Ahead 15-21 December 2008
2008-12-12 - 13:15
Plenary Session - Strasbourg
Nicolas Sarkozy. The French President will debate with MEPs the outcome of the latest summit focused on the economy, the Lisbon Treaty and climate change and the results of the French EU presidency as a whole. He will join EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering for a press conference (Tuesday) .
Climate change package. Parliament will debate then hold a series of legislative votes on the package of measures aimed at reducing Europe’s carbon emissions (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) .
Working time. The directive updating the rules on the length of the working week is due for a second reading – if Parliament makes any changes to the Council’s position, a conciliation procedure seems likely (debate Monday, vote Wednesday).
Sakharov Prize. Parliament marks the 20th anniversary of its prize for freedom of thought with a round table discussion featuring many former Sakharov winners (Tuesday) , then holds a ceremony to honour the winner of the 2008 prize, imprisoned Chinese dissident Hu Jia , in the presence of Elena Bonner (widow of Andrei Sakharov), who will also join President Pöttering for a press conference (Wednesday) .
Toy safety. Legislation to ensure high safety standards for toys is set for adoption at first reading, following an agreement between MEPs and the Council (debate Monday, vote Thursday).
Deposit guarantees. Parliament is set to increase to €100 000 guarantees for individuals’ accounts when banks fail (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday)
Budget 2009. Parliament is due to vote into law the EU budget for 2009 (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) .
French Presidency. The Economics Committee hears from Finance Minister Christine Lagarde (Monday) and the Constitutional Affairs Committee from Foreign Minister Bernard Kouchner (Tuesday) on developments in their respective fields during the last six months.
EP/Gulf States. President Pöttering begins an official visit to the Gulf States, with meeting with the heads of state and officials in Oman and the United Arab Emirates (Saturday and Sunday).

Presidency in office of the Council - Discussion with Bernard KOUCHNER, French Minister for Foreign and European Affairs 17:00 18:30 Strasbourg, SDM S1 Other events 20th anniversary of the Sakharov Prize for Freedom of Thought 2008 prize awarded to imprisoned Chinese dissident Hu Jia This year's Sakharov Prize ceremonies in Strasbourg have a dual character. Parliament celebrates the 20th anniversary of the award by welcoming a number of former winners of the Prize to take part in two round-table debates and attend this year's award ceremony.
This year's winner, Chinese dissident Hu Jia, will not be able to receive his award as he is imprisoned in China. Instead, a special ceremony - to be attended by Andrei Sakharov's widow, Elena Bonner, as well as the former laureates - will be held in Parliament's Plenary Chamber to honour Hu Jia, who is awarded the prize in honour of his work on the behalf of the silenced voices in China and Tibet.
MAIN EVENTS
Tuesday 16 December: round table debate with former Sakharov Prize winners
All former Sakharov Prize winners still living have been invited to come to the EP to commemorate the 20th anniversary. Of these, most have accepted and will take part in a special conference from 5.00 to 7.00pm on Tuesday 16 December. The discussions, to be moderated by Belgian journalist Jean-Paul Marthoz, will be open to the press and will cover two themes: The role of the Sakharov Prize in supporting human rights campaigners and The role of the Sakharov Prize winners in the development of European human rights policy. Room LOW N1.3
At the end of Tuesday evening's debates with former laureates, President Pöttering will officially launch the 'Sakharov Network' with Elena Bonner, widow of Andrei Sakharov. The aim of this project is to link up past winners of the prize in the form of a network to support human rights defenders around the world.
Wednesday 17 December: ceremony for 2008 prize winner Hu Jia
In the absence of Hu Jia, Wednesday's ceremony, at 12 noon, will include an official address by EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and an address by Elena Bonner, widow of Andrei Sakharov.
The ceremony will be followed at 12.30pm by a press conference with Mr Pöttering, accompanied by Mrs Bonner.
Press service contacts for interviews with former Sakharov Prize winners
Journalists will have the opportunity to interview former laureates Wednesday 17 between 09.00 and 11.30 in the audiovisual lobby area on the left side of the Plenary Chamber. Journalists are invited to register their interest.
