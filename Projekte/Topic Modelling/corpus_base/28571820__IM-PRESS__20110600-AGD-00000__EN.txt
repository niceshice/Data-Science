The Week Ahead 31 January - 6 February 2011
2011-01-28 - 09:49
Plenary session and committee meetings – Brussels
Brussels plenary session
Electronic waste. MEPs will decide on an update to EU rules on the disposal of electrical and electronic equipment (debate and vote Thursday).
Tunisia. High Representative/Commission Vice-President Catherine Ashton will debate developments in Tunisia with MEPs (Wednesday), who will then adopt a resolution on the issue (Thursday). A delegation of MEPs will visit the country after the session.
Bananas. An agreement aimed at ending the long-running dispute on trade in bananas will be put to Parliament (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday).
Committee meetings and other activities
Consumer rights. A package of legislation covering the rights of consumers when buying goods or services comes to a vote in the Internal Market Committee (Tuesday). Parliament’s Press Service is organising a seminar for journalists on this topic.
Financial supervisors. The Economics Committee will hear from the candidates proposed to head the EU’s new financial market supervisory authorities (Tuesday).
Work/residence permit. The Civil Liberties Committee also votes on new amendments to a draft law to give legal migrants the opportunity to make a single application for both work and residence permits in EU countries (Thursday). The proposal was returned to the committee by a vote at December’s plenary session.
Pension system. The Employment Committee will adopt its position on how to ensure EU countries maintain viable pension systems (Tuesday).
Iran. The Foreign Affairs Committee is set to adopt its position on the EU’s strategy towards Iran (Tuesday).
EU summit. EP President Jerzy Buzek will address the latest European Council meeting, before holding a press conference (Friday). Plans for the summit will be debated with Commission and Council representatives during the EP session (Wednesday).
