The Week Ahead 16-22 March 2009
2009-03-13 - 11:53
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
EU summit. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering will address EU leaders at the start of the Spring European Council before holding a press conference (Justus Lipsius building, Thursday).
Euro-Med Assembly. The Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly, which brings MEPs together with parliamentarians from the southern and eastern Mediterranean, holds a plenary session at the EP in Brussels, with President Pöttering in the chair (Monday-Tuesday) .
EP/Germany. President Pöttering makes an official visit to Germany, including meetings with President Horst Köhler, Chancellor Angela Merkel and senior parliamentarians (Tuesday-Wednesday).
Working time. The conciliation committee on the working time directive begins the formal negotiations aimed at reaching agreement between Parliament and Council (Tuesday).
Discharge 2007. The Budgetary Control Committee will vote on whether to give its approval to the financial management of the EU institutions in 2007 (Monday).
Non-discrimination. The Civil Liberties Committee votes on legislation to ensure equal treatment irrespective of religion or belief, disability, age or sexual orientation in areas other than employment (Monday).
Biometric visas. Legislation on the use of biometric identifiers in visa for third country nationals comes to a second reading vote, also at the Civil Liberties Committee vote (Monday).
Election communication strategy. Journalists are invited to the unveiling by Vice-Presidents Alejo Vidal-Quadras and Mechtild Rothe of Parliament's institutional communication strategy for the 4-7 June European elections (Tuesday, 1030, VoxBox area, 3rd floor ASP)
Plenary preparations. The political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 23-26 March plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda includes votes on cohesion policy and air traffic control systems, a debate on the outcome of the Spring Council and another, with UK Prime Minister Gordon Brown, looking ahead to the G20 summit on the financial crisis.
