What's on at the European Parliament 21-27 June
Institutions
2010-06-21 - 12:20
Busy Committee week in Brussels banking crisis, pesticides, waste electronic equipment all feature MEPs meet in Parliamentary Committees this week in Brussels where the banking crisis, pesticides, electronic equipment and EU enlargement are among the things on the agenda. Midweek sees a special session of the House to debate the summit last week of European leaders and their plans to increase economic cooperation in Europe. Parliament's President will also hold talks with Russia's President President Dmitri Medvedev during a visit to Moscow early in the week.
At a glance - some of the week's activities:
MEPs on the Economics and Monetary Affairs Committee will on Monday meet with European Central Bank President Jean-Claude Trichet and discuss economic coordination in the European Union. On Tuesday MEPs will also be talking about the pay of directors of listed financial services.
Ahead of next week's start of Belgium's EU presidency President Buzek and the political group leaders meet with the Belgian government on Thursday. Belgian takes over the 6-month rotating presidency from Spain on 1 July.
On Wednesday 23 June European Council President Herman Van Rompuy will brief MEPs on the outcome of last week's economic summit. The House will then debate the issues raised.
The Environment Committee votes on pesticides and rules on disposal of electronic equipment (Tuesday).
The Foreign Affairs Committee gives its views on Iceland's application to join the EU on Tuesday.
The Human Rights Sub Committee will hear from President Buzek (Thursday) on human rights following his recent visits to Russia and China.
Also featuring this week will be a discussion in the Employment Committee about what constitutes a minimum income.
