The Week Ahead 16-22 May 2011
2011-05-13 - 12:20
External Parliamentary Activities week
ACP-EU Assembly. The assembly, which brings together MPs from 78 African, Caribbean and Pacific countries with 78 MEPs, holds its 21st session, this time in Budapest. The agenda includes developments in Côte d'Ivoire, the impact of the 'Arab Spring' on ACP countries and water pollution, as well as challenges to democracy and issues surrounding development aid (Monday-Wednesday).
Eurolat Assembly. The Euro-Latin American Parliamentary Assembly holds its fifth full session in Montevideo, Uruguay. The assembly, bringing together MEPs with their counterparts from Latin America, will discuss EU-Mercosur trade negotiations, the effects of the financial crisis and the creation of an Observatory for Migration (Tuesday-Thursday).
EP President visits Armenia, Georgia and Azerbaijan. Jerzy Buzek will make official visits to three countries in the Caucasus. He will meet the parliamentarians, political leaders, including the heads of state, and civil society representatives (Wednesday-Friday).
Ombudsman. The European Ombudsman, Nikiforos Diamandouros, will outline his 2010 Annual Report in a press conference at 10h on Monday (Anna Politkovskaya Press Conference room).
External activities week. There are no official meetings in Brussels this week, as it is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies or to take part in working visits outside the EU. As well as the two assemblies, on this occasion these include meetings with parliamentarians in Japan, Kosovo, Croatia and Morocco.

During the trip, MEPs will assess the state of play of the accession process and the budgetary consequences of this enlargement for the EU.
Other participants are Carl HAGLUND (ALDE, FI), Jan KOZLOWSKI (EPP, PL), Georgios STAVRAKAKIS (S&D, EL), Lásló SURJÁN (EPP, HU) and Derek VAUGHAN (S&D, UK). 09:00 19:00 Iceland Website of the Budgetary committee Visit to Warsaw (Poland) In the light of Poland's upcoming presidency of the Council of the European Union, a delegation from the Women's Rights and Gender Equality Committee will visit Warsaw from the 16-18 May 2011 to learn about gender equality policies in Poland and exchange views about the priorities of the presidency programme in the field of gender equality.
The committee will meet Mrs Jolanta Fedak, Minister for Employment and Mr Radoslaw Mleczko, Under-secretary of State, Ministry of Labour and Social Policy, and Mrs E. Radziszewska, Government Plenipotentiary for Equal Treatment (tbc) as well as other members of the Polish government.
Participants include committee chair Eva-Britt SVENSSON (GUE/NGL, SE), Barbara MATERA (EPP, IT) and Marc TARABELLA (S&D, BE).

Developments in Côte d'Ivoire following the post-electoral crisis, including the worrying humanitarian situation, and the impact of the Arab spring on the ACP countries will be the main topics to be debated.
The two co-presidents, MEP Louis Michel (ALDE, BE), and David MATONGO (Zambia) and the President of Hungary, Pal SCHMITT, will address the assembly's opening session on Monday, 16 May, at 11.00, followed by a press conference at around midday.
Three reports jointly drawn up by MEPs and ACP will be discussed and then put to the vote in the afternoon of Wednesday, 18 May • Challenges for the future of democracy and respecting constitutional order in ACP and EU countries, drafted by Olle Schmidt (ALDE, SV) and François IBOVI (Republic of Congo) • Budgetary support as a means of delivering Official Development Assistance (ODA) in ACP countries, drafted by Enrique Guerrero SALOM (S&D, ES) and Mohamed Abdallahi Ould GUELAVE (Mauritania) • Water pollution, drafted by Bobbo HAMATOUKOUR (Cameroon) and Christa KlASS (EPP, DE)
The Assembly, which meets twice a year, brings together 78 MEPs with their counterparts from African, Caribbean and Pacific countries to ensure parliamentary scrutiny of EU development policy.
