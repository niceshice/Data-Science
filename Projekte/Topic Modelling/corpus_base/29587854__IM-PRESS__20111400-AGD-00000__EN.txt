The Week Ahead 28 March – 3 April 2011
2011-03-25 - 09:30
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Novel foods – conciliation. MEPs and Council representatives have a last-chance conciliation meeting on “novel foods” regulations. Parliament wants to ensure meat from cloned animals and their offspring is banned from sale – Member States have so far opposed a ban (Monday).
EU financial management. The Budgetary Control Committee will vote on whether to recommend that Parliament should grant discharge to the Commission for its management of EU funds in 2009 (Monday).
EU budget 2012. A meeting between Parliament, Council and Commission representatives will discuss next year’s budget and outstanding issues regarding spending in 2011 (Wednesday).
EP President - Netherlands. Jerzy Buzek makes an official visit to the Netherlands, meeting among others Prime Minister Mark Rutte and the Presidents and members of both chambers of the Dutch parliament (Tuesday).
EP - Commission. President Buzek will meet the full college of European Commissioners (Wednesday).
Planning for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 4-7 April plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda includes a debate with European Council President Herman Van Rompuy on the outcome of the EU summit, adoption of Parliament’s regular assessments of progress towards EU membership by Iceland and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, votes on the future of the single market and legislation on export credits and fisheries.
Pre-session briefing. The EP Press Service and the political group spokespeople will comment on the session agenda at the regular press briefing on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room, PHS 0A050).
