The Week Ahead 22-28 October 2007
Institutions
2007-10-19 - 10:31
Plenary Session - Strasbourg
Lisbon summit (18-19 October). After statements by Portuguese Prime Minister José Sócrates and Commission President José Manuel Barroso, MEPs will debate the summit outcome (debate Tuesday). Budget. MEPs will hold a first-reading debate on the EU's draft general budget for 2008, including proposals to increase funding for Galileo and the European technology institute (debate Tuesday, vote Thursday). Pesticides. MEPs will debate and vote on three reports on plans to modernise EU law on pesticides. These cover placing on the market and day-to-day use (both co-decision, first reading), and a sustainable use strategy (own-initiative) (debate Monday, vote Tuesday). Turkey. MEPs will debate and vote on a resolution on EU-Turkey relations and on the EU-Russia summit (Wednesday). TRIPS. The EP should vote on whether to grant its assent to amending the TRIPS agreement to enable developing countries to make or import generic substitutes for patented medicines (committee vote Monday, debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday). Car CO2 emissions. The house will debate an own-initiative report proposing that carbon dioxide emissions from cars and light commercial vehicles be capped at an average of 120g/km from 2012 (debate Monday, vote Wednesday). Tobacco smoke. An own-initiative report proposing a wide-range of restrictions on smoking in public places will be debated in plenary (Tuesday). Energy. MEPs will debate an own-initiative report on conventional energy sources, stressing the need to produce and use energy more safely, efficiently and sustainably (debate Monday). Official visit. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering, will pay an official visit to Algeria (Sunday). Sakharov Prize. President Pöttering will announce the winner of this year's edition of Parliament's prize for freedom of thought (Thursday, before the votes). LUX Prize. Parliament's European cinema prize will be presented on Wednesday by President Pöttering.
