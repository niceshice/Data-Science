The Week Ahead 1-7 November 2010
2010-10-29 - 12:20
External Parliamentary Activities Week
EU Budget 2011. Conciliation negotiations aimed at an agreement between Parliament and the Member States on the EU’s budget for the year ahead continue. EP President Jerzy Buzek will lead the Parliament delegation at the scheduled ‘trialogue’ meeting with Council and Commission representatives (Thursday).
External activities. Other than the budget talks, no official parliamentary business is scheduled to take place in Brussels, as the week is set aside for MEPs to take part in interparliamentary meetings and other working visits, or to work in their constituencies.
EP-China. A Parliament delegation will be in Beijing for the 30th EU-China Interparliamentary Committee meetings, with economic and monetary matters, research, culture and tourism among other items on the agenda (Monday-Thursday).
MEPs will also be holding talks with parliamentarians in Albania, Ukraine and Libya. A delegation will visit Beirut for talks on the security situation in southern Lebanon , and MEPs will be observing elections in Tanzania and Azerbaijan .
Finally, Parliament’s high-level contact group for relations with the Turkish-Cypriot community in the northern part of the island will be in Cyprus for its latest round of discussions.
All Saints Holiday. Please note that Parliament’s offices in Brussels are closed on Monday and Tuesday for the All Saints public holiday.
2010-11-01 Delegations Tanzania: observation mission for presidential and legislative elections A seven-strong delegation of MEPs will travel to Tanzania from 29 October to 2 November to observe the presidential and legislative elections on Sunday, 31 October.
In the partnership with the EU Election Observation Mission, MEPs will be deployed in Dar es Salaam, Zanzibar and Arusha.
Before the poll, the EP delegation will also hold talks with the President of Tanzania and the Minister of Foreign Affairs and will also meet Presidential Candidate Prof. Ibrahim LIPUMBA and the representatives of local political parties.
A press conference is scheduled for Tuesday, 2 November.
The delegation is led by Mariya NEDELCHEVA (EPP, BG). The other members are Sergio SILVESTRIS (EPP, IT), John ATTARD-MONTALTO (S&D, MT), Edward SCICLUNA (S&D, MT), Norica NICOLAI (ALDE, RO), Jacek Wlosowicz (ECR, PL) and Martin CALLANAN (ECR, UK).
12:30 19:00 Tanzania Election observation in the third countries Discussions on EU-China strategic partnership An EP delegation will take part in the 30th EP-China Interparliamentary Meeting in China from 1 to 5 November. MEPs and their Chinese counterparts will discuss in Beijing the perspectives of an EU-China strategic partnership, enonomic questions and other topics such as energy, tourism, culture, and research.
Enonomic questions to be discussed include the agricultural reform, monetary issues, trade relations, and intellectual property. Besides Bejing, MEPs will visit Wuhan and Chengdu.
The delegation is chaired by Crescenzio RIVELLINI (EPP, IT). Other delegation members are: Henri WEBER (S&D, FR), Iliana IVANOVA (EPP, BG), Helga TRÜPEL (Greens/EFA, DE), Emilio MENÉNDEZ DEL VALLE (S&D, ES), Eva LICHTENBERGER (Greems/EFA, AT), Hans-Peter MARTIN (NI, AT), Derek VAUGHAN (S&D, UK), Carlo CASINI (EPP, IT), Niki TZAVELA (EFD, EL), Zuzana ROITHOVA (EPP, CZ), and Olga SEHNALOVA (S&D, CZ).
12:30 19:00 Beijing Delegation for relations with the People's Republic of China Lebanon : MEPs to discuss the security situation in the Southern of the country Six members of Parliament's delegation for relations with the Mashreq countries will visit Beirut from 31 October to 5 November, where they will discuss the security situation in Southern Lebanon and the implementation of UNIFIL's mandate, the situation of refugees as well as the implementation of the EU election observation mission's recommendations on electoral reform.
The programme includes meetings with the Head of State, the leaders of the main political factions, students and NGOs. The Delegation will also look into the implementation of a new EU-funded project to enhance capacities of the Lebanese Parliament and visit the Nahr el-Bared refugee camp near Tripoli which was destroyed in 2007 and is now being rebuilt with EU support.
A press conference is scheduled for Tuesday, 2 November.
The delegation is led by Mário DAVID (EPP, PT). The other members are Marisa MATIAS (GUE/NGL, PT), Saïd EL KHADRAOUI (S&D, BE), Elena ANTONESCU (PPE, RO), Ria OOMEN-RUIJTEN (EPP, NL) and Frieda BREPOELS (Greens/EFA, BE).
12:30 19:00 Beirut, Lebanon Delegation for relations with the Mashreq countries Ukraine : Parliamentary Cooperation Committee and local elections A delegation of MEPs will visit Kyiv and Odessa from 30 October to 5 November. They will participate in the 15th meeting of the EU-Ukraine Parliamentary Cooperation Committee, and also observe the municipal elections on 31 October.
The delegation will meet, among others, President Viktor YANUKOVICH and Prime Minister Mykola AZAROV, as well as former Prime Minister Yulia TYMOSHENKO. MEPs will visit the field office of EU Border Assistance Mission EUBAM at the Ukraine-Moldova border.
The delegation will be chaired by Paweł KOWAL (ECR, PL). Other members are: Siiri OVIIR (ALDE, EE), Jan KOZŁOWSKI (EPP, PL), Krzystof LISEK (EPP, PL), Czeslaw SIEKIERSKI (EPP, PL), Dimitar STOYANOV (NI, BG), Hannes SWOBODA (S&D, AT).
12:30 19:00 Ukraine, Kyiv and Odessa Delegation to the EU-Ukraine Parliamentary Cooperation Committee Libya: interparliamentary meeting on framework agreement Members of the Parliament's delegation for relations with the Maghreb countries will travel to Tripoli to take part in the EP-Libya interparliamentary meeting from 1 to 5 November. Talks will focus on the EU-Libya framework agreement, immigration, health, business incentives, protection of the environment and water management.
During their visit, MEPs will meet Libyan Foreign Minister Moussa KOUSSA, secretary of the General People’s Congress Mohammed Al ZWEI, secretary of Foreign Affairs of the People’s Congress Suleiman SHAHOUMI and the secretary for legal affairs and human rights Hosni Sadegh LOUHICHI. MEPs will also visit detention centre for illegal immigrants in Touicha and the Benghazi hospital.
The EP delegation is chaired by Pier Antonio PANZERI (S&D, IT) and is composed of
Salvatore IACOLINO (EPP, IT), Inés AYALA SENDER (S&D, ES), Ana GOMES (S&D, PT),
Juan Fernando LÓPEZ AGUILAR (S&D, ES), Sonia ALFANO (ALDE, IT). 12:30 19:00 Tripoli, Libya Delegation for relations with the Maghreb countries Delegation to Cyprus 5 MEPs of Parliament's high-level contact group for relations with the Turkish Cypriot community in the northern part of the island are visiting Cyprus for the third time this year from Wednesday 3 till Friday 5 November 2010.
MEPs are to meet the President of Cyprus Demetris Christofias and the leader of the Turkish Cypriot community, Derviş Eroğlu. They are also expected to visit the newly-opened crossing point at Limnitis/Yeşilirmak and two Natura 2000 sites near Kyrenia/Girne at Alagadi and Halevga/Alevkaya.
A press conference is scheduled Friday 5 November 2010 at 11.30 at the Cyprus Community Media Centre (UN Buffer Zone).
The delegation is be led by Libor ROUČEK (S&D, CZ) and is composed Graham WATSON (ALDE, UK), Franziska KELLER (Greens/EFA, DE), Marina YANNAKOUDAKIS (ECR, UK) and Angelika WERTHMANN (NI, AT).
12:30 18:30 Cyprus Delegation visit to India Nov. 1 - Nov. 4
The delegation, led by Committee Chair Malcolm Harbour, will, among other things, look into how consumer protection, international standards and customs are handled in India with a view to gaining a better understanding of India's growth and development, also as a key EU trading partner and a global actor.

09:00 19:00 Beijing Delegation for relations with the People's Republic of China Albania: Visa facilitation to be discussed in Tirana
MEPs will meet Albanian parliamentarians in the second meeting of the EU-Albania Stabilisation and Association Parliamentary Committee from 4 to 5 November in Tirana.
The delegation is scheduled to meet Albanian Prime Minister Sali BERISHA and Jozefina TOPALI, Speaker of the Albanian Parliament. In view of the planned visa facilitation for Albanian citizens, the programme will also include a visit to a border checkpoint, and to the facility issuing biometric passports and ID cards.
