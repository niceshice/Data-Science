The Week Ahead 13-19 December 2010
2010-12-10 - 09:22
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Citizens’ initiative. Parliament will debate and vote on the draft law setting up the ‘citizens’ initiative’, which will require the Commission formally to consider proposals for EU action signed by a million citizens (debate and vote Wednesday).
EU Budget 2011. Having made sufficient progress in talks with the Council, both on the focus of the 2011 budget itself, and on their political demands, MEPs are scheduled to hold a debate (Tuesday) and vote (Wednesday).
Sakharov Prize/Human Rights. The 2010 winner of Parliament’s Prize for Freedom of Thought, the Cuban dissident Guillermo Fariñas, has been invited to Strasbourg (Wednesday), but it is still uncertain whether he will be able to attend. Parliament also makes its annual analysis of fundamental rights in the EU (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) and human rights around the world (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday).
Immigration and justice issues. Draft laws to tackle human trafficking, to give Europe-wide effect to protection orders for crime victims (debates/votes: Tuesday), to enable immigrants to make a single application for a work and residence permits, and on the status of refugees (debate Monday, vote Tuesday) are all on the agenda.
European Council/EU economy. MEPs will debate with Commission and Council plans for the EU summit, as well as the proposed permanent mechanism to deal with financial crises (Wednesday, vote on resolution on the crisis mechanism Thursday). At the summit itself, in Brussels, EP President Jerzy Buzek will address EU leaders, before holding a press conference (Thursday). European Council President Herman Van Rompuy will brief political group leaders on the outcome (Friday).
Wikileaks and Cybersecurity. Question Time with Commission President José Manuel Barroso will see group leaders put questions on any topic, with other MEPs asking about the issue of Wikileaks and cybersecurity (Tuesday).
Toy safety. MEPs will debate toy safety with the Commission (Tuesday, vote on resolution Thursday).
President Buzek’s activities. In Brussels, the EP President will meet King Abdullah of Jordan (Thursday) and Czech Prime Minister Petr Nečas (Friday).
