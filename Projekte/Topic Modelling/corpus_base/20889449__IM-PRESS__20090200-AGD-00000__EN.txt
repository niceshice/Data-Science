The Week Ahead 5-11 January 2009
2009-01-04 - 07:35
Committee meetings and political groups week
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the plenary session of 12-15 January in Strasbourg. The main items on the agenda include a debate with Czech Prime Minister Mirek Topolánek on the priorities of the Czech EU presidency, legislation to reduce the dangers of pesticides used in Europe, a series of measures on aviation issues and a debate to mark ten years of Economic and Monetary Union.
Pre-session press briefing. Changes to the plenary agenda made by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular press briefing at 11am on Friday, with Parliament’s press service and the spokespeople of the political groups on hand to comment on the session (Friday 9 January 2009, Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).

The Foreign Affairs Committee will hold an extraordinary meeting today, Thursday 8 January, on the Ukraine-Russia current gas supply dispute with Aleksey Miller, Gazprom Chair, and Oleh Dubyna, Chairman of Naftogaz, the Ukrainian gas company. The meeting takes place at 1100-1230 Room ASP 5 G 3.
Parliamentarians from Russia and Ukraine and Ukrainian ministers will also take part: the other Russian participants are Konstantin Kosachev, Chair of the Foreign Affairs Committee of the Russian Duma and Juri Lipatov, Chair of the Duma's Energy Committee. Ukraine will be represented by Hryhoriy Nemyria, Deputy Prime Minister, Yuriy Progan, Minister of Energy, and Borys Tarasyuk, Chair of European Integration Committee of the Verkhovna Rada (Ukrainian Parliament).
This meeting is jointly organised with the European Parliament's Delegation to the EU-Russia Parliamentary Cooperation Committee, chaired by Ria Oomen-Ruijten (EPP-ED, NL) and the Delegation to the EU-Ukraine Parliamentary Cooperation Committee, chaired by Adrian Severin (PES, RO).
