The Week Ahead 12-18 November 2007
2007-11-09 - 08:18
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Nicolas Sarkozy. The French President will address MEPs in a formal sitting. He will also have meetings with EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and with the Conference of Presidents. (Tuesday)
Aviation/Climate Change. A directive to include aviation in the EU’s emission’s trading scheme comes to first reading. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Soil protection. MEPs will consider legislation to protect soil from pollution and degradation, also at first reading. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Economy/globalisation. Parliament will debate with Commission and Council on ways to succeed in the age of globalisation, before voting on a resolution on economic and employment policy. (debate and vote Wednesday)
Free movement - Schengen enlargement. Following recent events in Italy, MEPs will hear a Commission statement on the application of rules allowing EU citizens to move and reside freely within the Union. (debate Monday, vote Thursday) Parliament will also be debating plans to widen the “borderless” Schengen area to Member States which joined the EU in 2004. (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday)
Pakistan. The state of emergency in Pakistan will be discussed with Commission and Council. (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday)
Commission work programme. All 27 Commissioners will be present in the Chamber for a debate on the Commission’s plans for 2008. (Tuesday)
Financial control. The EU’s Court of Auditors will present its annual report to the Budgetary Control Committee. (Monday) The committee holds a press conference to react to the report on Tuesday morning.
Charter of Fundamental Rights. A report on EP approval for the proclamation of the Charter will be put to the vote in the Constitutional Affairs Committee. (Monday)
Kosovo/elections. A delegation of MEPs will travel to Kosovo to observe the elections of 17 November. (Thursday-Sunday)
ACP-EU/Rwanda. The 14th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly begins its work in Kigali. (Saturday 17- Thursday 22 November)

These elections are an important step for Kosovo, since the Assembly will be given the task to choose a provisional President and government for the region, whose final status is still the subject of international diplomatic discussions with Serbia, under the auspices of the United Nations. The EU-Africa strategy, the Lisbon EU-Africa Summit in Lisbon in December, and developments in the Democratic Republic of Congo and Somalia, will be debated at the 14th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly (JPA), in Kigali (Rwanda) from 17 to 22 November 2007. The ACP-EU JPA brings together 78 MEPs and 78 parliamentarians from the African, Caribbean and Pacific (ACP) states. The inaugural session, at the Serena Conference Centre on Monday 19 November 2007, will be followed by a press conference at 12.30h.
The Assembly will debate and vote on three reports on:
- Impact of foreign direct investment (FDI) in the African, Caribbean and Pacific States - Astrid Lulling (EPP-ED, LU) et Timothy Harris (St Kitts & Nevis);
- Access to healthcare and medicines, with a particular focus on neglected diseases - John Bowis (EPP-ED, UK) and Martin Magga (Solomon Islands;
- Elections and electoral processes in ACP and EU countries - Miguel Angel Martinez Martinez (PES, ES) et Betty Amongi (Uganda) 15:00 20:30 Programme of the event ACP - Committee on Economic Development, Finance and Trade 18:00 20:30 Kigali, Rwanda, Serena Conference Centre, Plenary hall Programme of the event 2007-11-18 Delegations EP Delegation to the Kosovo legislative elections from 15 to 18 November 2007 The European Parliament is sending an electoral observation mission to Kosovo from 15-18 November, to observe the elections to a Kosovo-wide Assembly that will take place on 17 November.
