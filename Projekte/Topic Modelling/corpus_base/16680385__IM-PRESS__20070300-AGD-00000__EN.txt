The Week Ahead 15-21 January 2007
2007-01-18 - 09:55
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
EP Presidential Election. The first two days of the session will be devoted to events linked to the renewal of the main political offices of Parliament. On Monday, the outgoing President, Josep Borrell, will mark the end of his term in office with a speech to MEPs. On Tuesday, MEPs will elect a new President to serve for the next 2½ years, after hearing short addresses from the candidates. The fourteen posts of Vice-President and six of Quaestor will also be up for election.
Romanian and Bulgarian MEPs. The 35 Romanian and 18 Bulgarian MEPs who joined Parliament as their countries acceded to the EU on 1 January will be formally welcomed at the start of business. (Monday)
German Presidency/Angela Merkel. German Chancellor Angela Merkel and Commission President José Manuel Barroso will take part in a debate on the priorities of the new German presidency of the Council. (Wednesday)
Railway package. MEPs will hold the second reading debate of a package of legislation affecting Europe’s railways, with the issue of liberalising domestic passenger services still far from settled between Parliament and Council. (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday)
Libya/Medical staff . MEPs will discuss with Commission and Council the case of the Bulgarian and Palestinian medical staff facing a death sentence in Libya, before adopting a resolution on the issue. (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday)
Renewal of Parliamentary Committees. The Conference of Presidents will present proposals for the modified membership of Parliament’s committees during the second half of the present Parliamentary term. (announcement Wednesday, vote Thursday)
