The Week Ahead 12-18 November 2007
Institutions
2007-11-09 - 08:57
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Nicolas Sarkozy. The French President will address MEPs in a formal sitting. He will also have meetings with EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and with the Conference of Presidents. (Tuesday) Aviation/Climate Change. A directive to include aviation in the EU’s emission’s trading scheme comes to first reading. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday) Soil protection. MEPs will consider legislation to protect soil from pollution and degradation, also at first reading. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday) Economy/globalisation. Parliament will debate with Commission and Council on ways to succeed in the age of globalisation, before voting on a resolution on economic and employment policy. (debate and vote Wednesday) Free movement - Schengen enlargement. Following recent events in Italy, MEPs will hear a Commission statement on the application of rules allowing EU citizens to move and reside freely within the Union. (debate Monday, vote Thursday) Parliament will also be debating plans to widen the “borderless” Schengen area to Member States which joined the EU in 2004. (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) Pakistan. The state of emergency in Pakistan will be discussed with Commission and Council. (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) Commission work programme. All 27 Commissioners will be present in the Chamber for a debate on the Commission’s plans for 2008. (Tuesday) Financial control. The EU’s Court of Auditors will present its annual report to the Budgetary Control Committee. (Monday) The committee holds a press conference to react to the report on Tuesday morning. Charter of Fundamental Rights. A report on EP approval for the proclamation of the Charter will be put to the vote in the Constitutional Affairs Committee. (Monday) Kosovo/elections. A delegation of MEPs will travel to Kosovo to observe the elections of 17 November. (Thursday-Sunday) ACP-EU/Rwanda. The 14th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly begins its work in Kigali. (Saturday 17- Thursday 22 November)
