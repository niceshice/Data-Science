The Week Ahead 5 - 11 December 2011
2011-12-02 - 11:12
Committee meetings and political groups week
Single Permit. A deal struck with Council on a single permit to give non-EU workers similar rights to EU ones as regards working conditions, social security and access to public services will be put to the vote in the Civil Liberties and Employment committees. The permit would enable workers to obtain work and residence rights via a single procedure (Monday).
Milk negotiations. With the end of milk quotas approaching fast, MEPs, Council and Commission will hold a final trialogue meeting on Tuesday to strike a deal on a regulation aiming at strengthening milk producers' bargaining power and ensuring fairer prices for dairy farmers (Tuesday).
European Protection order . MEP in the Civil Liberties and the Women's Rights committees will vote on an agreement with Council to guarantee that crime victims who are granted protection in one EU Member State will be able to get similar protection if they move to another (Monday).
Greener motorbikes. The Internal Market Committee will vote on new rules to make motorbikes, scooters, and quadricycles safer and greener (Monday).
Climate Change. A 15-strong EP delegation travels to Durban, to take part in climate change conference negotiations for a post-Kyoto agreement (Monday to Friday) .
President's diary. On Monday, President Buzek will meet Ewa Kopacz, Marshal of the Polish Sejm and Bogdan Borusewicz, Marshal of the Polish Senat. He will also meet Nguyễn Sinh Hùng, Chairman of the National Assembly of Vietnam. On Friday, he will take part in the signing of Croatia's EU accession treaty and speak at the European Council, followed by a press conference.
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will prepare the 12-15 December plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda will feature the votes on single application for residence and work and on the Commission work programme for 2012. MEPs will also discuss the outcome of the December EU summit and results of the Polish Council Presidency.
Pre-session press briefing. The press briefing by the EP Press Service and political group spokespeople will exceptionally not take place on Friday at 11.00h in Brussels but on Monday, 12 December at 16.30h in Strasbourg (Briefing room LOW N -1/201).
