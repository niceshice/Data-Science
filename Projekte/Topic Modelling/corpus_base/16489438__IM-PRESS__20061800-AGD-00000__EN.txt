The Week Ahead 1 - 7 May 2006
2006-05-04 - 14:53
Committee meetings - Brussels
Football. UEFA president Lars-Christer Olsson and Bayern Munich president Karl-Heinz Rummenigge will be among guests at a hearing titled "Professional football - market or society?" organised jointly by four EP committees. EU legislation on football transfers, football as a social phenomenon, media issues, competition and corruption will be among the topics discussed (Wednesday).
CIA. The temporary committee on alleged illegal activities by the CIA in Europe will hear from Javier Solana, CFSP high representative and secretary-general of the Council, and Alvaro Gil-Robles, former human rights commissioner at the Council of Europe. The delegation of MEPs visiting Skopje at the end of April will report back to the committee (Tuesday).
NATO. NATO secretary-general Jaap De Hoop Scheffer appears before the Foreign Affairs Committee for the first time (Tuesday).
Roaming. A mini-hearing on roaming and its economic implications is being held by the Industry Committee and the Internal Market Committee. Representatives of telecoms companies and consumer organisations will take part (Thursday) .
Aviation. The Transport Committee votes at first reading on a draft regulation on common rules for civil aviation security (Tuesday).
Paediatric medicines. The Environment and Public Health Committee votes at second reading on a regulation on medicines specifically for use by children (Thursday).
Nuclear waste. Among other reports to be adopted this week is one at the Industry Committee on inspections of nuclear waste and spent nuclear fuel (Thursday) .
Environment. Parliament and Council delegations will try to reach agreement in a conciliation meeting on two directives: the first on batteries and accumulators, the second on the application of the Arhus Convention in the EU institutions (Tuesday, Justus Lipsius, Council) .
Torture. The Human Rights sub-committee will hold a hearing of experts on the EU's guidelines against torture. (Thursday).
Open Day in Brussels. Parliament throws its doors open to the public in Brussels for its annual Open Day. This year's theme is "worker mobility" (Saturday).

For the list of Members see Tuesday under Delegations . 09:00 12:00 Banja Luka (Republika Srpska, Bosnia) Public hearings Professional Football - Market or Society Four EP committees - Culture and Education, Economic and Monetary Affairs, Internal Market and Consumer Protection, Employment and Social Affairs - will host a p ublic hearing on "Professional Football -- Market or Society" this Wednesday, May 3rd.
Participants will tackle issues such as the future of European football; rules concerning home grown players; the relationship between sport and the Internal Market, including rules on the free movement of workers; and between the European football media market and EC competition law .
The hearing, due to take place in the hemicycle, will feature panellists such as UEFA CEO Lars-Christer OLSSON, former IOC member Hein VERBRUGGEN, Thomas KURTH, General Manager of the G-14, and Karl-Heinz RUMMENIGGE, President of Bayern München FC. 09:00 18:30 Brussels, European Parliament, Plenary Chamber To access the full programme The impact of increased fuel prices on the competititveness of the European fleet The Committee on Fisheries will hold a public hearing on "The impact of increased fuel prices on the competititveness of the European fleet" on Wednesday, 3 May. The recent rise in oil prices have had an especially strong impact on the fisheries sector, whose operating costs are to a large extent determined by the price of fuel. Experts will present a provisional study on the economic impact of rising oil prices on the European fisheries sector in general, while local representatives of the fisheries sector will give testimony about individual experiences in the Member States.
Present at the hearing will be Niels WICHMANN, Chairman of Europeche (Denmark), Javier GARAT, President of FEOPE (Spain), António SCHIAPPA, Pietra FERREIRA CABRAL, Secretary General of ADAPI (Portugal), and Pierre-Georges DACHICOURT, Chairman of CNPM (France) 09:30 12:30 Brussels, ASP 1G2 EU-India: a shift in the centre of gravity of trade policy
This mini-hearing, which is linked to an own-initiative report by Sajjad KARIM (ALDE, UK), will examine India's status as an emerging power along with the EU's role in foreign direct investment, India's new energy needs and its stance in the Doha Round negotiations. The hearing will also look at the role of the Indian diaspora in the economic growth of the country, which is located geographically between economically booming the Far East, the energy-rich Middle East and the countries of Central Asia.
Guest speakers will be: Dipak CHATTERIEE, India's Ambassador to the EU, Belgium and Luxembourg, Poonam KUMAR (Mega Ace Consultancy) and Willem VENDERGEEST, Director of the European Institute for Asian Studies (EIAS).

Committee on International Trade, 9:00-18:30, PHS 4B1 15:30 23:59 Brussels All this week's committee agendas are available on the Parliament website Public hearings Mini-Hearing on "International Roaming - its economic implications" The Committees on Industry, Research and Energy and the Committee on the Internal market and Comsumer protection jointly organise a hearing on "International Roaming - its economic implications". I t is the follow up of a hearing from 16.03.2005: (see link).
Members will discuss with experts whether progress has been reached concerning the high roaming prices and if further steps at the EU level are necessary. The Commission is momentarily working on an EU regulation on international roaming charges , which it could propose to the European Parliament and to the Council before the summer break. The new Regulation would not prescribe a specific “ideal price” for international roaming, but would require that international roaming charges are not higher than national roaming charges.
Experts invited to the hearing are Fabio Colasanti, D irector General of the Directorate General Information Society and Media of the Commission, representatives from Telecommunications companies (Gunnar Forsgren from Telia Sonera, Wolfgang Knopf from T-mobile and Richard Feasy from Vodafone, UK, as well as Erica Jacab from BEUC, the European Consumers Organisation and Paul Champsaur from the National Regulatory Authority of France. The hearing will be chaired by Giles Chichester (EPP-ED, UK) and Arlene McCarthy ( PES, UK )
