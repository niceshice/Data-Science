The Week Ahead 27 April - 3 May 2009
2009-04-24 - 13:09
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the final plenary session of 2004-2009 parliamentary term. The main items on the agenda include legislation on telecom networks, a package of measures on asylum systems, a new set of rules for the supervision of banks and legislation concerning maternity leave. There will be a debate with the Czech Council presidency on plans for the June EU summit. In addition, Hans-Gert Pöttering will make an address marking his final session as EP President.
EP/Lithuania + Poland. President Pöttering makes official visits to both Lithuania and Poland. In each country, he will meet the Presidents and Prime Minister among others (Monday - Wednesday).
Working time. Parliament and Council representatives meet for a final time in the Conciliation Committee to try to reach agreement on changes to EU rules on working time (Monday). If they succeed, this too will be put to the vote at the May plenary session.
Recovery plan. Budgetary changes needed to bring into effect the Commission's proposals for spending on energy projects as part of the effort to counteract the economic downturn will be put to the vote in the Budgets Committee (Monday).
Turkey/Women. The Women's Rights committee holds a hearing on the role of women in Turkey's social, economic and political life (Thursday).
Asylum. The Civil Liberties Committee votes on a range of measures concerning asylum provisions (Monday).
Moldova + Ecuador. A delegation of MEPs visits Chişinău to assess the situation follow Moldova's disputed elections (Monday-Tuesday) . Another delegation will be in Ecuador to observe presidential elections (Monday-Tuesday).
Pre-plenary press briefing. As Friday is a public holiday, the regular pre-session press briefing with the EP press service and political group spokespeople will take place on Monday in Strasbourg, just before the opening of the session.

Before the elections MEPs will meet several of the presidential candidates.
The presidential candidates will include Diego Delgado Jara, of the Movimiento de Integración y Transformación social (MITS – Lista 151) and Carlos González, of the Movimiento Independiente Justo y Solidario (MIJS – Lista 40). MEPs are also scheduled to meet Fernando Cordero, a candidate to be Member of Parliament for the Movimiento Alianza País (Lista 35) and current speaker of the National Assembly.
On Sunday 26 April, Ms Weber and Mr Evans will observe the vote in the town of Guayaquil; the other members of the delegation will monitor the elections in the capital, Quito.
MEPs will take part in the press conference of the EU election monitoring mission, led by Portuguese MEP Jose Ribeiro e Castro (EPP-ED). This will take place in the Swisshotel, Quito.
The other members of the EP delegation led by Renate Weber (ALDE, RO) are Agustín Díaz De Mera García Consuegra (EPP-ED, ES), Fernando Fernandez Martin (EPP-ED, ES), Robert Evans (PES, UK), Emilio Menendez Del Valle (PES, ES) and Vicente Miguel Garcés Ramón (PES, ES). 09:00 19:00 Ecuador Moldova: amid concern at human rights violations, MEPs visit Chisinau from 26 to 29 April The allegations of human rights violations that followed the publication of results of the elections of 5 April in Moldova will be the main focus of concern for a delegation of four MEPs, led by Marianne Mikko (PES, EE), who visit Moldova from 26 to 29 April on a fact-finding mission.
In Chisinau, MEPs will visit police stations and prison number 13 (Tuesday 28th). NGOs on the ground have denounced human rights violations and the inhumane detention conditions of demonstrators.
The delegation's programme includes meetings with the Civic Coalition for Free and Fair Elections known as "Coalition 2009", the Anti-Torture Alliance, the Soros Foundation, the Moldovan branch of Amnesty International, the National Council for the Prevention of Torture and the UN human rights adviser (Monday 27th).
MEPs will also have meetings with the Moldovan authorities. Talks may also be take place with the President of Moldova, the Speaker of Parliament, the Prime Minister and some ministers (Tuesday 28th).
Marianne Mikko is chair of the EP Delegation for relations with Moldova.
Other members of the delegation are Marie-Anne Isler-Béguin (Greens/EFA, FR), Marian-Jean Marinescu (EPP-ED, RO) and Karin RESETARITS (ALDE, AT). 09:00 19:00 Chisinau, Moldova 2009-04-28 The President's diary 10:15 - Meeting with Valdas Adamkus, President of Lithuania
