The Week Ahead 6-12 November 2006
2006-11-10 - 11:27
Committee meetings and political groups week - Brussels
Europe - Latin America. Parliament will host the inaugural meeting of the EUROLAT Parliamentary Assembly, bringing together MEPs and national MPs with parliamentarians from Latin America. EP President Josep Borrell will speak at the opening ceremony. (Wednesday and Thursday)
CIA/Poland. A delegation from the Temporary Committee looking into allegations of illegal CIA activity in Europe will be travelling to Warsaw for meetings with officials, NGOs and others. (Tuesday to Thursday)
Josep Borrell in Italy. The EP President will make an official visit to Italy. He will meet the President of the Republic, the Prime Minister, the Foreign Minister and the European Affairs Minister, as well as the Speakers of both chambers of the Italian Parliament. He will address the European Affairs Committees of both chambers. (Thursday to Sunday)
EP/Hong Kong. Earlier in the week, in Brussels, Mr Borrell will meet the Chief Executive of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of China, Donald Tsang. (Wednesday)
Citizens' Forums. The latest in a series of Citizens' Forums on the future of Europe, organised by the EP, take place in the Netherlands, Italy, the Czech Republic and Slovenia.
Congo/EUFOR. A delegation of MEPs will be in the Democratic Republic of Congo to see for themselves the work of the EUFOR military operation there. (Tuesday to Thursday)
Preparations for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 13-16 November. The main items on the agenda include the second reading vote on the Services Directive, a debate with the European Commission on its work programme for 2007 plus the first reading of proposals to create a globalisation adjustment fund and the presentation to the plenary of the 2005 annual report of the Court of Auditors.
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular press briefing at 11am on Friday 10 November.

The seminar will provide an opportunity to meet the key actors in one of the most important pieces of legislation - in political and industrial terms - of Parliament's 2004-2009 term of office.
Guido Sacconi (PES, IT), the author (or "rapporteur") of Parliament's report, Karl-Heinz Florenz (EPP-ED, DE), chair of the Environment Committee, and shadow rapporteurs Ria Oomen-Ruijten (EPP-ED, NL), Chris Davies ( ADLE , UK), Carl Schlyter (Greens/EFA, SV) and Jens HOLM (GUE/NGL, SV) will take part.
Parliament's role in the co-decision procedure will be explained as will the key issues of this new chemicals legislation, which is intended to balance protection of health and the environment with Europe's industrial competitiveness.
On 10 October the Environment Committee adopted its second-reading report on REACH by 42 votes to 12 with 6 abstentions. Negotiations have since begun with the Finnish Council Presidency and the Commission in the hope of reaching a compromise before the plenary vote, which is due towards the end of the year.
