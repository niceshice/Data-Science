The Week Ahead 18-24 June 2007
2007-06-15 - 10:33
Plenary Session - Strasbourg
EU Summit. Hans-Gert Pöttering will address the opening session of the European Council and will give a press conference. On the margins of the summit, the EP President will meet President of Cyprus Tassos Papadopoulos and Prime Minister of Slovenia Janez Janša (Thursday).
Middle East. Following a debate at the previous plenary session in Brussels, MEPs will vote on a resolution on developments in the Middle East (Thursday).
Portability of supplementary pension rights. MEPs will debate a first-reading report that broadly endorses the directive, but says it should apply to all supplementary schemes. (debate and vote Wednesday).
Equitable life. Parliament will debate and vote on the final report by the Committee of Inquiry into the crisis at the British life assurance society. The report calls on the UK government to compensate victims, and makes recommendations for fostering a healthy European pensions and insurance market (debate and vote Tuesday).
Spirit drinks. Parliament will put controversial new production and labelling rules to a first-reading vote. A compromise on vodka ingredients has been agreed with the Council (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Trade in cat and dog fur. MEPs will debate a complete ban on EU exports or imports of cat and dog fur. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Multiple Sclerosis . MEPs will ask the Commission what has been done to implement a code of practice for the EU on MS, following an EP resolution in 2003. (Debate Monday, vote Tuesday)
Postal services. Away from the plenary, the Transport Committee will vote on a directive on postal services. An amendment would put back the deadline for opening up all postal services to competition to 31 December 2010, two years later than the Commission proposed (Monday). A press conference on the outcome of the vote will also be held (Tuesday).
ACP/EU. The Joint Parliamentary Assembly of MEPs and parliamentarians from African, Caribbean and Pacific countries begins its latest session in Wiesbaden (Saturday and Sunday).

24th EP/China Interparliamentary meeting in Beijing and Tibet from 23-30 June 2007
From 23 to 30 June 2007, the EP's Delegation for Relations with the People's Republic of China will visit Tibet and Beijing for its 24th Interparliamentary meeting with Chinese lawmakers. The trip will begin with a two-day stay in Lhasa, and include meetings with local authorities in Tibet. The interparliamentary meeting will take place in Beijing, with two working sessions among legislators, and courtesy calls on party leaders and members of government. The EP Delegation will be led by its Chairman, Dirk STERCKX (ALDE, BE), and will include Vice-Chair Martine ROURE (PES, FR) and 22 additional Members. 09:00 19:00 Lhasa (Tibet) 13th Session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly, 23 to 28 June 2007 The ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly, which brings together 78 Members of the European Parliament and 78 MPs from African, Caribbean and Pacific countries, will discuss at its 13th session the economic partnership agreements (EPAs) and the situations in Darfur and Zimbabwe. The opening sitting on Monday 25 June will be followed by a press conference at 1pm in the Kurhaus, Wiesbaden.
The assembly will debate and vote on reports on: - migration of skilled workers and the effects on national development - Luisa MORGANTINI (GUE/NGL, IT);
- poverty reduction for small farmers in ACP countries - in particular in the fruit, vegetable and flowers sectors - Carl SCHLYTER (Greens/EFA, SE);
- good governance, transparency and accountability in relation with the exploitation of natural resources in ACP countries - Michael GAHLER (EPP-ED, DE).
The main focus of the talks will be the EPAs to be signed by the end of 2007, the deadline for the derogation granted by the WTO. Pascal LAMY, Director-General of the World Trade Organisation, will address MEPs.
Heidemarie WIECZOREK-ZEUL, German federal minister for economic cooperation and development and President-in-Office of the Council of the EU, and Timothy THAHANE, Minister for finance and development of Lesotho and President-in-office of the ACP Council, will also discuss the EPAs, which are intended to give a fresh boost to the partnership between the EU and the African, Caribbean and Pacific countries. 10:00 19:30 Wiesbaden, Germany, Kurhaus, Kurhausplatz 1 Programme of the event 2007-06-24 Delegations Delegation for Relations with the People's Republic of China
