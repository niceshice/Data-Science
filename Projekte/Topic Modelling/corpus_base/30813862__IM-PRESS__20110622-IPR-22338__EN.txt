Car industry in Belgium: EU unemployment aid approved
Budget
Plenary sittings
2011-06-23 - 12:47
A proposal to provide EU funding to help find new jobs for 2,834 workers made redundant in Belgium by General Motors Belgium and four of its suppliers was backed by the European Parliament on Thursday.
Belgium applied for €9,593,931 in European Globalisation Adjustment Fund (EGF) aid to help find jobs for 2,834 workers made redundant by General Motors Belgium and four of its suppliers in Antwerp, Dendermonde and Sint-Niklaas.
Parliament approved the aid with 560 votes in favour, 21 against, and 17 abstentions. It had been approved by the Council of Ministers on 30 May.
About the EGF
The EGF was set up to provide additional support for workers made redundant as a result of major structural changes in world trade patterns due to globalisation or the financial crisis and to help reintegrate them into the labour market. Its annual ceiling is €500 million.
