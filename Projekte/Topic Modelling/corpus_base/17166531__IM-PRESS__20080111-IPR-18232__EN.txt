The Week Ahead 14-20 January 2008
Institutions
2008-01-11 - 09:19
Plenary session – Strasbourg
Slovenian Presidency. Prime Minister Janez Janša of Slovenia will debate with MEPs the programme of his country’s presidency of the Council. He will also take part in a press conference with EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering (Wednesday). Grand Mufti of Syria. Ahmad Badr Al-Din Hassoun will be the first in a series of eminent figures invited to address a formal sitting of Parliament during the European Year of Intercultural Dialogue. He will join President Pöttering for a press conference and also take part in a debate with the Foreign Affairs Committee and the Delegation to the Mashreq countries (Tuesday). Consumer credit. Legislation setting out common rules for consumer credit, aiming to stimulate a Europe-wide market while still protecting consumers, comes to a second reading in Parliament (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday). Foreign Affairs. MEPs will debate with the Council and Commission the disputed presidential election in Kenya and its violent aftermath (debate Wednesday, vote on resolution Thursday) and the situation in Pakistan following the assassination of Benazir Bhutto (debate Wednesday). Children’s rights. Parliament will give its views on a future EU strategy on the rights of the child when it debates a report from the Civil Liberties Committee (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday). Airport charges. A directive on airport charges will be considered at first reading (debate Monday, vote Tuesday). CARS 21. MEPs will consider a report on the regulatory framework for the motor vehicle industry (debate Monday, vote Tuesday). Chinese dissident Hu Jia. One of the three finalists for the 2007 Sakharov Prize for Freedom of Thought, “cyber-dissident” Hu Jia, has been arrested by the Chinese authorities. Parliament will debate his case in the regular session on urgent human rights issues (debate and vote Thursday).
