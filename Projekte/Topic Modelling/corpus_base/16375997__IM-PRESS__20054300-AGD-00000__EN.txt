The Week Ahead 17 - 23 October 2005
2005-10-20 - 09:33
-
Sakharov Prize 2005. The selection of this year's winner of the Sakharov Prize has now been postponed to 26 October.
Commission President. At an extraordinary meeting of the Conference of Presidents open to all MEPs, Commission President José Manuel Barroso will outline the decisions taken that morning by the Commission with a view to the London summit taking place on 27 October. (Thursday afternoon)
Judicial cooperation. A meeting of MEPs and national parliamentarians will discuss ways to improve parliamentary scrutiny of judicial and police cooperation in Europe. Speakers include UK Home Secretary Charles Clarke and Commissioner Franco Frattini. (Monday and Tuesday)
Breast cancer. An exhibition and a press conference are being held to mark International Breast Cancer Awareness Month. In the evening one of Parliament's buildings will be illuminated in pink, the international colour of the fight against breast cancer. (Tuesday)
Strasbourg plenary. The political groups will be preparing for the plenary session of 24-27 October. The highlight of the session will be a debate with UK Prime Minister Tony Blair and Commission President Barroso just ahead of the London summit. MEPs will vote at first reading on the 2006 budget and also debate the progress made by Bulgaria and Romania towards accession. Any changes to the agenda decided by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the press briefing at 11am on Friday in Brussels.
President Borrell. EP President Josep Borrell has meetings this week with Adnan Terzic, Prime Minister of Bosnia (Wednesday), and Robert Kocharyan, President of Armenia (Thursday) .

09:00 12:30 Brussels - PHS Parliamentary Chamber Breast Cancer Day at the European Parliament The fight against breast cancer
October has been designated International Breast Cancer Awareness Month and the European Parliament is dedicating a day (Tuesday 18 October) to support this campaign. In recent years similar events have been held to support this campaign at major public buildings such as the Royal Palace in Amsterdam, the Leaning Tower of Pisa and the National Assembly in Paris.
- Karin Jöns (PES, DE), a member of the Women Rights Committee who drafted an own-initiative report on breast cancer in June 2003, launches the event in the morning with a press conference.
- In the evening Commissioner Markos Kyprianou opens an exhibition with the title Breast Cancer in the EU-25 .
- To crown the day's events, the Paul-Henri Spaak building will be illuminated in pink, the official colour of the campaign, at a ceremony with Parliament's President Josep Borrell and several EU Commissioners.
Detailed programme
10:00-10:30am Press conference by Karin Jöns on Breast Cancer Day in the European Union
(PHS 0A50)
6.30-8pm Exhibition - Breast Cancer in the EU-25 (ASP Building, 3rd floor exhibition area)
- The exhibition focuses on the impact of breast cancer on women within the EU and the updated EU Guidelines on Mammography Screening
- The exhibition, jointly organised by EUROPA DONNA (The European Breast Cancer Coalition) and EPGBC - The European Parliamentary Group on Breast Cancer, takes place under the patronage of the President of the EP
- Karin Jöns and Stella Kyriakides (President of EUROPA DONNA) will give an introductory speech. Commissioner Markos Kyprianou (Health and Consumer Protection) will present the 4th edition of the EU Guidelines on Mammography Screening and Specialist Breastcare Units.
9.45pm Illumination of Parliament building
- President Borrell will press the button to light up the Paul-Henri Spaak building in pink. Mr Borrell will be accompanied by Karin Jöns and other MEPs as well as Commissioners Margot Wallström, Danuta Hübner, Neelie Kroes, Viviane Reding and Vladimir Spidla.
