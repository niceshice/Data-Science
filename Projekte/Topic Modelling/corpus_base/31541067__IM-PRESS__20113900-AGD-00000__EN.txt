The Week Ahead 19-25 September 2011
2011-09-16 - 13:01
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Capital requirements for banks. The Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will examine new legislation on the level of reserves that banks must hold in order to withstand economic shocks. (Monday)
Home-made explosives. Civil Liberties Committee MEPs will vote on legislation on the marketing and use of explosives ingredients designed to restrict access to chemicals that can be used to make home-made bombs. (Monday)
Trafficking in firearms. The International Trade Committee will vote on the implementation of the United Nations Firearms Protocol which seeks to combat the illegal production and sale of firearms. (Monday)
Public procurement. The Internal Market Committee will outline its position on forthcoming public procurement legislation, aimed in particular at cutting red tape faced by small and medium-sized businesses and raising the threshold at which a contract has to be put out to public tender. (Monday)
U.S. Attorney General. Civil Liberties Committee MEPs will meet US Attorney General Eric Holder to discuss matters including data privacy and protection, transnational crime and terrorism, and enhancing US-EU law enforcement and cooperation in the area of justice and security. A press conference is scheduled for 10.30h. (Tuesday)
Preparing the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparing the 26-29 September Strasbourg plenary session. The main items on the agenda include the votes on the economic governance package, Commission President Barroso's State of the Union speech, Question Time with Eurogroup President Jean-Claude Juncker and debates with Commission Vice-President/High Representative Catherine Ashton on the Eastern Partnership Summit, the Middle East peace process and North Africa.
Pre-session press briefing. Parliament’s press service and the political group spokespeople will give their regular weekly briefing on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).
