The Week Ahead: 28 April - 4 May 2008
2008-04-25 - 12:50
External Parliamentary Activities
Eurolat. A European Parliament delegation will take part in the second plenary session of the Euro-Latin American Parliamentary Assembly (Eurolat) in Lima. MEPs and their Latin American counterparts will debate and vote on issues such as water and energy management, the Doha Agenda and poverty and social exclusion (Tuesday-Thursday).
Climate change/United States. An official delegation of the Temporary Committee on Climate Change is travelling to Washington DC to meet members of the US administration and Congress as well as NGO representatives (Monday-Wednesday).
Afghanistan. A delegation of fourteen MEPs is going to Afghanistan, where it will meet local government representatives and UN officials and visit projects that have received EU funding in two provinces (Monday-Saturday).
Brazil. From beef to biofuels, there will be no shortage of topics for discussion when MEPs visit Brazil, where they will meet their counterparts at the Brazilian parliament and the minister of agriculture (Monday-Thursday).
Charlemagne Prize. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and PES group chairman Martin Schulz will award the Charlemagne Prize for European Youth to three winners on Tuesday 29 April and hold a press point the next day in the margins of the Charlemagne Prize forum (in Aachen). President Pöttering and the prize winners will also attend the forum itself. This year's Charlemagne Prize will be awarded to German Chancellor Angela Merkel.
Parliament's offices will be closed on 1 and 2 May 2008.
2008-04-28 Delegations Meeting of EU-Georgia Parliamentary Cooperation Committee
The EP Delegation for the South Caucasus will be holding its Parliamentary Cooperation Committee meeting with Georgia next week.
The 7-Member Delegation will be led by its Chair Marie Anne Isler-Béguin (Greens/EFA, FR), and meet Parliament Chair Nino Burjanadze, Foreign Affairs Committee Chair Kote Gabashvili, as well as President Mikheil Saakashvili. The Delegation will also visit the UN Mission to Georgia UNOMIG in Gali, in the region of Abkhazia. The 6 other Members of the Delegation are: Georgs Andrejevs (ALDE, LV), Alessandro Battilocchio (PES, IT), Philip Bushill-Matthews (EPP-ED, UK), Árpád Duka-Zólyomi (EPP-ED, SK), Richard Piks (EPP-ED, LV) and Hannes Swoboda (PES, AT). 06:30 18:00 Tsibili, Georgia Delegation for Relations with the Mashreq visit to Jordan
The EP's Delegation for Relations with the countries of the Mashreq will hold its 5th Interparliamentary meeting with its Jordanian counterparts next week.
The Delegation, led by its Chair Béatrice Patrie (PES, FR), will meet with Prime Minister Nader al-Dahabi, as well as several ministers such as Foreign Minister Salaheddin Al-Bashir. MEPs will discuss issues of bilateral interest with representatives of both legislative chambers, among them Lower House Speaker Abdul Hadi Malaji and Senate Speaker Zaid Rifai. The 7-Member Delegation includes the Chair as well as: Carlo Casini (EPP-ED, IT), Magda Kósáné Kovács (PES, HU), Jamila Madeira (PES, PT), John Purvis (EPP-ED, UK), Fernand Le Rachinel (NI, FR) and Boguslaw Sonik (EPP-ED, PL). 08:30 17:00 Amman, Jordan MEPs to visit Afghanistan A 14-Member Delegation from the of MEPs will visit Afghanistan from 26 April until 2 May. The delegation will be led by Guido PODESTÀ (EPP-ED, IT), and hold meetings with representatives of the Afghan government, parliamentarians, UN officials, and the EU police mission in the country. The delegation will visit Kabul and two provincial centres to assess the effectiveness of EU projects. 09:00 19:00 Afghanistan Delegation of the Budgetary Control Committee to Sicily and Calabria Fact-finding delegation of the Budgetary Control Committee to Sicily and Calabria. 09:00 19:00 Sicily, Italy Delegation of the Regional Development Committee to Estonia On 28 - 30 April, Members of the REGI Committee will go for a delegation trip to Estonia. The main objectives of the visit are to: - get an overview of the implementation of EU Structural Funds; - get information on the capacity of their absorption by Estonian regions; - gain an insight into cross-border cooperation with Russia and Latvia Amongst the participants, there are: Evgeni KIRILOV (PES, BG) - Vice Chair of REGI, Rolf BEREND (EPP-ED, DE) - Vice Chair of REGI, Rumiana JELEVA (EPP-ED, BG), Tunne KELAM (EPP-ED, EE), Oldřich VLASÁK (EPP-ED, CZ), Brigitte DOUAY (PES, FR), Miloš KOTEREC (PES, SK), Francesco FERRARI (ALDE, IT), Toomas SAVI (ALDE, EE), Wojciech ROSZKOWSKI (UEN, PL), Dimitar STOYANOV (NI, BG). They will also visit projects of cross-border cooperation between Estonia and Latvia in Valga, as well as between Estonia and Russia. 09:00 19:00 Estonia EP ad hoc delegation to 7th session of UN Forum on Indigenous Issues
An ad hoc delegation of MEPs from the EP Human Rights Subcommittee is going to New York to attend the 7th session of the UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues, where the main item on the agenda is the impact of climate change on indigenous peoples.
The visit follows on from a public hearing held by the subcommittee on 31 March in Brussels on the implementation of the UN declaration on the rights of indigenous peoples. In the light of this hearing the EP delegation is keen to highlight violations of such rights.
The delegation, which will attend the forum's debates from Monday 28 to Wednesday 30 April, is led jointly by two MEPs: Hélène Flautre (Greens/EFA, FR), chair of the Human Rights Subcommittee, and Kinga Gál (EPP-ED, HU), vice-chair of the Civil Liberties Committee. The other members are Edit Bauer (EPP-ED, SK), Jas Gawronski (EPP-ED, IT), Martí Grau I Segú (PES, ES) and Bronislaw Geremek (ALDE, PL). 09:00 19:00 New York, USA Further information on the UN Forum MEPs in Washington to press for ambitious international agreement

The EP's Delegation for Relations with Croatia will be attending the 7th EU-Croatia Joint Parliamentary Committee Meeting next week in Opatija. MEPs will meet with their Croatian counterparts, including Mario Zubovic, Chair of the Croatian Parliament's Foreign Affairs Committee, to talk about the accession process, shipbuilding and judiciary reform, and they will also meet government representatives such as Foreign Affairs Minister Gordan Jandrokovic.
In addition, the Delegation will visit the shipyard in Rijeka, and talk to its Director as well as trade union representatives there. On the evening of 29 April in Aachen (Germany), EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and the Chairman of the Foundation of the International Charlemagne Prize, André Leysen, will award the European Charlemagne Youth Prize for projects that foster a shared sense of European identity and integration. Organised by the European Parliament and the Foundation, the prize will be awarded for the first time.
The winning projects will also serve as practical examples of Europeans living together as one community and promote European and international understanding.
The award ceremony will take place in Auditorium I of the RWTH University (Templergraben 55, 52062 Aachen) on 29 April. The event is scheduled to start at 18:00 and finish at 19:00 followed by a reception.
On 30 April from 13:15 to 13:45 a press conference will be organised in the conference hall of Haus Löwenstein (Markt 39, 52062 Aachen). It will be attended by the three winners, the EP President and the Chairman of the Foundation of the International Charlemagne Prize of Aachen. 09:00 19:00 Aachen, Germany 2008-04-30 The President's diary The President's diary

The EP's Delegation for Relations with the countries of the Mashreq will hold its 5th Interparliamentary meeting with its Jordanian counterparts next week.
The Delegation, led by its Chair Béatrice Patrie (PES, FR), will meet with Prime Minister Nader al-Dahabi, as well as several ministers such as Foreign Minister Salaheddin Al-Bashir.
