The week online: elections, energy, Iraq, Children's Day
Institutions
2007-11-19 - 16:20
This week, Parliament's website takes a look at Saturday's elections in the troubled province of Kosovo and EU elections next weekend in one of the newest EU members, Romania. Climate change and the steps you can take to reduce energy use also feature. International Children's Day on Tuesday is marked by an interview with an MEP drafting a report on children's rights. We also take a look at the Iraqi foreign minister's visit to the Parliament and the EU-ACP meeting in Rwanda.
International questions
Kosovo elections - as the MEPs return from their observation mission we hear from them.
On International Children's Day we talk to Italian MEP Roberta Angelilli (UEN), Parliament's rapporteur on children's' rights.
Also on Wednesday, an interview with Iraq's Foreign Affairs Minister after his visit to Parliament where he will hold talks with EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering.
Catch up with the ACP-EP meeting in Rwanda on Thursday. Thirteen years after the genocide, we hear about the reconciliation process.
Energy, climate
As the EU prepares the first energy package we take a look at energy use in the home and how we can all save it.
Climate change - is technology the answer is the question we will look at on Wednesday.
Elections in Romania
Ahead of European elections in Romania, we take a look at the issues, personalities and what is involved as one of the EU's newest members goes to the polls.
Last Plenary session in Strasbourg
Catch up on what happened during last week's plenary session in Strasbourg with our day by day account of all the debates and divisions.
As usual, the main events taking place during this Committee week (when MEPs gather to work on the dossiers) will be fully covered in our Press service section (first link below). Amongst the wide range of subjects under discussion are:
On Tuesday the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will meet with Eurogroup President Jean-Claude Juncker and Economic and Monetary Affairs Commissioner Joaquín Almunia.
On Wednesday, Geert Dancet, candidate to head the new EU chemicals agency, will talk to the Environment.
It's a busy week for agriculture, with Farm Commissioner Mariann Fischer Boel coming on Tueday to give a "Health Check" on the CAP and a vote reforming the EU wine market on Wednesday.
