The week ahead 16 - 22 October 2006

2006-10-13 - 10:16
Political Groups week - Brussels
Lahti summit and visit to Finland. EP President Josep Borrell will join the informal meeting of Heads of State and Government in Lahti. (Friday) This meeting is the centrepiece of his working visit to Finland, which will also include meetings with the speaker of the Finnish Parliament, Paavo Lipponen, with whom he will hold a joint press conference on Thursday, and with the European Affairs Minister. (Thursday and Friday) Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparing for the Strasbourg plenary session on 23-26 October. The main items on the agenda include the first reading of the EU’s budget for 2007 and a discussion with Commission and Council of the outcome of the Lahti meeting. There will also be a debate on the European Central Bank with its President, Jean-Claude Trichet. Pascal Lamy. The Director General of the WTO will update the International Trade Committee on the prospects for reviving multilateral trade talks. (Tuesday) EP/Norway. President Borrell with meet Torbjørn Jagland, the speaker of the Norwegian Parliament, in Brussels. (Monday) CIA/Romania. A delegation from the temporary committee investigating alleged illegal CIA activity in Europe will be travelling to Bucharest on a fact finding mission. (Tuesday to Thursday) Equitable Life/UK. Parliament’s Committee of Inquiry into the crisis of the Equitable Life Assurance Society is sending a delegation to London on Monday, for meetings with the British government, parliamentarians and officials. (Monday) Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the agenda of the Strasbourg session adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular press briefing at 11am on Friday.
