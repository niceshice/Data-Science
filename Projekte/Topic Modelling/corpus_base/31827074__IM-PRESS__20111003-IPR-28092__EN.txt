This note gives an overview of the events planned for this event and the material and facilities available for journalists.
The European Parliament Visitors' Centre is the largest parliamentary one in Europe and the first to be fully operational in 23 languages. It will use an extensive array of interactive multimedia tools to give citizens a unique insight into the European Parliament and the other European institutions. The Parlamentarium is the only part of the EU headquarters in Brussels that is accessible without an entrance pass to all citizens, 7 days a week. Entrance is free.
Information on the Parlamentarium and audiovisual material for downloading is available via the links below.
Press preview on Monday, 10 October at 15.00h
Prior to the official opening, journalists will have the opportunity to discover the Parlamentarium on Monday 10 October at 15.00h. For more information and booking please contact Constanze.Beckerhoff@europarl.europa.eu
Official inauguration, 11 October from 17.00h to 17.30h
President Buzek will inaugurate the Parlamentarium and unveil its inauguration plaque on Tuesday, 11 October at 17.00h. Her Royal Highness Princess Astrid of Belgium, European Commission Vice- President Viviane Reding and Karl-Heinz Lambertz, Minister-President of the Government of the German-speaking Community will be present.
The ceremony can be watched live via the link below. Videos and pictures will be made available via the links below.
Audiovisual press journalists wishing to follow the inauguration should meet at 16.45h at the press corner in the Parlamentarium entrance. There will be three photo opportunities, for which EP audiovisual accreditation is necessary.
Opening to the public, 14 October at 10.00h
On Friday, 14 October at 10.00h, the centre will open to the public. President Buzek together with Brussels Region Minister-President Charles Picqu√© and the mayors of the three neighbouring communes, Bruxelles, Ixelles and Etterbeek, will be cutting the ribbon at 10.00h.
