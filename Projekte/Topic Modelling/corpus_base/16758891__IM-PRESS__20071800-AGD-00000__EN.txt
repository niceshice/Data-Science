The Week Ahead 30 April - 6 May 2007
2007-04-27 - 10:11
Committee week - Brussels
Open Day at the EP. The theme of the European Parliament's Open Day 2007 is the 50th anniversary of the Treaty of Rome. This symbolic milestone will be the backcloth to debates, events, concerts and exhibitions organised for the public (Tuesday in Strasbourg, Saturday in Brussels) .
European Institute of Technology. The EIT - famous even before it is launched - does not, in the format proposed by the Commission, have a clear role or mode of operation. A joint public hearing of the EP's Industry Committee and Culture Committee will discuss the options (Wednesday) .
Hans-Gert Pöttering in Warsaw. The President of Parliament will deliver a speech at the Polish Senate as part of the ceremonies to mark the anniversary of the constitution of the union between Poland and Lithuania in 1791 (Wednesday) .
Space and defence. The Subcommittee on Security and Defence is holding a public hearing on space and European security and defence policy: space observation, space weapons and telecommunications are among the main topics of the meeting (Wednesday) .
Mercury. The Environment Committee will vote on a report on the export and storage of mercury. The amended draft regulation could take greater account of environmental issues and consumer protection (Thursday) .
Internet. An own-initiative report on broadband internet access will be put to the vote by the Industry Committee. In addition to access in rural areas, the report will look at various aspects of the "digital divide" (Thursday) .
Louis Michel. The Development Commissioner will reply to questions from members of the EP Development Committee (Thursday) .
Ombudsman. Nikiforos Diamandouros, the European Ombudsman, will present his 2006 annual report to the Petitions Committee (Wednesday) .
Cartooning for peace. To mark World Press Freedom Day, Plantu (France), Dilem (Algeria) and other well-known political cartoonists will present their initiative "Cartooning for peace" to the EP Subcommittee on Human Rights (Thursday) .
2007-05-01 Other events Extraordinary Open-Days of the European Parliament for the 50th Anniversary of the Rome Treaties "From the Rome Treaty to the Berlin Declaration: a wider and stronger Union"
Visitors will be able to follow a signposted route around the buildings (taking about 90 minutes), meeting those who are at the heart of Europe in Strasbourg: voluntary associations, organisations and institutions, who will present their European activities along a path where all the European Parliament's facilities can be seen (press room, reception area, restaurants, meeting rooms, plenary chamber), accompanied by various festive and musical activities.
From 15:30 to 17:00 a public debate will take place in the Parliament's plenary chamber on the theme of the day, with Members of the European Parliament.
This year's "traditional May European events" in Strasbourg will highlight topics such as the German presidency, the accession of Bulgaria and Romania and the 50th anniversary of the Rome Treaty.
Many other events are being held at the European Parliament as part of this Open Day, including a first "Europa 2007" stamp day, with a special post office.
Visitors will be able to reach the European Parliament by river-boat, made available free of charge by the Parliament and leaving from the jetty at Palais Rohan, or by bus from l'Aar car park and in front of the Orangerie Park.

The Committee for Industry, Research and Energy will hold a joint hearing with the Committee for Culture and Education on the creation of a European Institute of Technology (EIT). Together with various experts from universities, research organisations, and industry MEPs will discuss the objectives and functioning of the proposed EIT. The first panel will focus on the knowledge triangle of innovation, research and education which shall lay the foundations for the EIT's work. In the second panel, experts and MEPs will share their views on how the idea of an EIT can be best put into practice. Finally, Ján FIGEL, the Commissioner responsible for Education and Training, the draftswoman for the Culture Committee Erna HENNICOT-SCHOEPGES (EPP-ED, LU), and the EP's rapporteur Reino PAASILINNA (PES, FI) will draw their conclusions from the expert presentations. 09:00 12:30 Brussels, PHS 3C50 Programme of the event Sustainable Communities - "The places where people want to live and work, now and in the future" Committee on Regional Development
