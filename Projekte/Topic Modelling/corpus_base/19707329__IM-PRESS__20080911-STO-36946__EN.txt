LUX Prize 2008 - Delta, Lorna and Havel finalists
Culture
2008-09-17 - 14:37
LUX cinema prize - 3 films 1 cup Family relationships, love versus the law and the transition from communism to democracy are three themes of the films nominated for parliament's 2008 LUX prize. "Delta", "Le silence de Lorna" and "Obcan Havel" are nominees for the prize which promotes films that deal with Europe's values, culture and politics. Green MEP and EP Vice-President Gérard Onesta is the driving force behind the prize. At a press conference Tuesday he stressed the importance of culture in building ties in Europe.
The three finalists were picked by a 17-strong panel of film producers, festival directors and others who work closely in the film industry.
Sibling relations in the "Delta"
"Delta" is a German-Hungarian production is about a young man who returns to the waterways, small islands and dense vegetation of the Danube delta. Here he meets a long-lost sister and the inhabitants of the isolated villages find their subsequent relationship difficult to accept. Directed by Kornél Mundruczó , it was released this year. 92'
Lorna's snack bar silence
"Le silence de Lorna" is about a young Albanian women living in Belgium who becomes an unwanted accomplice to a wicked plan by her boyfriend to take over a snack bar. Directed by Jean-Pierre and Luc Dardenne it is a joint production by Belgian, British, French and Italian film makers. 105'
Havel's Czech journey
"Obcan Havel" looks at the influence both domestically and internationally of Czech playwright and anti-communist dissident V ác lav Havel. This is set against the difficulties of a society in transition from communism to democracy. Events in the Czech Republic and the wider international scene over the last three decades are also examined in this film directed by Mirslav Janek and Pavel Koutecky. 119'
The films will be shown to MEPs and visitors in a specially erected screen in the parliament in Brussels several times from 15 September to 17 October. Members will have the chance to select the winner as they constitute the jury and all have a vote.
Speaking at press conference Mr Onesta said the prize was one of the parliament's key "communication tools". He cited it alongside the recent African week and europarltv which aimed to make people more aware of the parliament's activities.
The winner will be announced on 22 October. The prize is for the film to be subtitled in the European Union's 23 languages and for one copy per language to be produced. There will also be adaptations for the deaf and hard of hearing. Depending on demand and budgetary constraints, there will be a special version for the blind or visually impaired. The value of the prize is estimated at €87,000.
