EP this week: ACTA, financial transaction tax, passenger rights
Institutions
2012-02-27 - 11:04
EP this week It's a packed week for MEPs with a raft of events around the controversial anti-counterfeiting trade agreement (ACTA), discussions on credit rating agencies and the financial transaction tax and meetings on economic governance and growth and fisheries policy reform. Outside EP, President Martin Schulz will address Greek MPs and a delegation heads to Burma.
ACTA
On Tuesday a 2.4 million petition against ACTA will be presented to the petitions committee at 1230 CET. That same day the industry committee will discuss the agreement, while the trade committee will debate it on Wednesday. This is the committee that will have to make a formal recommendation to the Parliament. In addition academics, civil society bodies and EU officials will have a discussion about ACTA on Thursday.
Economy
The economic and monetary affairs committee will be discussing on Tuesday how to strengthen economic and budgetary surveillance of financially unstable eurozone member states and establish common rules to assess eurozone budgets and correct excessive deficits. On Wednesday it will look at credit rating agencies, a proposal for a financial transaction tax in the EU and there will be an economic dialogue with Eurogroup president Jean-Claude Juncker.
Other items of interest
The civil liberties committee will today debate the passenger name record and the agreement on it with the US. The industry, research and energy committee will vote on Tuesday about cutting roaming charges further and improving energy efficiency, including in renovated public buildings. The transport and environment committees will be discussing aviation and emissions trading on Tuesday and Thursday respectively. The environment committee, which also covers public health and food safety, will also vote on tighter rules for baby food on Wednesday. The foreign affairs committee will be talking with ministers from Bahrain, Jordan and Quatar on Wednesday and Thursday.
Outside the EP
EP President Martin Schulz will address Greek parliament at 1800 CET on Tuesday, which will be followed by an exchange of views. Also an 11-member delegation will be visiting Myanmar until 2 March for the first interparliamentary meeting. This includes a meeting with opposition politician Aung San Suu on Wednesday.
