The Week Ahead 13-19 June 2011
2011-06-10 - 11:32
Committee meetings – Brussels
ECB: hearing of Mario Draghi. The Economics Committee will hold a hearing with the candidate to succeed Jean-Claude Trichet as President of the European Central Bank (Tuesday, vote Wednesday). The full Parliament will vote on Mr Draghi's candidacy on 23 June.
Tackling online child abuse. Parliament and Council representatives meet to try to reach agreement on proposed legislation on child protection, with the issue of how to deal with images of child abuse on the internet still to be resolved (Tuesday).
Food labelling. Another set of negotiations takes place aiming to find a second-reading agreement with Council on improvements to food labelling requirements (Tuesday).
Economic governance. MEPs and the Council also meet to negotiate on the package of legislation to improve the EU's economic governance with the aim of avoiding future crises (Tuesday-Wednesday).
EU-Serbia. Božidar Đelić, Deputy Prime Minister of Serbia, will discuss his country's application for EU membership with the Foreign Affairs Committee. The same committee also hears from János Martonyi, Foreign Minister of Hungary, on enlargement and the Western Balkans (Thursday).
Offshore safety. The Industry Committee votes on recommendations to improve the safety of offshore oil and gas activities in the light of the Deepwater Horizon disaster (Thursday).
Jerzy Buzek visits Palestinian territories and Israel. The EP President will make official visits to the Palestinian territories (Gaza Strip and West Bank) and to Israel, for meetings with political and legislative leaders, including Palestinian Authority President Mahmoud Abbas, Palestinian Prime Minister Salam Fayyad (both with press points), Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and President Shimon Peres (with press point) (Sunday-Wednesday).
Conference of Presidents in Poland. President Buzek will be joined in Warsaw by the leaders of the EP's political groups for a meeting with the Polish government ahead of Poland's presidency of the Council, which begins in July (Friday).
Whit Monday. Parliament's offices will be closed on the Whit Monday public holiday. Urgent press queries over the holiday weekend should be addressed to Andreas Kleiner on +32 498 98 33 22.
