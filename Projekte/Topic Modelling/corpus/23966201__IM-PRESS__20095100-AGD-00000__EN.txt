The Week Ahead 14-20 December 2009
2009-12-11 - 11:24
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Sakharov Prize . Oleg Orlov, Sergei Kovalev and Lyudmila Alexeyeva will receive the 2009 European Parliament Prize for Freedom of Thought on behalf of MEMORIAL and all other human rights defenders in Russia. After the ceremony, they will join EP President Jerzy Buzek for a press conference (Wednesday).
Budget 2010. Parliament will debate the EU budget for 2010 (Tuesday), before the final vote (Thursday), after which President Buzek will join the Swedish Presidency in signing it into law.
Swedish Presidency/EU Summit. Swedish Prime Minister Fredrik Reinfeldt will debate the outcome of his country’s EU Presidency – and the most recent EU summit – with MEPs and Commission President José Manuel Barroso (Wednesday).
Microfinance. MEPs will consider an EU law setting up a programme to boost microfinance facilities in Europe (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Question Time – Europe 2020. The third session of the now regular Question Time with Commission President Barroso will again feature questions on any topic from the group leaders, followed by questions from other MEPs on the issue how to follow on from the Lisbon Strategy on economic competitiveness for the ten years to come (Tuesday).
Middle East. The situation in the Middle East peace process and in particular the issue of East Jerusalem will be debated with Commission and Council (Tuesday).
Afghanistan/Pakistan . MEPs will discuss the new EU Action Plan for Afghanistan and Pakistan with the Council and Commission (Wednesday).
Foreign Affairs. Other external relations issues on the agenda include Belarus, violence in the Democratic Republic of Congo (both Wednesday, votes on resolutions Thursday) and the situation in Georgia (Tuesday).
COP 15. A delegation of MEPs will be in Copenhagen for the climate change summit (Monday-Friday). President Buzek will join them after the plenary session.

Mario Monti has been asked by the Commission to prepare a report on the relaunch the Single Market. 18:30 20:00 Strasburg, SDM S1 Watch webstreaming live Delegations Official EP-delegation to the UN Climate Conference in Copenhagen Parliament will be sending a 15-strong official delegation to the high-level segment of the 15th UN Conference of the Parties (COP) on Climate Change in Copenhagen, between 14 and 18 December. Parliament's President, Jerzy Buzek will also attend the conference, from 17 December.
MEPs will be in Copenhagen to follow the negotiations and support efforts to reach a follow-up agreement for the Kyoto Protocol. The delegation will be meeting parliamentarians from the US, Japan, Brazil and South Korea, government representatives, NGOs, international organisations, such as the International Civil Aviation Organisation, the World Bank, the Council of Europe and other players from across the world as part of a joint effort to create a strong global basis for climate protection. They will also have bilateral meetings with the UN chief negotiator on Climate Change Yvo de Boer (Tuesday) and the Danish Minister for the Climate Change Conference, Connie Hedegaard (Thursday ).
EP side event - linking cap and trade systems - a global carbon market
The European Parliament will be holding a side event on the role that international carbon trading can play in reducing the emissions that cause climate change. The event will provide an overview of existing and planned '"cap and trade" systems, including the EU emission trading system (ETS) and the planned "cap and trade" scheme in the USA. It will also look at how to link up carbon trade systems to create a common global market. (Thursday 17 December, 15.30 - 17.30, EU Pavilion).
Composition of the delegation
The official EP-Delegation will be lead by chairman Jo LEINEN (S&D, DE) and vice-chair Karl-Heinz FLORENZ (EPP, DE), and will consist of the following MEP's : Pilar DEL CASTILLO VERA (EPP, ES), Romana JORDAN-CIZELJ (EPP, SL), Iva ZANICCHI (EPP, IT) Corien WORTMANN-KOOL (EPP, NL), Dan JØRGENSEN (S&D, DK), Linda McAVAN (S&D, UK), Marita ULVSKOG (S&D, SV), Corinne LEPAGE (ALDE, FR), Satu HASSI (GREENS/EFA, FI), Miroslav OUZKY (ECR, CZ), Bairbre DE BRUN (GUE/NGL, UK), Anna ROSBACH (EFD, DK), Nick GRIFFIN (NI, UK).
