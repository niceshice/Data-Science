The Week Ahead 18-24 January 2010
2010-01-15 - 09:59
Plenary session and hearings of Commissioners-designate – Strasbourg
Commission hearings. Four further hearings of Commissioners-designate will take place (Monday-Tuesday). With all 26 hearings completed, EP President Jerzy Buzek, the leaders of the political groups and the chairs of the parliamentary committees will meet on Thursday to assess the performance of the proposed team of Commissioners.
Spanish Presidency. Spanish Prime Minister José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero will debate with MEPs the programme of the Spanish presidency of the Council during the first half of 2010. Mr Zapatero will have a bilateral meeting with President Buzek and also join him for a press conference. (Wednesday). Ombudsman election. Parliament will elect one of three candidates for the post of EU Ombudsman, responsible for investigating complaints of maladministration in the EU institutions (Wednesday).
Earthquake in Haiti. President Buzek will open the session with a statement on the earthquake in Haiti (Monday). Later, MEPs will debate the situation in Haiti with the Commission (Thursday).
Foreign Policy debates. MEPs will debate the situations in Iran, Iraq and Yemen, and EU/Tunisia relations, with the Council and Commission (Tuesday).
Copenhagen summit. Parliament will debate the outcome of the Copenhagen climate change summit with Commission and Council (debate Wednesday, vote on resolution in February).
SWIFT. MEPs will debate with the Council the situation over data protection agreements with the US covering banking data held by SWIFT (Wednesday).
ACP-EU agreement. Plans to revise the agreement on relations between the EU and African, Caribbean and Pacific countries will be debated (Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
EP President/Polish Parliament. President Buzek will address the Sejm in Warsaw and also meet leader of political parties there (Friday).

The first round is due to take place on 17 January.
The delegation is led by Polish MEP Pawel Kowal, a member of ECR group. He is accompanied by:
Andrzej GRZYB, EPP, Poland

Adina-Ioana VALEAN, ALDE, Romania
Rebecca HARMS, Greens/EFA, Germany
The European Parliament delegation is a part of the joint International Election Observation Mission that also includes the OSCE Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights (OSCE/ODIHR), the OSCE Parliamentary Assembly, the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe and the NATO Parliamentary Assembly.
Activities of the European Parliament Members in Ukraine
On 15 January in Kiev MEPs are to meet following candidates: Viktor Yushchenko (Independent Candidate), Arseniy Yatsenyuk (Independent Candidate), Anatoliy Hrytsenko (Independent Candidate), Oleksandr Moroz (Socialist Party of Ukraine), Viktor Yanukovych (Party of Regions), Volodymyr Lytvyn (People’s Party), Yulia Tymoshenko (All-Ukranian Union ‘Motherland’). There are also scheduled meetings with representatives of the Central Election Commission, the media and Ukrainian non-governmental organisations.
Chair of the EP delegation Pawel Kowal said: "Presidential elections in Ukraine will be observed with considerable interest by European public opinion as they will probably have an impact on political relations with the EU and Ukraine, including negotiations on an Association Agreement."
Press information
On Monday, 18 January, after 1300 local time, a final press conference will be held in Kiev.
In order to obtain any information about the European Parliament's observation mission in Ukraine either on the leader or other members' activities, please contact the EP Press Officer Cezary Lewanowicz who is travelling with the delegation.
