The Week Ahead 2-8 October 2006
Institutions
2006-09-29 - 11:59
Committee meetings - Brussels
Visit to India by EP President. The President of the European Parliament, Josep Borrell, will meet his Indian counterpart during an official visit to India from 30 September to 6 October, the focus of which will be economic globalisation. CIA. A delegation of the temporary committee on illegal CIA activities will be in London from 4 to 6 October to meet ministers, officials, NGOs and journalists. On Monday in Brussels, MEPs will meet the former lawyer of Abu Omar, who was kidnapped in Milan in 2003. Interception of bank data. A hearing on the interception of bank data transferred via the SWIFT system will seek to determine how far EU data protection rules have been complied with (Wednesday) . JHA. The EP holds a meeting with EU national parliaments to discuss topical justice and home affairs issues such as data protection, immigration and the management of the EU's external borders (Monday and Tuesday) . 2007 budget. The Budgets Committee will vote on no fewer than 1250 amendments during its first reading of the 2007 budget (Tuesday to Thursday) . REACH. MEPs will debate the 350 amendments tabled at second reading to the draft regulation on chemical products (REACH) ahead of the Environment Committee's vote, which is scheduled for the following week (Wednesday) . Services directive. The Internal Market Committee will also be looking at amendments for the second reading of its report on the services directive (Wednesday) . Equitable Life. A delegation of the committee of inquiry into the crisis of the Equitable Life Assurance Society travels to Dublin for a number of high-level meetings (Friday) .
