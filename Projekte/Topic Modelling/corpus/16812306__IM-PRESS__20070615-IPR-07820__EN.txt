The Week Ahead 18-24 June 2007
Institutions
2007-06-15 - 10:00
Plenary Session - Strasbourg
EU Summit. Hans-Gert Pöttering will address the opening session of the European Council and will give a press conference. On the margins of the summit, the EP President will meet President of Cyprus Tassos Papadopoulos and Prime Minister of Slovenia Janez Janša (Thursday). Middle East. Following a debate at the previous plenary session in Brussels, MEPs will vote on a resolution on developments in the Middle East (Thursday). Portability of supplementary pension rights. MEPs will debate a first-reading report that broadly endorses the directive, but says it should apply to all supplementary schemes. (debate and vote Wednesday). Equitable life. Parliament will debate and vote on the final report by the Committee of Inquiry into the crisis at the British life assurance society. The report calls on the UK government to compensate victims, and makes recommendations for fostering a healthy European pensions and insurance market (debate and vote Tuesday). Spirit drinks. Parliament will put controversial new production and labelling rules to a first-reading vote. A compromise on vodka ingredients has been agreed with the Council (debate Monday, vote Tuesday). Trade in cat and dog fur. MEPs will debate a complete ban on EU exports or imports of cat and dog fur. (debate Monday, vote Tuesday). Multiple Sclerosis . MEPs will ask the Commission what has been done to implement a code of practice for the EU on MS, following an EP resolution in 2003. (Debate Monday, vote Tuesday) Postal services. Away from the plenary, the Transport Committee will vote on a directive on postal services. An amendment would put back the deadline for opening up all postal services to competition to 31 December 2010, two years later than the Commission proposed (Monday). A press conference on the outcome of the vote will also be held (Tuesday). ACP/EU. The Joint Parliamentary Assembly of MEPs and parliamentarians from African, Caribbean and Pacific countries begins its latest session in Wiesbaden (Saturday and Sunday).
