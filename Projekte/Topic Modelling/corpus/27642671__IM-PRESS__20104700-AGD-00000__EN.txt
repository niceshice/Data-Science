The Week Ahead 15-21 November 2010
2010-11-17 - 11:18
Committee meetings and political groups week
EU Budget 2011. A special meeting of EP political group leaders will take place ahead of further negotiations between Parliament, Council and Commission on next year’s EU budget (both Monday). The Budgets Committee will meet to consider the outcome of the talks (Thursday) .
Reforming the CAP. Having unveiled plans for reform of the Common Agriculture Policy, Commissioner Dacian Cioloş debates them with MEPs in the Agriculture Committee (Thursday).
Protecting children from abuse. The Civil Liberties Committee will discuss with the Belgian presidency recent progress made towards agreement in the Council on enhanced protection for children from sexual exploitation (Monday).
Sports policy. The Culture Committee will take evidence from a range of experts on the future of EU sports policy (Thursday).
EP President – Middle East. Jerzy Buzek will hold a meeting with young political leaders from Israel, Palestine and from around the EU (Wednesday).
Human chain against poverty. A human chain will circle Parliament in Brussels to call for action to tackle poverty (Friday).
Preparing for the plenary. The political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 22-25 November plenary session. As well as the issue of the 2011 budget, the agenda includes legislation on reducing waste from electronic equipment and information on medicines. MEPs will have debates with Commission President Barroso on his institution’s plans for 2011, European Council President Van Rompuy on the October summit and ECB President Trichet on the central bank’s performance. Also on the agenda are plans for the Cancun climate change summit and the Anti-Counterfeiting Trade Agreement. Finally, the Parliament’s ‘Lux’ cinema prize will be awarded.
Pre-session press briefing. The regular press briefing takes place on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room, PHS 0A050).
