The week ahead in Parliament
Institutions
2009-02-16 - 13:48
A woman sits in the plenary chamber taking notes on her netbook The state of Europe's economy and its foreign policy feature this week in the European Parliament in Brussels. Foreign relations will feature during a two-day sitting from Wednesday whilst MPs from across Europe will join MEPs and economic experts to debate the state of the economy from Monday.
The website will host an online debate on the state of Europe's economy and what should be done next.
Some of the other key issues this week include:
Reduced VAT. Parliament will vote on plans to widen the scope for Member States to apply reduced VAT rates to certain services, including restaurants.
Foreign Policy. The EU’s High Representative for foreign policy, Javier Solana, will take part in a plenary debate on Wednesday on the Middle East and on the EU’s wider foreign policy activity.
Ecodesign and labelling. The Environment Committee will vote on a series of proposals to improve the environmental design and labelling of a range of products. The committee will also decide whether to propose blocking a move to phase out incandescent light bulbs . They will also decide legislation requiring those selling timber in the EU to take steps to minimise the risk that it is from illegal harvested sources . Pollution from ships. The Transport Committee will vote on legislation to ensure all Member States apply criminal sanctions to those causing ship-sourced pollution . Access to documents. The Civil Liberties Committee will vote on an update of the rules governing public access to EU documents . Euro-Med Gaza visit. President Pöttering will lead a Euro-Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly delegation on a fact-finding mission to Gaza and the Middle East .
You can follow these and other events here on the Parliament's website.
