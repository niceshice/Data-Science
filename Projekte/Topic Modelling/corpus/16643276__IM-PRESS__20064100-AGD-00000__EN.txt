The Week Ahead 9-15 October 2006
2006-10-10 - 17:15
Plenary session and committee meetings - Brussels
Lahti summit. The Brussels plenary will open with a debate with Council and Commission on the forthcoming informal meeting of EU leaders in Lahti on 20 October. (Wednesday)
Trade - shoe imports. The plenary will debate the Council's recent extension of anti-dumping duties on shoes from China and Vietnam, before considering a report on wider issues concerning trade defence instruments. (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday)
Fundamental rights agency. The creation of an EU Fundamental Rights Agency will also be in the session's spotlight as MEPs debate two reports on the subject. (debate and vote Thursday)
REACH. The Environment Committee will hold its second reading vote on legislation for the registration, evaluation and authorisation of chemicals. (Tuesday)
Trichet - ECB. European Central Bank President Jean-Claude Trichet appears before the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee for his regular monetary dialogue with MEPs. The committee will also vote on a report on ECB policy. (Tuesday)
Budget 2007. The marathon voting session in the Budgets Committee on the EU's 2007 budget should be completed. (Tuesday and Thursday)
EP/Iran meeting. The delegation for relations with Iran will have its first formal meeting with its counterpart from the Majlis, which will be followed by a press conference. (Monday)
EU communication policy. The Culture Committee will give its verdict on Commissioner Wallström's proposed changes in EU communication policy. (Monday)
National parliaments on budgetary control. The Budgetary Control Committee will be meeting its counterparts from national parliaments around the EU to discuss oversight of EU spending. (Monday)

On Tuesday, 10 October from 15h00 to 18h30 the Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection (IMCO) will hold a public hearing on " The Role of consumer organisations across Europe ". Speakers will include Health and Consumer Protection Commissioner Markos Kyprianou, members of consumer organisations from several EU Member States, as well as experts and scholars. In view of the first meeting of the Internet Governance Forum, scheduled to take place in Athens (30 October - 2 November 2006) as a follow up to the World Summit on Information Society (Tunis, November 2005), the ITRE Committee is organising a Conference on "The Internet Governance and the role of the Civil Society", on October 10th from 15.00 to 18.30. The conference will be based around three sessions, each of which will focus on different but interrelated topics: cultural diversity, technological neutrality as well as the role of the industrial world as part of the civil society . Its aim will be to clarify the role and the responsibility of the different actors involved (States, enterprises, users) . To this end, a series of questions will be raised and discussed including the ones of: How should governance be defined? , Citizens – Actors – Consumers: Which is the role of the civil society? ; What should be the economic model to follow the emergence of governance?
