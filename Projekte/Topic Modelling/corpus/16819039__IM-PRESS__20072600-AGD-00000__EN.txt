The Week Ahead 25 June - 1 July 2007
2007-06-22 - 11:33
Plenary Session and Committee meetings - Brussels
EP/German and Portuguese Presidencies. Chancellor Angela Merkel will report on Germany's EU Presidency and on the EU summit at an extraordinary plenary session in Brussels (Wednesday). The EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and the political group leaders then go to Lisbon for a meeting with the Portuguese government, which holds the Presidency in the second half of 2007 (Thursday-Friday).
EU Treaty. A discussion in the Constitutional Affairs Committee will give MEPs their first chance to react to the outcome of the summit. (Monday)
Roaming signature. The regulation on mobile phone roaming will be signed into law by Mr Pöttering and Mrs Merkel (Wednesday).
Serbia. The Foreign Affairs Committee will hear reports on Serbia's co-operation with the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia, first from its Prosecutor Carla Del Ponte, then from the Serbian foreign affairs minister Vuk Jeremic (Tuesday).
ACP-EU Assembly. Mr Pöttering will open the 13th session of EU-ACP Joint Parliamentary Assembly in Wiesbaden, Germany. The meeting is set to focus in particular on the proposed Economic Partnership Agreements (Monday-Thursday).
Pesticides. The proposed framework directive on the safer use of pesticides will be put to a first-reading vote in the Environment Committee (Tuesday).
Missile defence. The anti-missile shield that the USA would like to establish with bases in some EU member states will be the subject of a hearing in the Security and Defence Subcommittee (Thursday).
Darfur and Timor Leste. Five Development Committee MEPs will be in Darfur and eastern Chad to see conditions there for themselves (from Saturday until 6 July). Another group of MEPs will go to Timor Leste to observe elections (Wednesday-Monday).
European Youth Media Days. President Pöttering will welcome some 270 young journalists to Parliament to take part in a series of debates and workshops with key players from the media and the EU institutions (Wednesday to Friday).

The ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly, which brings together 78 Members of the European Parliament and 78 MPs from African, Caribbean and Pacific countries, will discuss at its 13th session the economic partnership agreements (EPAs) and the situations in Darfur and Zimbabwe. The opening sitting on Monday 25 June will be followed by a press conference at 1pm in the Kurhaus, Wiesbaden. The assembly will debate and vote on reports on: - migration of skilled workers and the effects on national development - Luisa MORGANTINI (GUE/NGL, IT); - poverty reduction for small farmers in ACP countries - in particular in the fruit, vegetable and flowers sectors - Carl SCHLYTER (Greens/EFA, SE); - good governance, transparency and accountability in relation with the exploitation of natural resources in ACP countries - Michael GAHLER (EPP-ED, DE). The Civil Liberties Committee will hold a public hearing on the protection of stateless persons on Tuesday. Academic experts and representatives from the Council of Europe and United Nations will discuss best alternatives to prevent and reduce "Statelessness" in Member States, which refers to the condition of an individual who is not considered as a national by any State under the operation of its law. It may arise as a result of a state succession, denial of a woman's ability to pass on nationality to her children, automatic loss of citizenship from prolonged residence abroad, etc. 09:00 12:30 Brussels, ASP 3E2 Programme of the event "The situation of women in prison and the impact of imprisonment of parents on social and family life" The Committee on Women's Rights and Gender Equality

The EP's Foreign Affairs Committee and its Security and Defence Subcommittee are holding a joint hearing next week with the title: "Does Europe need an anti-missile defence shield?" The recent row over US plans to establish an anti-missile defence (AMD) system, with radar installations and launch bases in EU Member States (the Czech Republic and Poland) has led to tensions with Russia, and has been a contentious point ever since. MEPs at the hearing will hear from a representative from the Missile Defence Agency of the Pentagon, a NATO representative and academics and experts on this controversial topic.
