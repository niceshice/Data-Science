The Week Ahead 26 February – 4 March 2007
2007-02-23 - 11:03
Committee Week - Brussels
Maritime safety. The lessons of the Erika and Prestige disasters have given rise to five reports on which the Transport Committee will vote this week. The aim of this "third maritime package" is to protect Europe against the dangers of accidents and pollution (Tuesday).
Intellectual property/online music. The Legal Affairs Committee will hold a first reading vote on European criminal law provisions dealing with counterfeiting and piracy. It will also vote on a report calling for a framework directive on cross-border copyright, with the aim of boosting the music industry (Tuesday).
Energy. Two hearings on European energy policy take place: at the Industry Committee, on the EU and internal aspects of energy policy (Tuesday); at the Foreign Affairs Committee, on a common foreign policy on energy (Wednesday) .
Euro. EP President Hans-Gert Poettering, ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet, Eurogroup Chair Jean-Claude Juncker, Commissioner Almunia and Nobel Prize winner Joseph Stiglitz will join a meeting of MEPs and national MPs on the euro (Wednesday and Thursday) .
Roaming. The Industry Committee will examine a draft report on the proposed roaming regulation which aims to give consumers more protection against excessive cross-border mobile phone charges (Tuesday).
Organic products. The Agriculture Committee will vote on a report on organic farming in Europe which focuses on the need to preserve consumer confidence (Tuesday).
Constitution. Following January’s Madrid conference of representatives of Member States which have ratified the constitutional treaty, the ratification procedure will be discussed at the EP Constitutional Affairs Committee (Wednesday).
Nordic dimension. At a special parliamentary conference, MEPs, national MPs and other experts will study ways of boosting cooperation and prosperity in the Baltic Sea and Arctic regions (Wednesday and Thursday).

A debate with National Parliaments Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs
Members of the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will be joined by national MPs from across the EU to debate the future prospects for the euro zone. The three main sessions are entitled “How to deal with divergence and how to seek convergence?”, “Governance and convergence: the state of play” and “Will convergence strengthen the role of the euro in the globalised world – reform of international financial institutions and global imbalances.”
Parliamentarians taking part include EP President Hans-Gert POETTERING, Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee Chair Pervenche BERES and the Chair of the German Parliament’s Economic and Technological Committee, Edelgard BULMAHN. They will be joined by Eurogroup Chair and Luxembourg Prime Minister Jean-Claude JUNCKER, ECB President Jean-Claude TRICHET, Economic and Monetary Affairs Commissioner Joaquín ALMUNIA, Portuguese Finance Minister Fernando TEIXEIRA DOS SANTOS and the Nobel Prize-winning economist from Columbia University, Joseph STIGLITZ.

15:00 19:00 Brussels, PHS 3C50 Programme of the event Parliamentary Conference on the New Northern Dimension Policy
Chaired by Diana WALLIS (ALDE, UK), Vice-President of the European Parliament
The Northern Dimension Policy brings together the relevant actors around the Baltic Sea and the Arctic to foster cooperation and increase the welfare of this region of Europe. The new policy, which entered into force on 1 January 2007, makes reference to a parliamentary forum, the establishment of which will be one of the key topics of debate at the upcoming Parliamentary Conference on the Northern Dimension on 28 February and 1 March, held at the European Parliament. Another subject of consideration will concern the practical implementation of policy priorities, especially to new areas, such as transport and energy. Participants will include MEPs and Members from several regional Inter-Parliamentary bodies, such as the Baltic Sea Parliamentary Conference, the Nordic Council, the Council of Parliamentarians of the Arctic Region, the Baltic Assembly and the Barents Parliamentary Conference. 15:00 18:30 Brussels, Plenary Chamber 2007-03-01 The President's diary .
