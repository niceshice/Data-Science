European Parliament and Pan-African Parliament seek to strengthen the parliamentary dimension of EU Africa strategy
External relations
2007-12-05 - 15:49
The European Parliament and the Pan-African Parliament are meeting this Friday, 7 December, in Lisbon on the eve of the EU-Africa summit which takes place over the weekend in the Portuguese capital. The two parliaments are planning to adopt a joint declarations calling for a strengthened parliamentary dimension for the EU-Africa strategy. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering is leading a 25-strong delegation of MEPs to the meeting.
The EU's Africa Strategy should more closely involve the European and Pan-African Parliament, according to the draft of the joint declaration set to be adopted during this inter-parliamentary meeting.
A delegation of 25 MEPs, most of them members of the EP's Development Committee, will meet an equal number of representatives of the Pan-African Parliament.
The EP President, Hans-Gert Pöttering, and the President of the Pan-African Parliament, Gertrude Mongella, will then address the Heads of State and Government gathered on 8 December for the inaugural session of the EU-Africa summit.
Practical information for the media:
Meeting venue: Assembleia da República, Sala do Senado, Lisbon
Timing: Friday 7 December, 9am-1pm
Press Conference: Presidents Pöttering and Mongella will give a joint press conference on Friday 7 December at 12.45pm, after the official signing of the joint declaration. Interviews can also be arranged.
