The Week Ahead 15-21 February 2010
2010-02-12 - 12:04
External Parliamentary Activities Week
External Activities. This week is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies or to take part in working visits of parliamentary delegations. These include interparliamentary meetings in Israel, Mauritania, Moldova and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia. A delegation from the Industry Committee will meet Spanish ministers in Madrid, discuss telecoms policy with Spanish officials in Barcelona and visit a solar power plant in Seville. MEPs will also be assessing environmental damage near Huelva in Spain, and the situation in Rosarno, Italy, after recent violent clashes between local residents and immigrants.
There are no official meetings of parliamentary bodies in Brussels this week.
Webstreaming of this week's meetings Follow the meetings on EuroparlTV 2010-02-15 Delegations Industry Committee meets Spanish Presidency in Spain
A delegation of Parliament's Industry Committee will travel to Spain to meet the Spanish Minster for Research and Innovation Cristina GARMENDIA as well as the Minister for Industry, Tourism and Trade Miguel Sebastián GASCÓN in Madrid.
MEPs also plan to visit a solar plant in Sanlúcar la Mayor and the Joint Research Centre's Institute for Prospective Technological Studies in Seville. In Barcelona the delegation will meet board members of Spain's Telecommunications Market Commission TMC to discuss the implementation of the EU's new telecommunication rules, the regulation of Next Generation Access networks and the use of the radio spectrum freed up by the switchover from analogue to digital broadcasting. The itinerary also includes a visit to the European Joint Undertaking for ITER and the Development of Fusion Energy.
The delegation will be led by Industry Committee Chair Herbert REUL (EPP, DE) and comprises Paul RÜBIG (EPP, AT), António Fernando CORREIA DE CAMPOS (S&D, PT), Norbert GLANTE (S&D, DE), Catherine TRAUTMANN (S&D, FR), Vladko Todorov PANAYOTOV (ALDE, BG), Reinhard BÜTIKOFER (Verts/ALE, DE) as well as - travelling hors quota - Pilar del CASTILLO (EPP, ES), Alejo VIDAL-QUADRAS (EPP, ES), Cristina GUTIÉRRES CORTINES (EPP, ES), Teresa RIERA MADURELL (EPP, ES), Maria BADIA I CUTHCET (S&D, ES) and Francisco SOSA WAGNER (Non-attached, ES). 09:00 19:00 Seville, Spain Website of Committee on Industry, Research and Energy 6th Inter-Parliamentary Meeting between the EP and the Mauritania A 5-strong delegation of MEPs will travel from 15-19 February 2010 in Mauritania for the 6th Inter-Parliamentary Meeting between the EP and Mauritania. Members will visit on this occasion, Tuesday, February 16, the detention centre in Nouadhibou (northern border with Western Sahara).
During their trip, members will meet President Mohamed Ould Abdel Aziz, the Prime Minister, Moulaye Ould Mohamed Laghdaf and the Minister of Foreign Affairs and Cooperation, Hamdi Ould Mouknass Nahamint (Monday). Members will also meet representatives of opposition parties: UFP, RFD and Tawassoul.
Meetings with representatives of Human Rights organisations and journalists (Monday) and with the Chairman of the National Assembly, Messoud Ould Boulkheir (Thursday), are scheduled. MEPs will hold a press conference at the premises of the EU delegation (Thursday 17.30).
The delegation headed by Pier Antonio PANZERI (S&D, IT) will consist of Cristian Dan PREDA (EPP, RO), Guido MILANA (S&D, IT), Malika BENARAB-ATTOU (Greens / EFA, FR) and Miguel PORTAS (GUE/NGL, PT). 09:00 19:00 Mauritania EP delegation to Rosarno, Italy The delegation is led by Juan Fernando LÓPEZ AGUILAR (S&D, ES), Chair of the Civil Liberties, Justice and Home Affairs Committee. The aim of the visit is to investigate several petitions concerning alleged environmental damages in the Huelva estuary by local industrial plants. The members of the delegation will meet with the petitioners, representatives of the industries involved and of the trade unions, as well as with local, regional and national authorities.
The complainants consider that toxic and radioactive discharges into the river Tinto marshes and the river Huelva have serious effects on public health and on the environment.
Members of the delegation: Pascale Gruny (EPP, FR), Victor Bostinaru (S-D, RO), Margrete Auken (Greens/EFA, DK), Carlos Iturgáiz (EPP, ES) (ex-officio), Willy Meyer (GUE, ES) (ex-officio) and Luis Yáñez (S-D, ES) (ex-officio). 09:00 20:00 Sevilla Website of the Committee on Petitions 12th meeting of the EU-Moldova Parliamentary Cooperation Committee An 11-strong delegation of MEPs will travel to Chisinau from 16 to 17 February for the 12th meeting of the EU-Moldova Parliamentary Cooperation Committee. MEPs will meet Moldovan NGOs and media representatives and Members of the Commission for Investigation of the 7 April events in Chisinau. They will also meet several Ministers including the ones responsible for Justice, Home Affairs Economy and Finances.
