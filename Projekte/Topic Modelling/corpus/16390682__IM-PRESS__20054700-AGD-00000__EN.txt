The Week Ahead 14-20 November 2005
2005-11-17 - 08:48
Strasbourg plenary session
REACH. After two years of discussion, the controversial chemicals regulatory package comes up for its first-reading vote. Parliament's main political groups have reached a last-minute compromise on the key issues of registration and the information manufacturers must supply if they wish to register substances and sell them on the European market ( debate Tuesday, vote Thursday) .
Air transport. MEPs will vote on plans for a Community blacklist of air carriers which do not meet international safety standards (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Iran. In a debate with the Council, represented by UK Foreign Secretary Jack Straw, and the Commission, MEPs will respond to the President of Iran's recent call for Israel to be "wiped off the map" (Wednesday).
Detention centres. The Commission will make a statement about the alleged existence of secret detention centres in certain European states (Monday) .
Iraq. The situation in Iraq following the constitutional referendum will be discussed with Council and Commission (Wednesday).
Enlargement. MEPs will discuss with the Commission the latter's recently published position on the EU membership aspirations of Turkey, Croatia and other Western Balkan countries (Tuesday).
Institutional affairs. The House will hear details of the Court of Auditors' 2004 annual report and the Commission's work and legislative programme for 2006 (Tuesday) . The outcome of the Hampton Court summit will also be discussed (Wednesday) .
Climate change. Following the recent hurricanes, the problem of climate change will be the subject of a debate with the Council and Commission (Wednesday) .
Afghanistan. EP President Borrell has a meeting with Afghanistan's President Hamid Karzai, who will be in Strasbourg to sign the EU-Afghanistan joint declaration with Council President Jack Straw (Wednesday) .

The 10th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly (JPA) will take place from 19 to 24 November 2005 at the International Conference Centre, Edinburgh, United Kingdom. The Plenary Session will be opened on Monday 21 by Jack McConnell, First Minister of Scotland, and will close on Thursday 24 November.
The JPA will deal with major issues relating to the ACP-EU partnership and will adopt the following reports:
The role of national parliaments in implementing the Cotonou Partnership Agreement - rapporteurs: Mauro ZANI (PES, IT) and Abubakar BAWA BWARI (Nigeria)
Agricultural and mining commodities - rapporteurs: Nirj DEVA (EPP-ED, UK) and Louis-Claude NYASSA (Cameroon)
Causes and consequences of natural disasters - rapporteurs: Fiona HALL (ALDE, UK) and Barry FAURE (Seychelles).
In addition, the JPA will discuss urgent topics such as the situation in West Africa and the registration, evaluation and authorisation of chemical substances (REACH).
The JPA will also hear and debate with the Presidency of the ACP-EU Joint Council, namely Ministers Gareth THOMAS (UK) and Olivier ANDRIANARISON (Madagascar), as well as Commissioner Louis MICHEL.
