Austria applied for €8,284,908 to help find new jobs for 356 redundant workers formerly employed by 54 firms in the Steiermark and Niederoesterreich regions. Altogether, 1,180 employees of these firms lost their jobs. A further €1,221,128 was requested for 74 workers at the printed circuit board manufacturer AT&S, in eastern Upper Styria. 167 AT&S workers lost their jobs.
The Netherlands applied for €5,884,197 for 1,764 workers made redundant by 101 firms in the graphics and printing sector. €2,649,148 was requested to help find new jobs for 800 workers made redundant by 52 firms in Zuid-Holland and Utrecht.
Another €1,849,086 was requested for 551 workers made redundant by 26 firms in Noord-Holland and Flevoland. In addition, €718,140 was requested for 214 workers made redundant by nine companies in Overijssel and €667,823 for 199 workers made redundant by 14 firms in Noord-Brabant.

About the Globalisation Adjustment Fund (EGF)
EGF aid helps find new jobs for workers made redundant due to major structural changes in world trade patterns caused by globalisation or the financial crisis. Its annual ceiling is €500 million.
