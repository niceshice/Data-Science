The Week Ahead 1-7 October 2007
2007-09-28 - 08:31
Committee Week - Brussels
Climate change. An interparliamentary meeting is being held jointly by the European Parliament and the Portuguese parliament to contribute to the debate on climate change. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and his counterpart from the Portuguese Assembly of the Republic, Jaime Gama, will hold a press conference ahead of this meeting (Monday and Tuesday, press conference Monday) .
Composition of the EP. The Constitutional Affairs Committee will adopt draft treaty amendments relating to the composition of the European Parliament (Tuesday) .
IGC. European Commission President José Manuel Barroso takes part in an extraordinary meeting of the Conference of Presidents (the EP's political group leaders) to review the preparatory work for the IGC (Tuesday) .
2008 budget. The EP's position on next year's budget will take shape when the Budgets Committee votes on the first-reading amendments (Monday-Wednesday) .
Iraq. MEPs will hold talks with their counterparts from the Iraqi parliament, led by the parliamentary speaker, at a meeting of the Foreign Affairs Committee (Tuesday) .
Turkey. The Foreign Affairs Committee votes on a draft resolution ahead of the unveiling by the Commission of its progress report on Turkey's accession to the EU (Tuesday) .
Middle East. The chair of the Knesset's foreign affairs and defence committee will be a guest at a meeting of the Subcommittee on Security and Defence (Tuesday) .
CFSP. Javier Solana, high representative for the EU's common foreign and security policy, apears before the Foreign Affairs Committee (Wednesday) .
Kinnock reforms. The Budgetary Control Committee holds a two-day hearing to assess the impact of the administrative reforms introduced by the European Commission (Tuesday-Wednesday) .
EP Cinema Prize. The procedure for choosing the first winner of the EP's Prix Lux for European cinema begins with a press conference by Vice-President Gérard Onesta (Monday) .

A 9-strong delegation of MEPs members of the International Trade Committee will visit Singapore and Vietnam from the 1 to the 4th of October to investigate the prospects for a Free Trade Agreement between the EU and ASEAN. The delegation will be led by Ms Corien Wortmann-Kool, the Committee’s 1st Vice Chair.
MEPs will meet politicians, trade officials and people from firms with European links so as to develop their understanding of the issues that will need to be addressed in the FTA negotiations and to hear at first hand a South East Asian perspective on our trade relations.
The information gathered will feed into a European Parliament report on trade and economic relations with ASEAN which will be prepared by Glyn Ford, one of the delegation members.
Members of the delegation are: Ignaci Guardans Cambó (ALDE, ES, 2nd Vice-Chair INTA), Peter Stastny (EPP-ED, SK), Glyn Ford (PES, UK), David Martin (PES, UK), Daniel Caspary (EPP-ED, DE), Christopher Fjellner (EPP-ED, SE), Ole Christensen (PES, DK), Eugenijus Maldeikis (UEN, LT), Csaba Öry (EPP-ED, HU).

09:00 19:00 Singapore & Vietnam Public hearings Hearing on desertification in the African continent Committee on Development
The EP Development Committee is holding a hearing on desertification, a phenomenon which particularly affects the African continent. The hearing will be chaired by Josep Borrell (PES, ES), chair of the Development Committee, and moderated by Luisa Morgantini (GUE/NGL, IT), who will give a welcoming speech. Guido Sacconi (PES, IT), chair of the Temporary Committee on Climate Change, will make an introductory statement. Other speakers: Patrice Burger, Director of CARI, the European network on desertification, Jacqueline Nkoyok, Executive Director of CONGAC, Coordinator of the Women and Desertification Caucus (Cameroon), and Marco Morettini, DG Development, European Commission.
10:30 12:30 Brussels, ASP 1G2 The UN and the Challenges Ahead - What Role for the EU? Committee on Foreign Affairs
The Foreign Affairs Committee will be holding a public hearing on "The UN and the challenges ahead -- what role for the EU?". This hearing will bring together experts on the ground, such as Alan Doss, Special Representative of the Secretary General to the UN Mission to Liberia, diplomats, such as Gunter Pleuger, former Permanent Representative of Germany to the UN, and EU officials, such as Pieter Cornelius Feith, the Council's Deputy Director General responsible for ESDP, and Karel Kovanda, the European Commission's RELEX Deputy Director General responsible for CFSP. MEPs will discuss with them about concrete experiences on the ground, and the reform of the UN Security Council.
