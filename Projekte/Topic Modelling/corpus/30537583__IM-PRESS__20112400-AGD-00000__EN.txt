The Week Ahead 6-12 June 2011
2011-06-01 - 16:46
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Future EU financial framework. Parliament will debate and vote on its proposals for the size and shape of the EU budget in the years after 2013 (Wednesday) .
E. coli poisoning. MEPs will debate with the Commission the recent outbreak of E. coli poisoning in Germany (Tuesday) .
Eurovignette. Legislation to ensure users of heavy goods vehicles pay for the damage they cause comes to a second-reading vote, following an agreement between MEPs and Council representatives (debate and vote Tuesday) .
Schengen enlargement. MEPs will vote on whether Romania and Bulgaria should join the border-free Schengen area (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) .
Eurozone. The Economics Committee holds discussions with Economics Commissioner Olli Rehn and Eurogroup chair Jean-Claude Juncker on the debt crisis in Greece (Monday) .
Credit rating agencies. A resolution setting out proposals on how credit rating agencies should operated in future will be put to the vote (short debate Monday, vote Tuesday) .
EU-Russia relations. A debate and vote on EU-Russia relations will be held in the light of the forthcoming EU-Russia summit (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday ) .
EP-Tajikistan. President Emomalii Rahmon of Tajikistan will meet EP President Jerzy Buzek and debate with MEPs from the Foreign Affairs Committee and Human Rights Subcommittee (Monday) .
President Buzek’s diary. The EP President will meet Nabil Al-Araby, Egyptian Foreign Minister and Secretary General of the Arab League (Wednesday) . After the session, he will make an official visit to Switzerland, accompanied by a number of EP committee chairs, for meetings with government and parliamentary leaders, including the President (Thursday-Friday) . He begins an official visit to the Palestinian Territories and then Israel on Sunday 12 June.
