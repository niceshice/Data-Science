The Week Ahead - 27 November to 3 December 2006
Institutions
2006-11-24 - 10:43
Plenary session and committee meetings - Brussels
Hearings of the Commissioners designate . The Commissioners designated by Bulgaria and Romania sit their oral exam before specialist European Parliament committees on Monday 27 November. Leonard Orban (RO) goes before the Culture Committee from 13.00h to 16.00h, and Meglena Kuneva (BG) before the Industry Committee from 16.30h to 19.30h. The evaluation of the hearings takes place the next day, Tuesday 28 November.
Brussels plenary. The session opens with a debate on the future of Europe with Irish Prime Minister Bertie Ahern. Reports on the accession of Romania and Bulgaria, and on EU-Russia relations, are also on the agenda (Wednesday and Thursday) .
Research. Also in plenary, Parliament examines, at the second reading, a series of reports on the 7th research framework programme, which is to enter into force on 1 January 2007 further to an agreement between Parliament and the Council. A press conference is to be held after the vote (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday) .
2007 budget. Following the failure of the budget conciliation on 21 November, Parliament, Council and Commission come together for an extraordinary meeting with the aim of reaching agreement ahead of the second reading of the 2007 budget (Tuesday) .
CIA. The Temporary Committee on illegal CIA activities in Europe discusses for the first time the report drafted by Claudio Fava, who will also give a press conference (Wednesday) .
Energy. The Industry Committee and the Council of Europe meet at a public hearing on Europe's energy security (Monday and Tuesday) .
Waste . The Environment Committee meeting takes its first reading vote on the framework directive on waste, and on the thematic strategy on the prevention and recycling of waste ( Tuesday ).
Venezuela . An ad hoc delegation led by Manuel Medina Ortega goes to Venezuela to observe the progress of the presidential elections ( Sunday 3/12 )
