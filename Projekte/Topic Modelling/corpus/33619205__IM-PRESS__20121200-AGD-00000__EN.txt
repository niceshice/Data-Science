The Week Ahead 19 - 25 March 2012
2012-03-16 - 11:57
Committee meetings - Brussels
Future financing. MEPs will meet national MPs and Commission and government representatives to discuss the financing of EU policies for 2014-2020, focusing on expenditure and EU own resources. A press conference with President Schulz, Danish Prime Minister Thorning-Schmidt annd Commission President Barroso is scheduled for 10.30 a.m. (Thursday).
Corporate Taxes. Draft legislation on an EU-wide corporate tax base will be put to the vote in the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee (Wednesday).
Transaction tax/financial services. The Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will debate a draft law to introduce a financial transaction tax and also broader financial service regulatory issues with Commissioners Algirdas Šemeta and Michel Barnier (Tuesday, Wednesday).
Iran/Syria. Foreign Affairs Committee MEPs will meet High Representative Catherine Ashton to discuss foreign policy developments, with Iran and Syria topping the agenda (Tuesday).
Resettling refugees. The Civil Liberties Committee will vote on legislation to set up an EU resettlement programme for refugees, aiming to improve international protection,especially of women at risk and unaccompanied minors (Wednesday).
Human Rights. The Foreign Affairs Committee will vote on its annual evaluation of human rights worldwide and related EU policies (Thursday).
Union for the Mediterranean Assembly. A delegation of 26 MEPs, will take part in the 24 and 25 March Union for the Mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly meeting in Rabat. EP President Schulz, who will be paying an official visit to Morocco, will also attend. MEPs have a key role in supporting fellow parliamentarians in the democratic transition of the Arab Spring countries. Syria and economic development will be also on the agenda (Friday-Sunday).
President's Diary. On Tuesday, President Schulz meets Switzerland's President Eveline Widmer-Schlumpf (followed by a press point). On Wednesday, he meets Chair of the Presidency of Bosnia and Herzegovina Bakir Izetbegović and President of South Sudan,Salva Kiir Mayardit. On Thursday, he meets Prime Minister of Montenegro Igor Lukšić (followed by a press point).
Press briefing. A briefing on key topics of media interest will be held on Monday at 11 a.m. (Anna Politkovskaya Room, PHS 0A050).
