The Week Ahead 11-17 July 2011
2011-07-08 - 11:31
Committee meetings - Brussels
Online child abuse. The Civil Liberties Committee is set to vote on an agreement reached with Member States' representatives on an EU law aimed at protecting children from abuse, including provisions to deal with online images of child abuse (Tuesday).
Offshore oil and gas drilling. The Industry Committee will vote on how to ensure the safety of European offshore oil and gas platforms, notably in the light of the Deepwater Horizon disaster (Tuesday).
Future of the CAP. MEPs in the Agriculture Committee will meet their counterparts from national parliaments for a debate on the reform of the Common Agricultural Policy (Tuesday).
Migration/refugees: MEPs visit Tunisia. A delegation from the Civil Liberties Committee will be in Tunisia for discussions with the Prime Minister, government members and NGOs on issues concerning both refugees and other migrants ( Wednesday-Friday ).
Events in Syria. The Foreign Affairs Committee will discuss the protests and crackdown in Syria with representatives of the Syrian opposition (Wednesday).
Polish Council Presidency. Members of the Polish government will be taking part in most of the EP committee meetings this week, to discuss the priorities for the Polish presidency of the Council during the rest of 2011 (Monday-Thursday). Waldemar Pawlak, Polish Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Economy, will hold a press conference at 13h on Tuesday (Room Anna Politkovskaya ).
EP President's activities. Jerzy Buzek will be in Dublin for an official visit to Ireland, including meetings with President Mary McAleese, Taoiseach Enda Kenny and parliamentary leaders (Monday-Tuesday). In Brussels, he will meet the leader of the Turkish Cypriot community Derviş Eroğlu (Wednesday).
