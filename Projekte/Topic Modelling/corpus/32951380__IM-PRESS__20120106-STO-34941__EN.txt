This week in Parliament: euro crisis, cyber crime, EU enlargement
Institutions
2012-01-10 - 09:30
EP building in Brussels 2012 is shaping up to be a busy year for the European Union and in the first full week after the recess MEPs will have their hands full. The economic crisis continues to bite, but new countries are still lining up to join the EU and cyber crime and privacy concerns remain high on the agenda. Also this week, catch up with EP President Jerzy Buzek on Facebook.
Economic governance, financial transaction tax - the eurozone crisis continues to top the agenda with an initial exchange of views Monday by the economic and monetary affairs committee on two proposals to further strengthen budgetary surveillance in the euro area. These proposals build on the "Six Pack" of laws beefing up the Stability and Growth Pact that were adopted last year. The committee also examined the proposal for a financial transactions tax .
Facebook chat with EP President - as Jerzy Buzek comes to the end of his 2 Â½ years as EP President, he will answer questions during a chat on Wednesday at 1430 CET. He will give a press conference Friday in Warsaw to mark the end of his term.
Also on Wednesday, the three MEPs who have already declared their candidacies for the post of president - Socialist Martin Schulz, Liberal Diana Wallis and Conservative Nirj Deva - will debate their policies live.
Passenger data and cyber crime - the Civil Liberties Committee discusses rules for the collection, storage and use within the EU of Passenger Name Record (EU PNR) data on passengers entering or leaving the EU, for the purpose of fighting terrorism and serious crime on Thursday. Measures to tackle new cyber crimes and ensure that Europe can defend its key information systems from large-scale cyber attacks are also on the agenda .
Serbia, Turkey and Belarus - Serbia's European integration process and the 2011 progress report on Turkey are on the Foreign Affairs Committee agenda Thursday. It will also meet with the Belarus delegation to discuss the EU's approach to the country since the December 2010 presidential election .
