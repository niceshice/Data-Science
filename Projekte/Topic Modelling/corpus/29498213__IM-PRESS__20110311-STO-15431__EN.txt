Buzek visits North Africa: welcomes no-fly zone over Libya
External relations
2011-03-18 - 13:07
Buzek with Béji Caïd Essebsi, Prime Minister of the Interim government in Tunis The President of the European Parliament, Jerzy Buzek, visits Tunisia and Egypt between 17 and 21 March to hold talks with official authorities, the main actors of the democratic transition, representatives of civil society and students. He will also discuss with leaders from Tunisia, Egypt and the Arab League how the resolution of the Security Council imposing a no-fly zone on Libya can be implemented.
Speaking about Thursday's UN Security Resolution President Buzek said that "I am glad and relieved that the international community has finally taken concrete action to stop Gaddafi killing his own people."
He went to say: "The European Parliament has explicitly backed the possibility of a no-fly zone in its resolution of last Thursday."
A video of the EP President statement on UN Resolution on Libya, is available at 1430 on the link below.
Earlier Mr Buzek spoke about his programme in Tunisia and Egypt.
Mr Buzek said: "First and foremost, I am here to pay tribute to the courage and determination of the Tunisian and Egyptian peoples. This is a revolution for human dignity. Second, I am here to listen: to the aspirations of the people. Third, I am here to offer Europe’s support wherever it is requested. Democracy can never be imposed from outside, but the road towards true democracy is long. We offer a new kind of partnership, oriented more towards values and people".
On Thursday and Friday, in Tunis, he met Beji Caid Essebsi, Prime Minister of the Interim government and hold bilateral meetings with political parties leaders, after addressing students at Faculty of Law and Political Science.
He will also deliver the Opening speech at the seminar on democratic transition organised by the International Federation for Human Rights and the Euro-Mediterranean Human Rights Network
Before leaving to Cairo on Saturday, Buzek will discuss the humanitarian crisis in Libya with representatives from the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees, International Organization for Migration, Red Cross and Red Crescent.
In Cairo, the President will meet the Egyptian authorities, representatives of political parties and Amr Moussa, the Secretary-General of the league of Arab States. He will also meet civil society, human rights activists and the Coalition of the Youth of the Revolution.
