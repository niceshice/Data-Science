The Week Ahead 14 - 20 April 2008
2008-04-11 - 09:45
Committee and political group meetings - Brussels
Preparing the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparing for the 21-24 April plenary session in Strasbourg. Highlights will include a Commission statement on totalitarianism, combating organ shortages and "transplant tourism", and the 2006 discharge. Also important are the EU-Latin America and Caribbean summit, the follow-up to last month's EU-US ministerial troika, road haulage and the international carriage of passengers by coach and bus.
Iraq's Prime Minister at the EP. Nouri al-Maliki will exchange views with MEPs at a joint meeting of the Foreign Affairs Committee and the Ad hoc delegation for relations with Iraq and will also meet President Pöttering (Wednesday - with press point).
Women and science. The Women's Rights Committee is to adopt a report voicing concern about the under-representation of women in the scientific world (Monday).
EU and Muslim women. The situation of Muslim women and the role of the EU will be debated by experts and NGO representatives at a Women's Rights Committee hearing (Thursday).
Trade. Economic Partnership Agreements (EPAs), currently being negotiated to renew the partnership with African, Caribbean and Pacific states will be debated at an International Trade Committee workshop attended by Commissioner Mandelson (Thursday).
Kosovo. A delegation of six MEPs led by Doris Pack, will visit Pristina to meet the President, Prime Minister and Speaker of the Kosovo Parliament (Thursday to Saturday).
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda made by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular briefing at 11am on Friday.

Community Tobacco Fund - vote on report by Sergio BERLATO (UEN, IT) 09:00 12:30 Brussels, PHC 3B050 Delegations Delegation of the Committee on Foreign Affairs to Kosovo
The Foreign Affairs Committee of the European Parliament is sending a 6-Member Delegation to Kosovo next week. Led by the Chairwoman of the EP's Delegation for Relations with South-East Europe, Doris PACK (EPP-ED, DE), the delegation will travel to Pristina to meet with President Fatmir SEJDIU, Prime Minister Hasim THACI and National Assembly Speaker Jakup KRASNIQUI. It will also visit a Serbian village to talk to local authorities.
The other 5 Members of the Delegation are Ryszard CZARNECKI (UEN, PL), David HAMMERSTEIN (Greens/EFA, ES), Marian-Jean MARINESCU (EPP-ED, RO), Hannes SWOBODA (PES, AT) and István SZENT-IVÁNYI (ALDE, HU). 09:00 19:00 Pristina, Kosovo Public hearings Workshop on Economic Partnership Agreements
Committee on International Trade The International Trade Committee, chaired by Helmut Markov (GUE/NGL, DE) is holding a workshop on Economic Partnership Agreements (EPAs). These agreements, which are to renew the partnership with African, Caribbean and Pacific states, are under negotiation. Seven "interim" EPAs - governing only trade in goods - have been signed with African and Pacific states. Only one full EPA - including provisions for opening up these countries' markets to investment and services - has been signed with the Caribbean region. Trade Commissioner Peter MANDELSON will attend this meeting. 09:00 12:30 Brussels, ASP 5G3
