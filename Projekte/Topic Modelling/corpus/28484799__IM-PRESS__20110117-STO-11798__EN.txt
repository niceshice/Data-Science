European Parliament's Journalism Prize 2011
Culture
2011-01-21 - 12:11
Jerzy Buzek at the Journalism Prize ceremony in 2010 There are four categories, written press, radio, TV and the internet Must be on European issues or about European Institutions or policies The deadline for applications is 31 March 2011 If you are a journalist and believe that your work has touched on crucial issues confronting the Europe of today then perhaps you would like to enter the European Parliament's Journalism Prize 2011? It is also open to those journalists who have promoted a better understanding the EU's Institutions or policies. There are four categories, written press, radio, TV and the internet and the work must have been published or broadcast between 1 April 2010 and 31 March 2011.
A look at the 2010 winners shows the variety and depth of talent that entered the competition.
Last year Witold Szabłowski of the Polish daily Gazeta Wyborcza won the written press award for his article "Today two bodies will wash ashore" about illegal immigration.
James Clive-Matthews of the UK won the Internet award for his blog article "EUtopia - What percentage of laws come from the EU?".
Kajsa Norell and Nuri Kino of Sveriges Radio Ekot (Sweden) won the radio award for an investigative report on EU financial support to Turkey.
Zsolt Németh of MTV Hungary won the TV award category for his "Euforia" programme, which presents the history of the European Union in an understandable and entertaining way to younger people
The deadline for applications is 31 March 2011 inclusive. The award will be made in October - please see the website below for more details.
