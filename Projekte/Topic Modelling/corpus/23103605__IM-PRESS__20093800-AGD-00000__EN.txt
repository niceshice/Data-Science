The Week Ahead 14-20 September 2009
2009-09-11 - 09:31
Plenary Session – Strasbourg
Commission Presidency. José Manuel Barroso will debate his candidacy for a new term as Commission President with MEPs (Tuesday afternoon) ahead of a vote on his appointment (Wednesday).
EP President’s inaugural speech. Following his election as EP President in July, Jerzy Buzek will address Parliament, setting out his priorities for his term in office. There will then be reactions from the political groups, the Council and the Commission (Tuesday). Mr Buzek will hold a press conference after the debate.
Commissioners-designate. Parliament will vote on whether to approve the appointments of three new proposed Commissioners to fill vacancies in the current European Commission whose mandate runs to the end of October (Monday).
EU summit – G20 summit. Parliament will debate preparations for the G20 summit in Pittsburgh on the economic and financial market crisis with the Council and Commission (Wednesday). President Buzek will then join EU heads of state and government in Brussels for an informal European Council on the same issue (Thursday).
Milk prices. MEPs will debate the problems facing dairy farmers as a result of low milk prices and vote on legislation extending a temporary programme of buying up milk powder and butter (Thursday).
Forest fires. This summer’s devastating forest fires, in particular in Greece, will be the subject of a debate with the Commission (debate Monday, vote on resolution Tuesday).
Data protection – SWIFT. MEPs will hold a short debate with the Council presidency and vote on a resolution on the latest developments regarding the access the United States authorities have to bank transfer data via SWIFT (Wednesday).
