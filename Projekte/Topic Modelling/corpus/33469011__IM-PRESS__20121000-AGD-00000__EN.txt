The Week Ahead 5-11 March 2012
2012-03-02 - 11:44
Committee and political group meetings - Brussels
International Women's Day. Parliament will mark International Women's Day on 8 March with a meeting between MEPs and national MPs on "Equal pay for work of equal value", which will assess the impact of measures to reduce the gender pay gap and consider possible further initiatives to tackle its causes. President Martin Schulz will open the debate. On 7 March, a gender equality workshop for journalists, where Women's Rights Committee chair Mikael Gustafsson, will present Eurobarometer survey results on "Gender inequality in Europe".
President's diary. On Wednesday, President Schulz will meet Palestinian women to discuss the situation of women in the Middle East, Pál Schmitt, President of Hungary, Serzh Sargsyan, President of Armenia (followed by a press point) and Jean-Claude Mignon, President of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe.
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will prepare for the 12 - 15 March plenary session, in Strasbourg, where there will be votes on cross-border inheritances, the international cocoa agreement, EP electoral law reform and the progress of Iceland, Bosnia and Herzegovina and the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia towards possible future EU membership. Parliament will also set out its position on the 2013 EU budget, vote a resolution on gender equality and debate the outcome of the 1-2 March EU spring summit.
Pre-session press briefing. The regular briefing by the EP Press Service and political group spokespeople will be held on Friday at 11 a.m. (Anna Politkovskaya Room, PHS 0A050).
