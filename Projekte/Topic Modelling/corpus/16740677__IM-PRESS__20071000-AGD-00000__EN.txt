The Week Ahead 5-11 March 2007
2007-03-02 - 10:28
Political groups week, Brussels
Spring European Council . Hans-Gert Pöttering will take part in his first European Council as EP President, and will hold a press conference at the start of the meeting (Thursday). On the fringe of the Council, he will hold meetings with Lech Kaczynski, President of Poland, Victor Yushchenko, President of Ukraine, Boris Tadic, President of Serbia, Gediminas Kirkilas, Prime Minister of Lithuania, Ivo Sanader, Prime Minister of Croatia, and Zeljko Sturanovic, Prime Minister of Montenegro, the Foreign Minister of Portugal, Luis Amado, the CFSP High Representative, Javier Solana, and Sir Nicholas Stern, author of an influential report on climate change.
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the coming week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 12 to 15 March. The agenda includes debates on the Berlin Declaration of 25 March marking the 50th anniversary of the Rome Treaty, and on the outcome of the spring summit.
Women's Day. To mark International Women's Day (8 March), the Women's Rights Committee is holding a seminar entitled "Women building the Future of Europe" . President Pöttering, former EP President Nicole Fontaine, the German Minister for Economic Cooperation and Development, Heidemarie Wieczorek-Zeul, and the Commissioner for Social Affairs and Equal Opportunities, Vladimir Spidla, will take part (Thursday) .
The future of Europe. Parliament is continuing its programme of citizens' forums in the Member States as part of its contribution to the debate on the future of Europe. A number of MEPs will take part in these meetings, which are being hosted by Paris (France), Stockholm (Sweden), Cardiff (United Kingdom) and Valga (Estonia) on Monday, Wednesday, Thursday and Friday respectively.
EU/Israel. President Pöttering will meet the Israeli Foreign Minister, Tzipi Livni. (Monday)

Seminar on the occasion of International Women's Day
The seminar will highlight the achievements and milestones in the area of women's rights and gender equality over the past 50 years, as well as looking at persisting inequalities and gaps. The second half of the seminar will concentrate on the future and define what the main challenges are for women's advancement. The focus will be on the Lisbon strategy, women on the labour market, the demographic challenge and the integration of migrant women.
Among the participants will be Heidemarie WIECZOREK-ZEUL (German Federal Minister for Economic Cooperation and Development), Vladimir ŠPIDLA (European Commissioner for Employment, Social Affairs and Equal Opportunities) and Anita GRADIN (former European Commissioner for Immigration, Home Affairs and Justice).
09:00 12:00 Brussels, European Parliament, ASP 5G3 Programme of the hearing Other events Seminar for journalists from DG Information : Women's Day in the European Parliament, in the context of 2007 as European year of Equal opportunities
The year of the 50th anniversary of the Treaty of Rome is also the year of Equal Opportunities for all. In this context, the committee on women's rights is organising a seminar to highlight the achievements along these years, to look at persisting inequalities and gaps and to define main challenges for the future.
