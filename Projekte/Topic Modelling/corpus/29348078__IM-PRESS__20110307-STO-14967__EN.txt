A future member takes their first steps? With Libya's rebellion, euro stability, financial transaction tax, Roma rights, Iran policy, Turkish membership and industrial policy on the plate, MEPs also found time for such diverse issues as swine flu, volcanic ash crisis, protein and rare mineral shortage. All of it - in the backdrop of the 100th Women's Day celebrations.
Taxing financial transactions
On Tuesday, the Parliament voted in favour of a tax on financial transactions. Apart from generating revenue which could be used to support sustainable development, MEPs saw this as a mechanism to reduce speculation and improve the safety of the financial sector.
Turkey and Montenegro accession talks
Working on accession to the EU, Turkey disappointed the MEPs, while Montenegro was applauded, but still criticised for its corruption. Parliament has concerns about the slow progress on human rights and institutional reforms in Turkey. MEPs expressed their hope that accession talks with Montenegro could begin this year.
International Women's Day
Female MEPs dressed as men to draw attention to gender inequality in Europe. In plenary, celebrating the centenary of the International Women's Day, MEPs and their invitees spoke in favour of equal pay, more women in top jobs, and protecting working mothers.
Libya
MEPs demanded the enforcement of a no-fly zone over Libya and the recognition of rebel authorities to help the insurrection against the regime. They also proposed to create a "Marshall plan" for the region in order to give the people a perspective of peace and democracy.
Innovative industrial policy
Europe's industry that creates almost 60 million jobs is feeling dire consequences of economic downturn and increasing competition. According to MEPs, it can survive globalisation only by moving to innovation, resource efficiency and knowledge economy, supported by smart industrial policy.
Securing supply of rare earth elements
MEPs demanded Commission to ensure that EU is not hurt by imminent shortage of imported rare earth elements (REE), essential for green, medical and military technologies.
Responsibility and solidarity
There were they key concepts during Wednesday's address of the president of Hungarian republic to the Plenary. The former MEP summed up the priorities of HU presidency and highlights the EP as 'soul of the EU.'
2 in 1
MEPs adopted on Wednesday the schedule of Parliament's plenary sessions for 2012 and 2013. By secret ballot, MEPs adopted amendments to the calendars so that two sessions in Strasbourg will be held during a single calendar week in October.
11th March: European Day on Remembrance of Victims of Terrorism
On Thursday, the Parliament observed a minute of silence in memory of the victims of terrorism. President Buzek said: "no terrorist or any criminal organization is able to weaken our faith in the values which built our united Europe".
Volcanic Ash crisis
MEPs asked the Commission about the measures foreseen to avoid another crisis like the one caused by the eruption of an Icelandic volcano on May last year. The economic consequences of the crisis, passengers'' safety, better coordination and forecasting mechanisms were some the issues mentioned during the debate. Many MEPs pointed out that the implementation of the single European sky will help to better managing the air space in the future.
