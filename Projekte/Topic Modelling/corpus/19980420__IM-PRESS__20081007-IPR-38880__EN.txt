Opening of the Brussels plenary session: Changes to the agenda
Institutions
2008-10-08 - 16:06
Vice-President Rodi KRATSA-TSAGAROPOULOU (EPP-ED, GR) announced several changes to the final draft agenda of the Brussels plenary session including that the debate on the preparation of the European Council will also cover the situation of the global financial system and the title of this debate will be modified accordingly. The tabling of a motion for resolution of the ECON committee in this respect shall be included in the votes of October II part-session in Strasbourg.
Changes to the agenda
The following changes to the agenda were adopted at the start of the sitting of 8 October:
Wednesday 8 October
The debate on the preparation of the European Council will also cover the situation of the global financial system and the title of this debate will be modified accordingly.
The tabling of a motion for resolution of the ECON committee in this respect shall be included in the votes of October II part-session in Strasbourg.
The Ieke VAN DEN BURG (PES, NL)/ Daniel DĂIANU (ALDE, RO ) report on the Lamfalussy follow up - Future Structure of Supervision (foreseen on Thursday) will be debated after the European Council preparation and world financial system situation debate.
The sitting will continue without interruption until midnight.
Thursday 9 October
As the van den Burg / Daianu report has been moved to Wednesday, the Carlos CARNERO GONZÁLEZ (PSE, ES) report will become the first item on the agenda on Thursday morning.
The following item will be included in voting time:
The report by Jo LEINEN (PES, DE) (A6-0372/2008) on a proposal for a decision concerning the approval of a Joint Declaration on "Communicating Europe in Partnership", adopted on 7 October by the AFCO committee according to Rule 43(1).
