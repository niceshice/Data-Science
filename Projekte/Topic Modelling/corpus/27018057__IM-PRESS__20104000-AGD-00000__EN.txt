The Week Ahead 27 September - 3 October 2010
2010-09-30 - 11:24
Committee meetings – Brussels
Budget 2011. The Budgets Committee holds its main vote on the EU's budget for next year – with Parliament for the first time having an equal say with Member States on the whole of the budget (Tuesday-Thursday).
Roma in Europe. The Civil Liberties and Employment Committees will hold a discussion with three Commissioners to follow up on the recent debates on the situation of the Roma in Europe (Wednesday).
Financial crisis. ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet has his regular discussion with the Economics Committee (Monday). Later, Parliament's Special Committee examining the financial market crisis is due to vote on its interim report (Wednesday).
Jacob Zuma. The President of South Africa will take part in a meeting of the Foreign Affairs and Development Committees and a bilateral meeting with EP President Jerzy Buzek (Wednesday).
EEAS representatives. The Foreign Affairs Committee holds the first of its meetings with newly-appointed Heads of EU delegations and Special Representatives (Wednesday).
Medicine information. The Environment Committee will vote on new rules covering information provided by pharmaceutical companies on prescription medicines (Tuesday).
Emissions limits from vans. The same committee will vote on new emission standards for light commercial vehicles (Tuesday).
Country of origin labels. The Trade Committee votes on a controversial proposal concerning the labelling of imported goods with their country of origin (Wednesday).
Security and Defence. MEPs meet national MPs from around Europe to discuss the EU's security and defence policy (Tuesday).
President's activities. Jerzy Buzek will attend the funeral of former EP President Egon Klepsch (Monday) and take part in events in Bremen marking the 20th anniversary of German reunification (Sunday). He will also formally sign Parliament's environmental policy before the Environment Committee (Tuesday).
