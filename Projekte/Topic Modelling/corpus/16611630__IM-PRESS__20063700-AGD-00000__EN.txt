The Week Ahead - 11 to 17 September 2006
2006-09-12 - 17:11
Committee week - Brussels
Official visits. President Pervez Musharraf of Pakistan comes to Parliament next week, where he will be received by EP President Josep Borrell and attend meetings of the Conference of Presidents and the Foreign Affairs Committee. Mr Borrell also has a meeting with Gloria Arroyo, President of the Philippines. (Wednesday)
CIA. The temporary committee on illegal activities of the CIA will hear from Spanish Foreign Minister Miguel Ángel Moratinos as well as three victims. This will be the first time the committee has met since President Bush admitted the existence of illegal CIA prisons. (Thursday)
Passenger data. The Civil Liberties will discuss with Commissioner Frattini the outcome of talks on the EU-US agreement on access to personal data of air passengers. (Tuesday)
Sakharov Prize. The names of candidates for the 2006 Sakharov Prize will be presented before the Human Rights Subcommittee, the Foreign Affairs Committee and the Development Committee. (Tuesday)
Services of general interest. The Economic Affairs Committee votes on a report on the Commission's white paper on services of general interest in the internal market. The EU has yet to decide whether a legal framework is needed for these services of key importance to the public. (Tuesday)
2004 discharge. The Budgetary Control Committee votes on the second draft report on Parliament's 2004 budget discharge, which was refused in the spring in view of complications relating to the purchase of Parliament's buildings in Strasbourg. (Thursday)
Nuclear non-proliferation. The future of nuclear non-proliferation will be the subject of a public hearing at the Subcommittee on Security and Defence. (Thursday)
Immigration. An event to raise awareness about the protection of illegal immigrants is being held on Parliament's premises. A concert by Italian singer Claudio Baglioni takes place in the Plenary Chamber and will be preceded by a press conference. (Wednesday)

Following several recent international challenges to nuclear non-proliferation, such as the US-Indian agreement and the Iran nuclear crisis, the EP's subcommittee on security and defence is organizing a public hearing on the future of the nuclear non-proliferation treaty (NPT). The hearing will bring together academics (Professor William C. POTTER from the Monterey Institute, USA and Bruno TERTRAIS from the Fondation pour la Recherche Stratégique, France), EU policymakers (Annalisa GIANNELLA, Javier SOLANA's personal representative for matters of non-proliferation, and Lars-Erik LUNDIN, Head of Unit, Deputy Political Director, Security Policy in RELEX, European Commission) and civil society (Stephen PULLINGER, Director of ISIS Europe and Janet BLOOMFIELD, Honorary Vice-President of the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament, UK ).
