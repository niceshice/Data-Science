The Week Ahead 28 November - 4 December 2005
2005-11-30 - 16:41
Brussels plenary session and committee meetings
Plenary session
WTO talks. In the light of efforts on all sides to play down expectations of major progress in the world trade talks, Parliament will debate the prospects for December's WTO Ministerial Meeting in Hong Kong with the Council and Commission. (Wednesday)
Bird flu. Two reports on avian influenza come before the House. They are aimed at toughening up measures to be taken in the event of crises and introducing strict surveillance of both low and high pathogenic forms of the virus. (Wednesday)
Drugs patents. MEPs vote at first reading on proposals for issuing licences to allow developing countries facing major health problems to import generic drugs. (Wednesday)
Committees
Agriculture. Commissioners Mandelson (Tuesday) and Fischer Boel (Thursday) will address the Agriculture Committee on the subjects of the WTO talks and the plans to reform the Community sugar market. The committee also adopts its report on the sugar reform (Tuesday) .
Economic affairs. Following the ECB's signal that interest rates are about to rise, the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will question the president of the euro group, Luxembourg Prime Minister Jean-Claude Juncker (Tuesday), and Economics Commissioner Joaquin Almunia (Monday) on the prospects for the European economy.
Enlargement. The Foreign Affairs Committee votes on a report on accession negotiations with candidate countries Turkey, Croatia and FYROM and on negotiations for association and stabilisation agreements with Albania, Bosnia-Herzegovina and Serbia Montenegro. The report also deals with the future status of Kosovo. (Tuesday)
2004 budget discharge. The annual discharge process gets under way at the Budgetary Control Committee with a series of meetings with Commissioners and Members of the Court of Auditors. (Monday and Tuesday)
EP President. Josep Borrell will attend the Euromed summit of Heads of State and Government in Barcelona marking the 10th anniversary of the Euromediterranean Partnership. (Monday)
Charlemagne Prize debate. Five former winners of the Charlemagne Prize will take part in a special debate on the future of Europe with MEPs. (Thursday)

15:00 18:30 Brussels, PHS 3C050 Other events - Launch of the State and Outlook Report on the European environment
In cooperation with the EP Committee for the Environment, the European Environment Agency (EEA) will launch its third "State and Outlook Report" on the European environment at the EP on Tuesday. This third report since 1994 is a 570 pages document, full of facts, figures and maps giving a global overview of the evolution of our environment since five years, but also its trends and prospects for the coming years. The Agency is required to produce such a report every five year. It is a firsthand tool to support strategic action in the EU and beyond.
The report analyses the main environmental challenges that Europe faces, including climate change, air pollution and health, biodiversity. It gives a huge number of indicators on environment and economic sectors. It also provides a detailed country level analysis.
The main conclusions will be presented in a joint press conference by:
Prof. Jacqueline McGLADE, Executive Director of the EEA;
Karl-Heinz FLORENZ, chairman of the EP Committee for the Environment;
Stavros DIMAS, Commissioner for the Environment.

Europa - Quo Vadis?
A special debate on the future of Europe will be held at the European Parliament on Thursday 1 December, in the afternoon following the plenary session Organised by the EP, the City of Aachen and the Charlemagne Prize Foundation, a panel of four former winners of the Charlemagne Prize will discuss the future of Europe with an invited audience. The debate will be shown live on Europe by Satellite.
Panel
Gro Harlem Brundtland, former Prime Minister of Norway (1994 winner);
Jacques Delors, former President of the European Commission (1992 winner);
Gyula Horn, former Prime Minister of Hungary (1990 winner);
György Konrád, novelist, former President of the Academy of Arts, Berlin (2001 winner)
The discussion will be moderated by Pat Cox, former President of the European Parliament and winner of the prize in 2004. The current EP President, Josep Borrell, will open the event, with other participants including André Leysen of the Charlemagne Prize Foundation, Jürgen Linden, Mayor of Aachen, Belgian Prime Minister Guy Verhofstadt, other former Charlemagne Prize laureates and the leaders of Parliament's political groups. Prince Philippe of Belgium will be guest of honour.
The International Charlemagne Prize, for distinguished service on behalf of European unification, has been awarded annually by the City of Aachen since 1950.
Location: Yehudi Menuhin hall, 1st floor, PHS building
Time: 3pm Thursday 1 December 2005
The event can be followed on EbS in the EP Press Room.
