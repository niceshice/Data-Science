The Week Ahead 18-24 September 2006
2006-09-19 - 16:44
Political groups week - Brussels
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparing for the Strasbourg plenary session of 25-28 September. The main points on the agenda include a debate on the EU's area of freedom, security and justice, as well as reports on Turkey's progress towards EU accession, air quality and services of general interest.
Romanian Prime Minister. Calin Popescu-Tariceanu, Prime Minister of Romania, will meet EP President Josep Borrell and the leaders of Parliament's political groups. He will also hold a joint press conference with Mr Borrell. (Thursday)
Moldovan Parliamentary speaker. Mr Borrell will also be meeting Marian Lupu, Speaker of the Parliament of the Republic of Moldova. (Wednesday)
Citizens' Forums. The first in a new round of Citizens' Forums on the future of Europe, organised by Parliament as part of the 'period of reflection', take place in Rokycany (Wednesday) and Horazdovice (Friday) in the Czech Republic.
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the regular press briefing at 11am on Friday.
2006-09-18 Delegations Delegation to Turkey from 18-22 September 2006 A delegation of five MEPs of the Human Rights Subcommittee is travelling to Turkey on 18 September for a four-day fact-finding mission. The five MEPs will see Sakharov Prize winner Leyla Zana in Ankara and have meetings with a number of human rights organisations, the Turkish parliament's human rights investigation committee and with government ministers. The MEPs will also visit the most eastern Turkish province of Hakkari to talk to the authorities and NGOs there. At the end of their stay they will be in Istanbul to meet with journalists and writers and with religious communities. On Friday morning at 10.30am the MEPs will hold a press conference in the Information office of the European Commission in Istanbul.
The five MEPs on the delegation are Hélène FLAUTRE (Greens/EFA, FR), chair of the Human Rights Subcommittee, Simon COVENEY (EPP-ED, IR), Richard HOWITT (PES, UK), Laima ANDRIKIENE (EPP-ED, LT) and Vittorio AGNOLETTO (GUE/NGL, IT). 09:00 19:00 Turkey 2006-09-19 The President's diary Meeting with ambassadors from the ACP countries - 17:30 19:00 Brussels Press conferences - 11:00 "Costs of lacking a Constitution" - Jo LEINEN (PES, DE), President of the Committee on Constitutional Affairs
