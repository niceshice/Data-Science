The Week Ahead - 11-17 June 2007
2007-06-08 - 11:03
Committee meetings and political groups week
Future of Europe. Talks on this subject continue at a new interparliamentary meeting being held at the EP jointly with the Bundestag. The conclusions of this debate between MEPs and national MPs will be communicated to the European Council taking place on 21 and 22 June. The workshops on Monday will look at climate change, the architecture of the new treaty and the role of national parliaments in the reform process (Monday and Tuesday).
This event will open with a press conference before the official proceedings begin. Participants: EP president Hans-Gert Pöttering, Bundestag speaker Norbert Lammert and Giuliano Amato, Italian minister of the interior and former vice-president of the Convention (Monday, 2.30pm, PHS 00A50).
ECB. Following the European Central Bank's decision to raise its key interest rate to 4%, ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet will appear before the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee as part of the regular monetary dialogue with the EP (Monday).
Visits by the President. EP president Hans-Gert Pöttering will meet Robert Zoellick, president-designate of the World Bank, as well as Romanian prime minister Calin Popescu-Tareceanu, and CFSP high representative Javier Solana (Tuesday) .
EIT and Galileo. The Budgets Committee is due to adopt its position on the method for funding the European Institute of Technology (EIT). A resolution on the funding of Galileo, the European satellite navigation system, is also on the committee's agenda (Monday) .
Preparations for the June plenary in Strasbourg. The political groups will be preparing for the plenary session of 18 to 21 June. Key points on the agenda will include the portability of pension rights, definitions and labelling of wines and spirits, and the final vote on the report of the committee of inquiry into Equitable Life. Outside the plenary, the Transport Committee will vote at first reading on amendments to the directive on postal services (this vote will be on the evening of Monday 18 June) .

As part of its contribution to the debate on the future of Europe and the period of reflection following the 'no' votes on the constitution in France and the Netherlands, the European Parliament is organising a series of citizen's forums to enable members of the public directly to enter the debate on the main issues. This forum, in Madrid, will focus on "50 years of the Treaty of Rome: 50 years of building Europe".
Participants: Miguel Angel MARTÍNEZ MARTÍNEZ (PES, ES), Carlos CARNERO GONZÁLEZ (PES, ES), Salvador GARRIGA POLLEDO (EPP-ED, ES), José Maria GIL ROBLES, ex-President of the European Parliament. 09:00 19:00 Madrid, Spain Joint Parliamentary meeting on the Future of Europe:"Together… but how?" Members of the national parliaments and of the European Parliament will meet, for the third time, in Brussels on the EP's premises to discuss the 'Future of Europe' on 11 and 12 June 2007. This conference will give citizens' national and European representatives an opportunity to discuss the key issues which are of concern to them.
The first two meetings, held on 8 and 9 May and on 4 and 5 December 2006, helped to the re-launch, at national level, of the debate on the future of Europe. The issues to be addressed during the meeting, in working parties and in plenary, will include: "the new challenges for the EU, especially climate change and energy security", "architecture and substance of a future Treaty" and "the role of national parliaments in the reform process".
