The Week Ahead 26 March - 1 April 2007
Institutions
2007-03-23 - 10:15
Plenary session and Committee meetings - Brussels
Plenary session
Berlin Declaration. German Chancellor Angela Merkel and Commission President José Manuel Barroso will take part in a debate on the Berlin Declaration (Wednesday) .
Missiles. CFSP High Representative Javier Solana will present the EU's foreign policy plans for 2007 and discuss the USA's plans for an anti-missile shield in Europe (Thursday).
Budget. A European tax, the British rebate and funding of the CAP are the focus of a report on the review of the own-resources system (Wednesday) .
Football. MEPs will look at the need for EU legislation on professional football as well as television rights and better management of competition between clubs (Wednesday) .
Maritime safety. The lessons of the Erika and Prestige disasters are the subject of two reports which are part of the "third maritime package" (Wednesday) .
In committee
Personal data. A public hearing organised by the Civil Liberties Committee will look at the protection of transatlantic data swapping, with the focus on SWIFT, PNR and international principles (Monday) .
Life+. An EP-Council conciliation will try to reach agreement on the regulation governing Life+, the EU's financial instrument for the environment. (Tuesday).
Iraq. A delegation from the Iraqi National Assembly appears at the Foreign Affairs Committee on Wednesday morning. On Tuesday the committee votes on reports on reforms in the Arab world, Croatia, the CFSP in 2005 and human rights in the world in 2006.
Official visits. President Pöttering has a meeting with Ukrainian prime minister Viktor Yanukovich on Tuesday, followed by a meeting with the press. On Thursday Mr Pöttering meets Nebojsa Radmanovic, President of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Federation.
