The Week Ahead 31 October - 6 November 2011
2011-10-28 - 12:57
External Parliamentary Activities
President Buzek/Tunisia. After his visit to Libya, President Buzek will pay an official visit to Tunisia, including meetings with Prime Minister Beji Caid Essebsi and representatives of civil society and youth. The President's visit comes just a few days after the European Parliament awarded the 2011 Sakharov Prize to five representatives of the Arab people, in recognition and support of their drive for freedom and human rights, one of the winners being Mohamed Bouazizi , a Tunisian market trader who set himself on fire in protest at incessant humiliation and badgering by the Tunisian authorities (Monday).
Turkey. Consumer protection, market surveillance, customs checks and product safety will be discussed by a delegation of 13 Internal Market Committee MEPs, visiting Ankara and Istanbul. They will meet Turkish government ministers and representatives of Turkish industry, business and consumer organisations (Wednesday-Friday).
Gaza/Egypt/Israel. A delegation of six MEPs travels to Gaza to meet members of the Palestinian Legislative Council and representatives of the UN Relief and Works Agency and NGOs, before moving on to Cairo, where they meet the Egyptian Foreign Affairs Minister Mohamed Kamel Amr (Sunday-Thursday). Women's Rights Committee MEPs go to Israel for talks with their Knesset counterparts, and will also visit Gaza to assess the situation of women there (Tuesday-Friday)
Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia. A joint parliamentary meeting will be held in Skopje. The country's accession prospects will be on the agenda (Wednesday-Friday).
President Buzek/Balkans. On Wednesday, Mr Buzek pays an official visit to Croatia, including meetings with Prime Minister Jadranka Kosor and Parliament's Speaker Luka Bebić. On Thursday, he visits Serbia, to meet President Boris Tadić, Prime Minister Mirko Cvetković and National Assembly Speaker Slavica Đukić Dejanović. On Friday and Saturday, President Buzek pays an official visit to Bosnia and Herzegovina, including meetings with House of Peoples Speaker Ognjen Tadić, and House of Representatives Speaker Denis Bećirović, He will also visit and lay a wreath at the Potočari genocide memorial, Srebrenica.

The main theme of the visit is "the situation in Russia on the eve of the elections to the Duma". The Members will be debriefed by the Head of EU Delegation in Moscow, Mr Valenzuela, and representatives of the diplomatic corps accredited in Moscow. In order to widen the picture meetings with independant Russian and international journalists, as well as various political foundations will be organised. The Delegation will also meet with the heads of fractions of the Duma as well as parties, which are not represented in the parliament. It will meet with the the Russian Central Electoral Committee in order to discuss the preparations for the elections and the current electoral law in Russia (the topic of election law standards was discussed during the last EU-Russia PCC in Warsaw on 19-20 September with the participation of ODIHR and the Russian CEC). The Delegation will also meet with the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs to discuss the state of play of the EU-Russia relations.
