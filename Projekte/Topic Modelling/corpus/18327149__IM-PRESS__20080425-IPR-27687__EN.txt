The Week Ahead: 28 April - 4 May 2008
Institutions
2008-04-25 - 13:00
External Parliamentary Activities
Eurolat. A European Parliament delegation will take part in the second plenary session of the Euro-Latin American Parliamentary Assembly (Eurolat) in Lima. MEPs and their Latin American counterparts will debate and vote on issues such as water and energy management, the Doha Agenda and poverty and social exclusion (Tuesday-Thursday).
Climate change/United States. An official delegation of the Temporary Committee on Climate Change is travelling to Washington DC to meet members of the US administration and Congress as well as NGO representatives (Monday-Wednesday).
Afghanistan. A delegation of fourteen MEPs is going to Afghanistan, where it will meet local government representatives and UN officials and visit projects that have received EU funding in two provinces (Monday-Saturday).
Brazil. From beef to biofuels, there will be no shortage of topics for discussion when MEPs visit Brazil, where they will meet their counterparts at the Brazilian parliament and the minister of agriculture (Monday-Thursday).
Charlemagne Prize. EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering and PES group chairman Martin Schulz will award the Charlemagne Prize for European Youth to three winners on Tuesday 29 April and hold a press point the next day in the margins of the Charlemagne Prize forum (in Aachen). President Pöttering and the prize winners will also attend the forum itself. This year's Charlemagne Prize will be awarded to German Chancellor Angela Merkel.
Parliament's offices will be closed on 1 and 2 May 2008.
