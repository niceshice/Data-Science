November 2010, things we learnt in the Plenary

2010-11-26 - 12:55
It is time to start for the November plenary session in Strasbourg. "Wear rose petals to show your support to women". It is president Jerzy Buzek speaking, in the opening of the session. November 25 was the UN International Day for the Elimination of Violence against Women, a moment to remember all the women still suffering violence, rape and humiliation. The president of European Central Bank Jean-Claude Trichet Environmental issues on the stage, waiting for the start of the UN climate conference in Cancun, Mexico next Monday. MEPs discussed the European strategy with Jo Leinen, chair of the Environmental Committee. Irish bailout & EU budget dominate week MEPs unite in welcoming release of Aung San Suu Kyi With the Irish bailout, EU budget and climate crises taking centre stage at the lively November plenary sitting, MEPs discussed the situation with Commission head José Manuel Barroso and ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet and issued a common resolution on the 2011 budget. They also approved stricter rules on prescription drugs, heard Georgian President Mikheil Saakashvili and welcomed the release of Burmese opposition leader Aung San Suu Kyi.
MEPs also awarded Austrian Director Feo Aladağ Parliament's 2010 LUX film prize for her film "Die Fremde".
As the Session opened EP President Jerzy Buzek, welcomed Burmese dissident leader's Aung San Suu Kyi release from house arrest and confirmed that the Sakharov prize laureate has been invited to attend the European Parliament at a future date.
On Tuesday almost all political groups stood firm behind Parliament's effort to apply the rules decided under the Lisbon Treaty in the negotiations with the governments concerning the EU budget for 2011 and beyond.
With the fiscal crisis engulfing Europe after the Irish bailout, European Central Bank President Jean-Claude Trichet was asked by MEPs to take a more proactive role in defending stability and the plenary held a very lively discussion and what can and must be done at the political level.
Georgian President Mikheil Saakashvili addressed the plenary, making a formal commitment to only seek a negotiated solution to his country's problems with Russia, which earned him President Buzek's commendation for showing "wisdom".
MEPs backed plans to keep unprofitable mines in Germany, Spain and Romania open till 2014 while support for the miners is set up to help their transition after the mines finally close.
Days after severe floods struck several EU countries leaving many dead and millions of Euros worth of damage, Parliament called for the EU solidarity fund to be deployed to help the farms and companies worst hit by the disaster.
The Cancun climate talks and the EPs mission to the summit were discussed by MEPs while on Tuesday, Environment Committee chairman, Jo Leinen (S&D), chatted with Parliament friends on Facebook.
Au strian filmmaker Feo Aladag was awarded Parliament's 2010 LUX film prize for "Die Fremde", her dark, moving exploration honour killings and the tensions between family and community. Akadimia Platonos by Filippos Tsitos and Illegal by Olivier Masset-Depasse were the runners-up this year.
Patients taking prescription drugs will receive more information on exactly what and how their medicine does and how it should be taken, following rules approved by the plenary, which also draw a clearer line forbidding open advertising of medicines.
In a widely reported incident on Wednesday morning, the session was suspended for a few minutes and a member was expelled from the chamber after he verbally attacked S&D group leader Martin Schulz using a Nazi slogan.
Finally, MEPs passed a resolution welcoming the newly-finalised but yet-to-be-signed multilateral Anti-Counterfeiting Trade Agreement, ACTA , as a step in the right direction, but called on the European Commission to ensure that it will have no impact on basic freedoms such as internet access.
