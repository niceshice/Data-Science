The Week Ahead - 6-12 March 2006
2006-03-14 - 11:28
Group week - Brussels
Women's Day. To mark International Women's Day on 8 March, Parliament is holding a seminar on the growing phenomenon of forced prostitution at major international sports events, looking ahead in particular to this summer's World Cup in Germany. The Vice-President of UEFA and several European Commissioners are scheduled to speak. (Wednesday)
President Borrell in Algeria. Parliament's President Josep Borrell makes an official visit to Algeria, during which he will meet the president and prime minister. (Monday to Wednesday)
Financial Perspective. The Budgets Committee meets to assess the state of play in the talks on the next Financial Perspective. (Monday)
Victims of terrorism. A ceremony takes place at Parliament to commemorate the victims of terrorist attacks in Europe, notably those in Madrid and London. (Friday)
CIA. The temporary committee investigating allegations that the CIA illegally transported prisoners around Europe continues its work. (Monday)
Euromed. The sensitive issue of freedom of speech and respect for religious beliefs is among topics on the agenda of the Euro-mediterranean Parliamentary Assembly's political affairs committee. (Monday)
Strasbourg session. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the coming week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 13-16 March, during which the President of the Palestinian Authority, Mahmoud Abbas, and the President of Germany, Horst Köhler, will both address the House. Parliament will also discuss the upcoming European Council and hold debates on the free movement of workers, company relocations and trafficking in human beings (see above - Women's Day).
Any changes to the plenary agenda decided by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the press briefing on Friday 10 March.
2006-03-06 The President's diary Official visit to Algeria President Borrell makes an official visit to Algeria from 6 to 8 March 2006, his final visit to the region as president of the Euromed Parliamentary Assembly .
He will meet President Abd El-Aziz Bouteflika, Prime Minister Ahmed Ouyahia and Foreign Minister Mohamed Bedjaoui as well as Amar Saadani, Speaker of the National People's Assembly, and Abdelkader Bensalah, Speaker of the Council of the Nation. He will give a speech to an audience of members of the two chambers.
Mr Borrell will also have meetings with representatives of civil society, including NGOs, trade unions and employers.
