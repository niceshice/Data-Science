The Week Ahead 1-7 June 2009
2009-05-29 - 10:03
European Parliament Election Week
European Parliament Elections. Some 375 million voters across Europe are eligible to vote in the seventh set of direct elections to the European Parliament, which take place from Thursday to Sunday 4-7 June, according to different national traditions.
Election results night. On Sunday night, as results start to come in, Parliament’s press service offers a range of facilities for both the written and audiovisual media to keep up with the latest exit polls, early results and preliminary projections of the make-up of the new Parliament. Press conferences linking up the political group leaders in cities around Europe will be broadcast in the Parliamentary chamber. Parliament’s press room will be open on Saturday from midday to midnight, on Sunday from midday to 03.00h on Monday, and on Monday morning from 8h.
Press briefing on the election night. A Press Service technical briefing on press events and facilities at the 7 June European election night will be given at 11.00h on 2 June in the Anna Politkovskaya Room (PHS 0A50).
Whit Monday Holiday. Please note that Parliament’s offices will be closed on Monday 1 June, which is a public holiday.
2009-06-02 Press conferences Today's press conferences 11:00 - Technical Briefing on the European Elections 11:00 12:00 Brussels, Anna Politkovskayaroom, PHS 00A050 2009-06-04 Delegations Pan-African Parliament Delegation to observe the European Elections A 8-strong delegation of MPs from the Pan-African Parliament will travel to observe the European elections in the UK (London) and in Germany (Wiesbaden). On June 7, they will take part in the EP Election night in Brussels.
On Election Day in the UK (Thursday 4 June), they will observe the elections in various polling stations. Ahead of the ballot, MPs will meet UK MEPs Claude Moraes (PES), Jean Lambert (Greens/EFA) and Robert Evans at the EP London office (Wednesday 3).
In Wiesbaden, MPs will meet federal, regional and municipal election officers, accompanied by local MEP Michael Gahler (EPP-ED), Chair of the ad-hoc delegation for relations with the Pan-African Parliament (Friday 5).
They will follow Mr Gahler campaigning (Saturday 6) and observe the election in polling stations (Sunday 7 morning). On the evening of the 7th, MPs will take part in the EP Election night.
The PAP delegation will be led by its President, Gertrude Mongella (Tanzania), and will be composed of Hon. M. T. Khumalo (Swaziland), Hon. Sunil Dowarkasins (Mauritius), Hon. Juliana Kantengwa (Rwanda). 09:00 19:00 London, Wiesbaden, Brussels Other events European Parliament election night - practical information for the media On the night the European election results are announced, Sunday 7 June, the European Parliament's Press Service will provide a full range of services to enable the media and the general public to follow the exit polls and official results as they come in.
Election results from the EU 27 Member States will be made available as and when they arrive after 22.00h on Sunday 7 June, when all polling stations will have closed. Later in the evening, Parliament's services will provide a preliminary prognosis of how the results will shape the composition of the new Parliament.
The EU-27 results will be shown on a giant screen in the plenary chamber and will be followed by TV links to press conferences with outgoing EP-President Hans-Gert Pöttering and political group leaders. All this will be broadcast by EbS and webstreamed live on EuroparlTV.
Election results will be available in due course on the website http://www.elections2009-results.eu/ which will be operational from Sunday 7 June from 18.00h.
The third floor of the PHS building, near the plenary chamber, will be turned into a giant "TV zone" for the use of media and information professionals, including a special platform around the VoxBox and areas with individual backdrops for political groups which will also be present on election night.
MEDIA FACILITIES
Parliament’s press room (PHS 0B01) will be open on Saturday from midday to midnight, on Sunday from midday to 03.00h on Monday, and on Monday morning from 8h. Workstations will be available to journalists in the press room on a first come, first served basis.
Press officers will be in the press room to provide information in 22 languages on exit polls and official results
A news desk in front of Plenary Chamber will also brief print and audiovisual media on latest news.

MAIN EVENTS
19.15h-21.30h: Three TV debates with prominent public figures
These debates on major issues facing Europe today will each be organised and moderated by journalists from a different pan-European public TV channel. They will be broadcast on these channels and on EbS, europarlTV and EPLive (22 EU official languages + Arabic + Russian).
Venue: VoxBox area, 3rd floor PHS building. Participants to be announced nearer the date.
· 19.15h-19.50h: The economic and financial crisis (France 24 and Radio France International);
· 20h-20.30h: Social aspects of the crisis. Relaunching the economy (Euronews);
· 20.40h-21.30h: Democracy and citizenship in the EU. Quo Vadis Europa? (Canal 24H/TVE).
From 22.00h: election results to be shown on giant screen in Plenary Chamber
Broadcast on EbS and europarlTV.
From 23.00h: Post-election press conferences on multiplex screen in Plenary Chamber
Outgoing President Pöttering and the EP's political group chairs, speaking from Brussels and national capitals, will comment on the first wave of election results. As the technology is interactive, journalists will be able to put questions to the MEPs. They will be broadcast on EbS, EP Live and europarlTV.
