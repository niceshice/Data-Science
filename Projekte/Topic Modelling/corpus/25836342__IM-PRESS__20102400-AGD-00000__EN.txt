The Week Ahead 7-13 June 2010
2010-06-04 - 10:14
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Energy: joint parliamentary meeting. EP President Jerzy Buzek and other MEPs are joined by national MPs from around the EU to discuss energy policy, and in particular the idea of a European Energy Community . Parliament’s scientific assessment panel, STOA, holds a related meeting with experts from the Energy Foresight Network (both events Monday-Tuesday).
Residence and work permits. A draft law to create a “one-stop-shop” system for third country nationals to be able to make one application both to reside and work in an EU country will be put to the vote in the Civil Liberties Committee (Thursday).
Language rights in criminal proceedings. The Civil Liberties Committee also votes on common standards to ensure that citizens involved in criminal trials have access to interpretation and translation facilities (Thursday).
EP President in Romania. Jerzy Buzek makes an official visit to Romania, including meetings with President Traian Băsescu, Prime Minister Emil Boc and parliamentary leaders (Wednesday).
President’s activities. Mr Buzek also meets Montenegro Prime Minister Milo Đukanović in Brussels (Thursday, with press point) and travels to Lisbon and Madrid for ceremonies marking 25 years of Portuguese and Spanish EU membership (Saturday).
MEPs in US for talks on economic crisis. MEPs from the committee on the economic and financial crisis will be in Washington DC for talks with the IMF, World Bank and members of the US Congress before meeting key financial players in New York (Monday-Friday).
Preparing for the plenary. The political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 14-17 June plenary session in Strasbourg. The draft agenda includes legislation on working time of lorry drivers and food labelling, plus a vote on intellectual property and a debate on plans for the 17 June EU summit.
Pre-session briefing. The regular briefing with political group spokespeople and the EP Press Service reviewing the agenda will take place on Friday at 11am (Anna Politkovskaya room, PHS 0A050).

The ten-strong delegation, led by committee chair Wolf Klinz (ALDE, DE), wants to learn about U.S. measures for dealing with the crisis, stimulating the real economy and boosting employment. It also aims are to see how far the two sides share the same outlook and compare different proposals for tackling the crisis.
The mission includes a series of high-level meetings. In Washington, the committee members will meet Congress and U.S. Administration policymakers as well as IMF, SEC and World Bank representatives. In New York they will discuss the crisis with industry representatives (Goldman Sachs, Deutsche Bank, SIFMA, hedge fund managers) and senior academics at Columbia University.
The committee was set up in October 2009 with a one-year term of office, which may be extended until 2011. Its mandate is to assess the extent and impact of the crisis and make practical recommendations. The recommendations will be included in a mid-term report to be put to the vote in the committee in July and in Parliament's plenary session in September 2010.
