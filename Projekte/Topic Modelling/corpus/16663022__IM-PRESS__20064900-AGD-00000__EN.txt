The Week Ahead 4-10 December 2006
2006-12-04 - 11:23
Political groups week - Brussels
Future of Europe. MEPs and national MPs will meet to debate different aspects of the future of Europe. The financing of the EU, European energy policy and the EU's role in conflict prevention are the main themes for this meeting, along with an evaluation of the period of reflection on the proposed Constitution. The meeting follows on from a similar gathering in May this year. At the end of the session, there will be a joint press conference with EP President Josep Borrell and the Speaker of the Finnish Parliament, Paavo Lipponen. (Monday and Tuesday)
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 11-14 December. Among the main items on the agenda are the second reading of the REACH legislation on chemicals, the vote of approval for the Romanian and Bulgarian Commissioners-designate, the presentation of the 2006 Sakharov Prize to Alexander Milinkevich, the adoption of the 2007 EU budget and the first reading vote on updating the 'Television without Frontiers' directive.
CIA/Portugal. A delegation from the temporary committee investigating illegal CIA activity in Europe will go to Portugal, where MEPs will meet the Foreign Minister, amongst others. (Tuesday)
Croatia. Josep Borrell will meet the Croatian President, Stipe Mesic. (Tuesday)
Kazakhstan. The President of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev, will also be meeting Mr Borrell. They will make a short statement to the press after their discussion. (Wednesday)
Pre-session briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda made by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the usual pre-session press briefing, at 11am on Friday 8 December.
2006-12-04 Other events 2nd Joint Parliamentary Meeting on the Future of Europe
"From reflection to action"
Organised jointly by the European Parliament and the Parliament of Finland
Co-chaired by Josep BORRELL, President of the European Parliament and Paavo LIPPONEN, Speaker of the Parliament of Finland.
Special guests: Matti VANHANEN, Prime Minister of Finland, President-in-Office of the Council and José Manuel BARROSO, President of the European Commission.
Members of the national parliaments and of the European Parliament will meet, for the second time this year, in Brussels on the EP's premises to discuss the 'Future of Europe' on 4 and 5 December 2006. This conference will give citizens' national and European representatives an opportunity to discuss the key issues which are of concern to them.
The first meeting, held on 8 and 9 May, helped to the re-launch, at national level, of the debate on the future of Europe. On this occasion, with a new title: "from reflection to action", the meeting is about to raise the level of the debate and address concrete questions.
The issues to be addressed during the meeting, in working parties and in plenary, will include: "the future financing of the Union", "towards a European energy policy" and "the European Union's role in conflict prevention".
This event, jointly organised by the European Parliament and the Finnish Parliament, will contribute to the discussions of the European Council meeting, due to be held on 14 and 15 December.

As part of its contribution to the debate on the future of Europe and the period of reflection following the 'no' votes on the constitution in France and the Netherlands, the European Parliament is organising a series of citizen's forums to enable members of the public directly to enter the debate on the main issues. This forum, in Den Bosch in the Netherlands, which has as its main theme "What has Europe done for me? And what can it do for me?, will focus on:
Work and income
Security
Infrastructure
Sustainable development and agriculture
Water, air and environment
How democratic is this Europe for me? The role of the European Parliament !
