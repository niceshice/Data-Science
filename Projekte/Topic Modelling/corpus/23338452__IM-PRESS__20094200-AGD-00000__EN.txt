The Week Ahead 12 - 18 October 2009
2009-10-09 - 10:39
Committee meetings and political groups week - Brussels
EU Enlargement Strategy. The day after the Commission adopts its annual strategy document on EU expansion, Commissioner Olli Rehn will discuss the issue with the Foreign Affairs Committee (Thursday).
EP/Ukraine. EP President Jerzy Buzek will meet Ukrainian President Viktor Yushchenko (Thursday). A press point will be held after the meeting.
Financial crisis. The new Special Committee on the Financial and Economic Crisis will meet to elect its chair and vice-chairs (Thursday).
EU/United Nations. The Foreign Affairs Committee will hear from Asha-Rose Migiro, Deputy UN Secretary General (Monday), while the Human Rights Subcommittee will meet the President of the UN Human Rights Council, Alex Van Meeuwen (Thursday). President Buzek will also be meeting Dr Migiro (Monday) and UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Navanethem Pillay (Wednesday).
President’s activities. Mr Buzek will also meet EU Foreign Policy High Representative Javier Solana (Wednesday) and will address both the European Innovation Summit (Tuesday) and the European Jewish Congress (Wednesday). Finally, Mr Buzek will make an official visit to Austria (Friday).
EP Prize for journalism. The winners of the European Parliament Prize for Journalism 2009 will receive their awards from President Buzek (Thursday).
Preparing for the plenary. The political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 19-22 October plenary session. The main items on the agenda include the 2010 EU budget and debates on climate change and the forthcoming EU summit. It is also planned to hold the first Question Hour with the Commission President, which is set to become a regular feature.
Pre-session briefing. The updated session agenda will be presented at the regular press briefing with the EP Press Service and political group spokespeople (Friday 11am Anna Politkovskaya room, PHS 0A050).

Workshop on "Anti-Personnel Landmines: achievements and practices a decade after the 1997 Mine Ban Treaty" - see Public Hearings 10:45 12:30 Brussels, ASP 5G3 Public hearings Workshop on Anti-Personnel Landmines Sub-committee on Security and Defence
The Sub-Committee on Security and Defence (SEDE) will hold a workshop on "Anti-Personnel Landmines: achievements and practices a decade after the 1997 Mine Ban Treaty". Guest speakers will include Mr Stephan HUSY, Director of the Geneva International Centre for Humanitarian Demining, Mr Lars-Erik WINGREN, Coordinator for Landmines in the Swedish Ministry for Foreign Affairs and Mr Lars-Gunnar WIGEMARK, Head of the Security Policy Unit of European Commission's DG RELEX.
10:45 12:30 Brussels, ASP 5G3 Programme of the workshop Other events Journalism Prize Award Ceremony President Jerzy BUZEK will present winners with the European Parliament Prize for Journalism. This second edition will again go to journalists who have covered major European issues or promoted a better understanding of the EU institutions and/or EU policies. The EU-wide winners in the categories for the written press, internet, radio and television each receive a trophy and € 5 000. The awards ceremony will be followed by a panel discussion on the theme "The role of new media: an opportunity for democratic participation?". Over 60 national winners from the Member States will attend the event, as well as 40 young journalists who are participating in a workshop hosted by the European Parliament.
