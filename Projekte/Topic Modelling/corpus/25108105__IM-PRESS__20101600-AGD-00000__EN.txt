The Week Ahead 12-16 April 2010
2010-04-09 - 11:03
Committee meetings and political groups week – Brussels
Economic Crisis. The Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs will hold a public hearing on the fiscal crisis in the European Union and the lessons to be learned from Greece with regard to economic surveillance, statistics and off-balance sheet operations. (Wednesday)
EP President’s activities. Jerzy Buzek will undertake an official visit to Romania. The visit will include meetings with President Traian Basescu, Prime Minister Emil Boc, as well as the Presidents of the Chamber of Deputies and the Senate (Monday) . The President will have an audience with Pope Benedict XVI in the Vatican City (Friday) . In Brussels, he will meet with Albanian Prime Minister Sali Berisha (Thursday, followed by a press point) .
Plenary preparations. Parliament’s political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the 19-22 April plenary session in Strasbourg. The agenda includes the budget discharge, with Parliament voting on whether to approve the EU institutions’ financial management in 2008, and a vote on airport security charges. MEPs will also debate the Commission's legislative and work programme for 2010 and the coordination of humanitarian aid in Haiti. There will also be votes on the EU's animal welfare action plan as well as on the Europeana digital library project.
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary session's agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be presented during the regular press briefing at 11am on Friday. Parliament’s press service and the spokespeople of the main political groups will be on hand to comment on the session ahead (PHS 0A050 Anna Politkovskaya room) .

The committee is organising its second public hearing on Greece this Wednesday. The main themes which will be addressed will be those of statistics reporting, Eurozone surveillance, off-balance sheet operations and sovereign debt.
MEPs will question a number of actors closely linked with the situation in Greece.
