The Week Ahead 29 March – 4 April 2010
2010-03-25 - 17:13
External Parliamentary Activities Week
ACP-EU Assembly. The 19th plenary session of the Joint Parliamentary Assembly which brings together 78 MEPs with 78 parliamentarians from Africa, the Caribbean and the Pacific takes place in Tenerife, with migration policy, the trade in bananas, climate change and the economic crisis all on the agenda (Monday-Thursday).
External activities. This week is set aside for MEPs to work in their constituencies or to take part in parliamentary delegations. As well as the ACP-EU assembly, the week’s activities include interparliamentary meetings with Croatia and Georgia, and a visit by a delegation from the special committee on the economic crisis to Latvia.
There are no official meetings of Parliamentary bodies scheduled in Brussels this week. Please note that Parliament’s offices will be closed for the Easter holiday from Thursday to Monday 5 April inclusive.
2010-03-29 Press conferences 11.00 - Support for the peace process in the Basque Country - Frieda BREPOELS (Greens/EFA, BE) 11:00 11:30 Brussels, Room PHS 0A50, Anna Politkovskaya Delegations ACP-EU Assembly: EU- Latin America Bananas agreement and migration will top the agenda The EU- Latin America Bananas Agreement and migration will take centre stage at the 19th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly (JPA) in Tenerife ( Spain) from 27 March to 1 April 2010.
Delegates will also debate Haiti's reconstruction and the consolidation of the peace process in Southern Sudan ahead of the general elections of mid-April.
The formal opening on Monday 29 March at 11.00 am will begin with speeches by the co-presidents, Louis Michel and Charles Milupi. Paulino Rivero, President of the Regional Government of the Canary Islands, will also address the Assembly.
Following the opening ceremony, the co-presidents will hold a press conference around 12.15 pm (local time).
The ACP-EU JPA twice a year brings together 78 MEPs and 78 parliamentarians from the African, Caribbean and Pacific (ACP) states.
Debate on migration and the EU- Latin America Bananas Agreement
The JPA will debate the banana issue following the EU-Latin America agreement signed 15 December 2009 by which the EU will cut its import tariffs (customs duties) for products coming from these countries. Most of the banana-producing organisations of the ACP countries (chiefly the African and Caribbean states) fear their production will plummet following the December 2009 accord.
The Canary Islands (Spain) are a major banana-producing region in the EU, with over 350,000 tonnes grown in 2009.
MEPs and ACP delegates will debate the migration issue in Tenerife and are scheduled to visit a retention centre for minors.
The assembly will also debate and vote on the following reports:
- The financial and economic impact of climate change in ACP countries - Buti Manamela (South Africa) and Frank Engel (EPP, LU)
- The social impact of the global crisis - Gibson Hlophe (Swaziland) and Juan Fernando Lopez Aguilar (S&D, ES)
The votes on the resolutions and reports will take place on Thursday 1 April. 08:00 19:00 Tenerife (Spain) Draft agenda (revised) and Work Programme Croatia: MEPs to discuss the country's compliance with enlargement benchmarks MEPs will discuss with their counterparts from the Sabor (Croatian Parliament) the latest developments regarding Croatia's compliance with enlargement benchmarks during the 11th EU-Croatia Joint Parliamentary Committee (29 to 30 March). Gunnar Hökmark will chair Parliament's delegation.
During their stay, MEPs will meet the Croatian President, the Prime Minister, the Minister of Justice and the Minister of Foreign Affairs and European Integration as well as the Chief Negotiator on behalf of the Government of the Republic of Croatia.
The delegation will be composed of Gunnar HÖKMARK (EPP, SE), Lena EK (ALDE, SE), Tanja FAJON (S&D, SL), Andrew BRONS (NA, UK), Antonio CANCIAN (EPP, IT), Nadja HIRSCH (ALDE, DE), Petra KAMMEREVERT (S&D, DE), Bernd POSSELT (EPP, DE), Pal SCHMITT (EPP, HU), Luigi BERLINGUER (S&D, IT) and Silvana KOCH-MEHRIN (ALDE, DE). 09:00 19:00 Zagreb (Croatia) Website of the Delegation to the European Union-Croatia Joint Parliamentary Committee EP delegation to visit Georgia ahead of local elections on 30 May MEPs will discuss with their Georgian counterparts the political, economic and social situation in the country as it prepares to hold local elections on 30 May, when they visit Georgia next week.
The 12th meeting of the EU-Georgia Parliamentary Cooperation Committee on 29 and 30 March will also discuss the Georgian government's strategy towards the separatist regions of Abkhazia and South Ossetia.
MEPs will meet the President of Georgia (to be confirmed), the Deputy Speaker of Parliament, the Minister for European and Euro-Atlantic Integration, the Minister for Georgian Reintegration, the head of the Central Electoral Commission and the deputy head of the EU Monitoring Mission based in Tbilisi. The EP delegation will also visit a refugee camp in the Gori region.
Parliament's delegation will consist of the following MEPs: Milan CABRNOCH (ECR, CZ), Zoran THALER (S&D, SL), Jan BŘEZINA (EPP, CZ) and Ulrike LUNACEK (Greens/EFA, AT). 09:00 19:00 Tbilisi Website of the Delegation to Armenia, Azerbaijan and Georgia Economic Crisis Committee assesses situation in Latvia A delegation of MEPs from the Special Committee on the Financial, Economic and Social Crisis will be in Latvia to assess how the country is dealing with the impact of the credit crunch and the global economic downturn. The delegation, led by the committee's chair, Wolf Klinz (ALDE, DE), will have meetings with officials at the Bank of Latvia, Members of the national parliament, the Minister of Finance, Einars Repše, representatives of Latvian trades unions, businesses and banks.
The visit will conclude with a meeting with Prime Minister Valdis Dombrovskis followed by a press conference (Wednesday 0945).
As well as Mr Klinz, the following MEPs are part of the delgation: Regina Bastos (EPP, PT), Theodor Stolojan (EPP, RO), Pervenche Berès (S&D, FR, the rapporteur for the committee) and Sven Giegold (Greens/EFA, DE), joined by two Latvian MEPs, Roberts Zīle (ECR) and Arturs Krišjānis Kariņš (EPP). 13:00 20:00 Riga (Latvia) 2010-03-30 Delegations ACP-EU Assembly: EU- Latin America Bananas agreement and migration will top the agenda The EU- Latin America Bananas Agreement and migration will take centre stage at the 19th session of the ACP-EU Joint Parliamentary Assembly (JPA) in Tenerife ( Spain) from 27 March to 1 April 2010.
