The Week Ahead 24-30 May 2010
2010-05-21 - 12:58
External Parliamentary Activities
EP President visits China. Jerzy Buzek will make an official visit to the People's Republic of China, including meetings with Premier Wen Jiabao, Vice-President Xi Jinping, and Foreign Minister Yang Jiechi. He will also give a speech at Nanjing University and open a conference organised by the European Parliament at the Shanghai EXPO EU pavilion. A group from the Special Committee on the Economic and Financial Crisis will also be in China.
Israel/Palestinian territories. A special delegation will travel to the Middle East to assess the humanitarian situation, the peace process and the use of EU funds in the Palestinian territories. The MEPs will meet Israeli and Palestinian political leaders, NGOs and international bodies. Part of the delegation will travel to Gaza via Egypt.
Georgia: local elections. MEPs will be in Tbilisi and elsewhere in Georgia to observe the local elections planned for 30 May.
Syria. An EP delegation will be in Syria to discuss the country's association agreement with the EU with President Bashir el Assad, Deputy Prime Minister Abdulla Dadari and parliamentary speaker Mahmoud al-Abrash.
External activities week. This week is set aside for MEPs to take part in visits of parliamentary delegations or to work in their constituencies. As well as the working visits mentioned above, an EP delegation is set to meet President Lula in Brazil, and in Argentina MEPs will meet President Kirchner. A delegation from the Civil Liberties Committee will go to Mali to discuss immigration management with the authorities.
There are no official meetings of parliamentary bodies in Brussels.
2010-05-24 Delegations Mercosur: relaunch of negotiations and elections in Brazil A six-strong delegation of MEPs of the EP delegation for relations with the Mercosur countries is due to meet the presidents of Brazil and Argentina to discuss the relaunch of negotiations with Mercosur. During their official visit to Brasilia and Buenos Aires from 24 to 28 May, the MEPs are also expected to meet the main candidates for Brazil's presidential election, which takes place on 3 October this year.
The EP delegation is set to meet Brazil's President Lula and the main candidates for the presidential elections. MEPs are also due to meet the Speaker of the Brazilian Senate, José Sarney, and the Speaker of the Chamber of Deputies, Michel Temer. In Argentina they will mee President Kirchner as well as the Speaker of the Senate, Julio Cobos and the chair of the foreign affairs committee of the Chamber of Deputies, Alfredo Atanasof.
The delegation will be led by Luis Yanez-Barnuevo (S&D, ES). The other members are Edite Estrela (S&D, PT), Ilda Figueiredo (GUE/NGL, PT), Nathalie Griesbeck (ALDE, FR), Iliana Ivanova (EPP, BG) and Vital Moreira (S&D, PT). 09:00 19:00 Brazil and Argentina link to the delegation MERCOSUR Nepal: peace process and climate change Parliament's delegation for relations with the countries of South Asia will travel to Kathmandu to participate in the EP-Nepal interparliamentary meeting from 24 to 29 May. The delegation will focus on the effects of climate change and on the peace process in Nepal.
MEPs will meet President Ram Baran Yadad, Prime Minister Dadhav Kumar, and representatives of the political parties, civil society organisations, and the UN mission. MEPs will also visit a camp of tibetan refugees and meet representatives of Bhutanese refugees.
The delegation is composed of Jean Lambert (Greens/EFA, UK) chair, John ATTARD-MONTALTO (S&D, MT), Thomas MANN (EPP, DE), Lidia Joanna GERINGER DE OEDENBERG (S&D, PL) Niccolò RINALDI (ALDE, IT) and Slavi BINEV (NI, BG). 09:00 19:00 Kathmandu, Nepal link to the delegation for relations with the countries of South Asia EP mission to Israel and the Palestinian Territories A 15-strong delegation of MEPs will visit Israel and the Palestinian Territories from 24 to 27 May. The mission will focus on the humanitarian situation, the peace process and the use of EU funds in the Palestinian Territories. MEPs have deplored the Israeli authorities' refusal to grant access to Gaza.
The joint delegation of MEPs from the foreign affairs, development and budgets committees, together with the chairs of the working group on the Middle East and of the interparliamentary delegation for relations with the Palestinian Legislative Council, will travel to the Middle East to meet Israeli and Palestinian politicians, NGOs and representatives of international organizations.

The MEPs will hold talks with Syrian president Bashir el Assad, deputy prime minister Abdullah Dardari and the speaker of the Assembly Mahmoud al-Abrash. The talks will focus on negotiations for an association agreement, which was signed in 2004 but is still being finalised. An Interparliamentary meeting will be held with members of the Syrian Parliament's foreign affairs committee. The MEPs will visit one of the projects managed by UNRWA - the United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees - aimed at tackling exclusion among young Palestinians.
The delegation will be chaired by Mario David (EPP, PT). The other members will be Marco Scurria (EPP, IT), Saïd El Khadraoui (S&D, BE), Antonyia Parvanova (ALDE, BG) and Sajjad Karim (ECR, UK).
