The European Parliament's Transport Committee visits the West of Ireland A delegation from the European Parliament's Transport and Tourism Committee has spent three days in the West of Ireland examining ways to strengthen transport and tourism infrastructures in the region. They focused on questions such as the future development of Shannon airport, the development of the Western Rail Corridor and the balance between objectives in the agricultural, environmental and tourism sectors.
The delegation also looked at best practices in the promotion of the region's natural and cultural heritage. The delegation included MEPs from the UK, Germany, Romania, and Hungary, together with Irish MEPs Seán Ó Neachtain (Fianna Fáil) and Jim Higgins (Fine Gael).
Speaking at the conclusion of the visit, the head of the delegation, British MEP Brian Simpson said that the visit had been "useful" and that the delegation felt that the Western Rail Corridor was "an essential project for the West of Ireland, along with the ongoing development of Shannon Airport."
German MEP Renate Sommer , who also participated in the delegation, said that it had been an interesting visit and the "we need to be ready to make further progress on sustainable infrastructure in transport and tourism."
Seán Ó Neachtain MEP (Fianna Fáil), who organised parts of the visit said that he was delighted that the delegation could "see for themselves some of the problems that we face on the ground and some of the ways in which the EU might be able to contribute in the future."
