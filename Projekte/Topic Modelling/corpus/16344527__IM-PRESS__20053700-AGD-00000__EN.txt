The Week Ahead 5 - 11 September 2005
2005-09-09 - 17:10
Strasbourg plenary session
Debates on topical issues . At the start of Monday's sitting, Parliament's President Josep Borrell will ask the House to observe a minute's silence for the victims of the many tragic events that have taken place around the world this summer. MEPs will then hear a Commission statement on aid for the regions affected by the forest fires in Portugal and Spain and the floods in Austria and Germany. Later there will be a debate on air safety, following the series of aircraft accidents in August.
Terrorism. On Wednesday morning the House holds a debate with UK Home Secretary Charles Clarke and Commissioner Franco Frattini on ways to combat terrorism more effectively without undermining civil liberties.
Health. Health issues are high on this week's legislative agenda, with MEPs voting at second reading on rules to protect workers against optical radiation (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) and at first reading on the manufacture of special medicines for children (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday) .
Environment. A directive on the management and storage of heavily polluting forms of mining waste comes up for its second reading (debate Monday, vote Tuesday).
Internet. The protection of young people surfing the internet is the subject of a first reading vote (debate Tuesday) .
Textiles. In response to the flood of Chinese textile exports into Europe since the start of this year, MEPs will vote on proposed EU measures to support the European textile industry (Tuesday) .
