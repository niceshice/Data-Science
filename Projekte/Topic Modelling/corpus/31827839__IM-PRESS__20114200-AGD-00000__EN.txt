The Week Ahead 10-16 October 2011
2011-10-07 - 10:45
Plenary Session and Committee Meetings – Brussels
Brussels session
Schengen. MEPs debate the decision by two Member States to block the accession of Bulgaria and Romania to the border-free Schengen area (Debate on Wednesday, vote on resolution on Thursday).
European Council. Preparations for the next European Summit, from 17 to 18 October, on economic policy, the G20 and climate change, will be debated by Parliament with the Council and Commission (Wednesday).
Yemen and Bahrain. The latest developments in Yemen and Bahrain are the subject of a debate with the Council (Wednesday).
Trafficking in firearms. MEPs vote on the implementation of the United Nations Firearms Protocol which seeks to combat the illegal production and sale of firearms (Thursday).
Committees and other business
Reform of farm policy. The Agriculture Committee will discuss with Commissioner Ciolos his proposals to make the CAP more competitive and greener, with secure supplies of quality foodstuffs for EU citizens and a fairer distribution of EU funds amongst farmers (Wednesday).
European Central Bank. The Economics Committee will question Jörg Asmussen, candidate for the vacant post on the ECB Executive Board, at a public hearing (Monday).
Committee Chairs and Commissioners. Parliament's committee chairs meet all the European Commissioners jointly to discuss the Commission's work programme for the coming year, with the focus for 2012 on tackling the economic crisis and boosting growth and employment (Wednesday).
Parlamentarium. The new European Parliament Visitors' centre will be opened officially on 11 October by President Buzek, in the presence of Her Royal Highness, Princess Astrid of Belgium (Tuesday). On 14 October, the Centre opens to the public (Friday). Using innovative multimedia tools the centre aims to show how decisions are made and how they influence their daily lives.
President's activities. President Buzek opens the 9th European Week of Regions and Cities (Open Days), bringing together around 6,000 participants from all Europe's regions (Monday). Also, the President will receive Andris Bērziņš, President of Latvia (Wednesday) , Reuven Rivlin, Speaker of the Israeli Knesset (Monday) and Mircea Geoană, President of the Romanian Senate (Tuesday).

Official inauguration of the Parlamentarium
President BUZEK will inaugurate the Parlamentarium and unveil its inauguration plaque on Tuesday, 11 October at 17.00. Her Royal Highness Princess ASTRID, Commission Vice- President Viviane REDING and Karl-Heinz LAMBERTZ, Minister-President of the Government of the German-speaking Community will be present.
14 October, 10.00 to 18.00
Opening to the public of the Parlamentarium
On Friday, 14 October at 10.00, the centre will open to the public. President BUZEK together with Brussels Region Minister-President Charles PICQUÉ and the mayors of the three neighbouring communes will be cutting the ribbon.
