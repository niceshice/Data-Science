The Week Ahead 7 – 13 November 2011
2011-11-04 - 12:56
Committee meetings and political groups' week
EU budget 2012. The Parliament-Council conciliation procedure continues, with the first meeting of the EU Budget Conciliation Committee to find an agreement on EU spending for next year before 21 November. The meeting will opened by President Jerzy Buzek (Tue p.m.).
Future of EU agricultural policy. For the first time, Agriculture Committee MEPs will meet their co-decision counterparts, the farm ministers, and Commissioner Dacian Cioloş, to discuss the proposed reform package (Mon). There will also be a trialogue on dairy sector reform (Thu).
Financial support for redundant workers. The Budgets Committee will vote to mobilise European Globalisation Adjustment Fund aid for redundant workers in Ireland, Austria and Greece (Mon).
Economic governance. The Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee will hold a public hearing on the recently-agreed "European Semester" for economic policy coordination ( Mon).
Climate Change. The Environment Committee will welcome Climate Commissioner Connie Hedegaard, to discuss preparations for the UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (COP17) in South Africa, in December (Mon). Ms Hedegaard will also meet the Transport Committee, to discuss the forthcoming inclusion of aviation in the Emissions Trading System (Thu).
Afghanistan. The Budgetary Control Committee will vote on a report on EU financial assistance to Afghanistan.
President's diary. Jerzy Buzek meets European Council President Herman Van Rompuy on Tuesday. On Wednesday, in Brussels, he meets Yiannakis Omirou, Speaker of the Cyprus House of Representatives and the College of Commissioners.
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament’s political groups will prepare the 14-17 November plenary session in Strasbourg. There will be debates on Economic governance, the December EU Council and the Commission work programme for 2012, plus votes on legislation to increase transparency for short selling and banning certain trades in credit default swaps, new inquiry powers for Parliament, plans for a EU Platform against poverty and the award of the LUX film Prize 2011.
Pre-session press briefing. The regular press briefing by the EP Press Service and political group spokespeople is on Friday at 11.00h (Anna Politkovskaya room PHS 0A050).
