The Week Ahead - 23-29 June 2008
2008-06-20 - 11:55
Extraordinary plenary session and committee meetings
Debate on EU summit. At a special plenary session, MEPs will assess the outcome of the European Council, particularly its conclusions on the Lisbon Treaty. Following the debate EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering will hold a press conference with Council President Janez Jansa (Tuesday).
French presidency. President Pöttering and EP political group leaders go to Paris to meet French President Nicolas Sarkozy, members of the government and the speakers of the Senate and National Assembly. Several French ministers will present their work programmes before the EP's committees in Brussels (Wednesday, Thursday) .
ECB President / EMU. With inflation rising and an interest rate hike possible, Jean-Claude Trichet will appear before the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee for the quarterly monetary dialogue (Wednesday) . In addition, MEPs and a panel of high-level experts will review the outcome of ten years of monetary union in Europe (Tuesday) .
Legal immigration. Following Parliament's vote on the "return" directive, the focus switches, at a hearing of the Civil Liberties Committee, to legal immigration and the blue card (Wednesday) .
Animal cloning. The Agriculture Committee will vote on a draft resolution, to be submitted to the July plenary, calling for a ban on the cloning of animals for food (Wednesday) .
Climate change. The best ways of achieving swift reductions in CO2 emissions will be discussed at the eighth thematic session of the Temporary Committee on Climate Change (Monday) . Elsewhere, the Environment Committee will discuss three reports: on emissions trading, carbon storage and reducing greenhouse gases (Monday-Wednesday) .
Enlargement/Montenegro. The Foreign Affairs Committee adopts its annual report on enlargement (Tuesday) and also receives Milo Djukanovic, prime minister of Montenegro (Monday) .
Toy safety. The Internal Market Committee holds a hearing with representatives of manufacturers, distributors and consumers (Monday) .

Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection
This public hearing is part of a series of consultations and debates intended to help MEPs contribute effectively to the revision of the directive on the safety of toys. The aim is to find the right balance between protecting the safety of consumers and not damaging the competitiveness of European companies. The hearing will also look at the best ways of detecting hazardous toys, from certification to testing. Representatives of consumers, manufacturers, distributors and inspection bodies will try to deal with these issues. The Internal Market Committee will vote in November on a report by Marianne THYSSEN (EPP-ED, BE) on toy safety. Committee on Budgets
Once a year the members of the EP Budgets Committee have a meeting with the chairs of the Budgetary Committees of EU national parliaments. There are two aims: to swap ideas on the EU budget and to share experiences on budgetary matters. Committee on Development
The role of Parliaments in aid effectiveness and the involvement of new Member States of the EU and Development Cooperation will feature high on the agenda of this joint meeting with the national parliaments. Josep Borrell (PSE, ES), Chair of the Committee on Development, European Parliament, and Jožef Jerovšek, Chair of the Committee on Foreign Policy, National Assembly of the Republic of Slovenia will moderate the meeting.
Alain Hutchinson (PES, BE) and Frithjof Schmidt (Greens/EFA, DE) will deliver speeches on the Role of Parliaments in the Aid effectiveness agenda (Accra High Level Forum) in the context of policy coherence for development.
The situation of the new Member States and EU Development Cooperation will be dealt with by Danutė Budreikaitė (ALDE, LT) and Filip Kaczmarek (EPP-ED, PL). Many new member states have evolved from a recipient to a donor situation. The development policy being part of the "acquis communutaire", new member states have to contribute to the development of the EU. Joint meeting of the Committee on Budgets and the Committee on Budgetary Control
The Budgetary Control Committee and Budgets Committee meet to pave the way for the next revision of the financial regulation. The financial regulation, which is reviewed every three years, is the "Bible" governing Community expenditure on both internal and external policies. The meeting will examine the functioning of the financial regulation and hear the views of the various parties concerned.
