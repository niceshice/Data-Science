The Week Ahead 29 August - 4 September 2005
2005-09-09 - 13:20
Parliamentary committee meetings resume this week in Brussels following the summer recess.
At the Development Committee on Monday several ministers from Togo, including the foreign minister, will discuss with MEPs the human rights situation following the disputed polls and political violence in the country.
On Tuesday morning the Croatian foreign minister reports to the Foreign Affairs Committee on her country's progress in meeting the EU accession criteria. The same day sees votes on European cooperation to guarantee standards in higher education (Culture Committee), trade relations with China (International Trade Committee) and air services agreements with Croatia and Bulgaria (Transport Committee).
Parliament's President Josep Borrell takes part in a conference titled Constituting Europe being held in Santander, Spain.
The rest of the week in Brussels is devoted to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 5 to 8 September, at which health and safety issues will feature prominently. MEPs will look at medicinal products for children, exposure to optical radiation at the workplace and serious but neglected diseases in developing countries. Also on the agenda will be the management of waste from the mining industries, the protection of children from the objectionable aspects of the internet and refugees' rights.
Any changes to the plenary agenda decided at next Thursday's meeting of the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the Friday press briefing, which takes place at 11am.
