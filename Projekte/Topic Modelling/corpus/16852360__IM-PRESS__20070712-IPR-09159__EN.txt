The Week Ahead 16 to 22 July 2007
Institutions
2007-07-13 - 09:15
Committee week - Brussels
IGC. Following Parliament's adoption on 11 July of its opinion on the convening of the intergovernmental conference, the Constitutional Affairs Committee resumes the EP's preparatory work on the IGC by holding meetings with Portugal's foreign minister, Luis Amado, and the former president of the Convention, Valéry Giscard d'Estaing (Tuesday, with press conference).
CIA. At a joint meeting of the Civil Liberties Committee, the Foreign Affairs Committee and the Human Rights Subcommittee, MEPs will exchange views with Dick Marty, author of the Council of Europe's report on secret detentions and illegal transfers of detainees (Tuesday, with press conference).
Energy. The Foreign Affairs Committee will adopt a report on the EU's common foreign policy in the field of energy. The draft report calls for the Commission to devise a roadmap on this matter, given the growing importance of energy issues (Tuesday).
Portuguese presidency. Representatives of Portugal's presidency of the EU will explain their policy priorities for the second half of 2007 to Parliament's committees (Monday, Tuesday).
Milk. Three reports on different aspects of dairy farming in the EU will be put to the vote at the Agriculture Committee (Tuesday) in response to proposals to reform this sector.
Intellectual property and the WTO. The International Trade Committee will vote on the granting of Parliament's assent to the WTO protocol amending the Agreement on Trade Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights (TRIPS) (Tuesday). The aim of the protocol is to facilitate access to medicines by poor countries. In a resolution adopted on 12 July, the EP called for the protocol to be backed up with additional measures.
EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering , visits Paris on Saturday, at the invitation of French President Nicolas Sarkozy to take part in the 14 July national day festivities.
Parliament's activities resume on 27 August.
