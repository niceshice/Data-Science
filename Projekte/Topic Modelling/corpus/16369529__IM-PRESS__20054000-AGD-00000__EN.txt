The Week Ahead 26 September - 2 October 2005
2005-09-29 - 12:11
Strasbourg Plenary Session
Parliament's autumn agenda. EP President Josep Borrell will make a statement on Monday on Parliament's work priorities, in which he will review the past year and look at current issues such as the future of Europe, the financial perspective and the Commission's plans on the legislative front. Parliament's political group leaders will then state their views. Mr Borrell holds a press conference on the autumn political agenda on Tuesday and the Conference of Presidents will discuss the same issues on Wednesday with Commission President José Manuel Barroso.
Bulgarian and Romanian observers. At the start of Monday's sitting Mr Borrell will welcome the parliamentary observers from Romania and Bulgaria, who have been invited to follow the European Parliament's work until their countries join the EU.
Turkey. A few days before the start of the accession negotiations, MEPs will make known their views on EU-Turkey relations (debate and vote on Wednesday morning).
Railways. The House examines the rail package, whose aims include promoting rail freight and injecting more competition into the provision of passenger services (debate Tuesday, vote Wednesday).
Immigration and anti-terrorism measures. MEPs vote on EU rules on the granting of refugee status and on measures on the retention of telecommunications data (Tuesday).
External relations. Debates are scheduled on the price of oil, the outcome of the World Summit and Belarus (Wednesday).
Human rights. Outside the plenary, the Foreign Affairs Committee selects a shortlist of three candidates for the 2005 Sakharov Prize (Monday).
