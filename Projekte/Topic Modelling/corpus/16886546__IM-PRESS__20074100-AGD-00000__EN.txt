The Week Ahead 8-14 October 2007
2007-10-05 - 09:54
Plenary session and committee meetings – Brussels
EP seats. MEPs will debate and vote in plenary the future distribution of seats between Member States in the 750-member Parliament proposed by the EU Reform Treaty (debate Wednesday, vote Thursday).
Also at the Brussels plenary: MEPs will debate preparations for the Lisbon Summit (Wednesday) and the situation in Gaza (Wednesday, vote Thursday). The agenda includes a report on the 'open skies' agreement with the USA (debate and vote Thursday). President Hans-Gert Pöttering will open the session with a statement marking the World Day against the death penalty . (Wednesday)
Budget 2008 . The Budgets Committee continues its vote on the 2008 EU budget, with Foreign and Security Policy spending under the microscope (Monday).
Protecting air and soil. Rules aimed at improving air quality will get a second reading vote in the Environment Committee, while legislation to protect soil from pollution comes to its first reading (Tuesday).
ECB. Jean-Claude Trichet, President of the ECB, appears before the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee for his regular ‘monetary dialogue’ with Parliament (Tuesday).
TRIPS. The Trade Committee will vote on whether to approve the 2005 Agreement on Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights (Tuesday).
Bulgarian medical staff. The Bulgarian nurses and Palestinian doctor recently freed from custody in Libya will visit Parliament at the invitation of President Pöttering (Wednesday).
Foreign Affairs: Armenia, Afghanistan, UN. President Pöttering has meetings with the President of Armenia, Robert Kocharian, and the UN General Assembly President, Srgjan Kerim (Thursday), while the Foreign Affairs Committee meets Armenian Foreign Minister Vartan Oskanian and the EU special representative for Afghanistan, Francesc Vendrell (Tuesday).
Sakharov Prize. A round table event with MEPs and experts will highlight the achievements of the three finalists for Parliament’s 2007 Prize for Freedom of Thought (Wednesday).

The aim of this public hearing on "The integration of economic migrant workers", organised by the Employment and Social Affairs Committee, is to discuss whether immigration is an answer to demographic change in Europe and the decline of working-age population in various Member States. MEPs and experts will discuss among other things if the Australian and Canadian model, which sets immigration quota per economic sector, is a suitable solution for the European Union and how third-country workers can be successfully integrated into European societies. The hearing shall also facilitate the EP's current work on the Commission's proposal for a directive on "sanctions against employers of illegally staying third-country nationals". 16:00 18:30 Brussels, PHS 5B1 Programme of the event Other events EP Citizen's Forum on the Future of Europe As part of its contribution to the debate on the future of Europe the European Parliament is organising a series of citizen's forums to enable members of the public directly to enter the debate on the main issues.
