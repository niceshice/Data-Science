Caucasus need to reform, cooperate: EP President Buzek
External relations
2011-05-24 - 08:35
EP President Buzek in front of the memorial for the victims of the Armenian genocide During a three-day visit to the South Caucasus, EP President Jerzy Buzek said advancing democracy, embracing human rights, fostering the rule of law, strengthening stability and increasing prosperity are key to the partnership between the EU and its Eastern partners.
Mr Buzek underlined EP support for Georgian independence, visited the memorial dedicated to the victims of the Armenian genocide and paid tribute to the victims of the Soviet invasion in Azerbaijan during his visit to the Republic of Armenia, Georgia and the Republic Azerbaijan from 18 to 21 May.
On Wednesday he met Armenian President Serzh Sargsyan in Yerevan, where "I called on the Armenian authorities to speed up reforms," Mr Buzek said: He focused "particularly on the importance of enhanced media pluralism".
During his visit to Tbilisi in Georgia on Thursday, he said, "to deepen our partnership, further improvements are necessary in respect of human rights, media pluralism, independence of judiciary, rule of law, cooperation with the opposition".
"The EU supports the independence, sovereignty and territorial integrity of Georgia," Mr Buzek said. "I deeply regret the continued presence of Russian troops in the breakaway regions...The European Parliament has rejected the unilateral declaration of independence of South Ossetia and Abkhazia."
In Azerbaijan on Friday, Mr Buzek said, "We would like to strengthen cooperation in economy, trade and energy. Cooperation in the field of energy is of crucial importance to both partners".
