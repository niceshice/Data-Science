2006-12-01 - 10:59
Political groups week - Brussels
Future of Europe. MEPs and national MPs will meet to debate different aspects of the future of Europe. The financing of the EU, European energy policy and the EU's role in conflict prevention are the main themes for this meeting, along with an evaluation of the period of reflection on the proposed Constitution. The meeting follows on from a similar gathering in May this year. At the end of the session, there will be a joint press conference with EP President Josep Borrell and the Speaker of the Finnish Parliament, Paavo Lipponen. (Monday and Tuesday) Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary session of 11-14 December. Among the main items on the agenda are the second reading of the REACH legislation on chemicals, the vote of approval for the Romanian and Bulgarian Commissioners-designate, the presentation of the 2006 Sakharov Prize to Alexander Milinkevich, the adoption of the 2007 EU budget and the first reading vote on updating the 'Television without Frontiers' directive. CIA/Portugal . A delegation from the temporary committee investigating illegal CIA activity in Europe will go to Portugal, where MEPs will meet the Foreign Minister, amongst others. (Tuesday) Croatia. Josep Borrell will meet the Croatian President, Stipe Mesic. (Tuesday) Kazakhstan . The President of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev, will also be meeting Mr Borrell. They will make a short statement to the press after their discussion. (Wednesday) Pre-session briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda made by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the usual pre-session press briefing, at 11am on Friday 8 December.
