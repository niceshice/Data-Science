The Week Ahead: 3 to 9 November 2008
Institutions
2008-10-31 - 11:51
Parliamentary committee meetings - Brussels
Arab week. The Arab world will be the focus of a series of meetings and events organised by the EP as part of the year of intercultural dialogue. The Arab week will be inaugurated by EP President Hans-Gert Pöttering, who will receive inter alia Secretary-General of the League of Arab States Amr Moussa (Tuesday, press point at 18.00h) . Among other guests, Iraqi MPs and Iraqi government spokesman Ali Dabbagh will be received by the delegation for relations with Iraq ( Wednesday) . Hans-Gert Pöttering. The EP President will be received in the Hague by Queen Beatrix and Dutch Prime Minister Jan-Peter Balkenende (Tuesday). Financial crisis. Eurogroup chair Jean-Claude Juncker will give the Economic Affairs Committee his assessment of the financial crisis and its impact on the real economy (Tuesday). Working time. The Employment Committee takes its second-reading vote on the proposed revision of the EU Directive (Wednesday). Immigration. The Civil Liberties Committee will vote on draft directives providing for sanctions against employers of illegally staying third-country nationals and introducing a European "blue card" for highly-qualified workers from third countries (Tuesday). Pesticides. The Environment Committee takes its second-reading vote on the framework directive on the sustainable use of pesticides and new marketing authorisation rules for plant protection products (Wednesday). Toy safety. The Internal Market Committee takes its first-reading vote on a draft directive designed to upgrade protection for children (Thursday). Mobile roaming. The Industry Committee will hold a first exchange of views on a proposal to extend the "Roaming" regulation to SMS and other mobile data transmission services (Tuesday). Enlargement. Commission Rehn will present the Commission's annual reports on accession candidate countries (Wednesday).
