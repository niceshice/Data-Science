The Week Ahead 16-22 April 2007
2007-04-13 - 10:42
Political groups week - Brussels
Rights of the child. Seven EP committees are working together to hold a special public hearing on an EU strategy on the rights of the child. MEPs and experts from NGOs will be joined by children in the plenary chamber in Brussels to debate a range of issues from violence against children to child poverty (Tuesday).
Energy policy and candidate countries. MEPs will join national MPs from Croatia, Turkey and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia to debate energy policy (Monday and Tuesday).
Hans-Gert Pöttering in Bulgaria. The EP President will make an official visit to Bulgaria to mark the start of the European election campaign there. He will meet President Georgi Parvanov, Prime Minister Sergey Stanishev and the Parliamentary Speaker, Georgi Pirinski, among others, as well as addressing the Bulgarian Parliament. (Thursday and Friday)
EP/United States. A delegation of MEPs from the Civil Liberties Committee will travel to Washington DC for meetings with the US administration and Congress on transatlantic data protection issues, including air passenger data and the SWIFT case. (Monday- Wednesday)
Citizens Forums. The latest in Parliament's meetings enabling members of the public to join the debate on the future of Europe take place in Aachen (Monday) and Stockholm (Thursday).
Preparing for the plenary. Parliament's political groups will devote most of the week to preparations for the Strasbourg plenary of 23-26 April. The main items on the agenda include rules on advanced therapy medicinal products, a package of improvements of maritime safety regulations, a debate with Council and Commission on transatlantic relations and the annual report on human rights around the world. There will also be a formal address from the President of India, Dr A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.
Pre-session press briefing. Any changes to the plenary agenda adopted by the Conference of Presidents will be announced at the usual press briefing at 11am on Friday 20 April.
2007-04-16 Delegations EP Delegation to discuss data protection and CIA renditions in US Congress
The Civil Liberties Committee will send a delegation of 9 MEPs to Washington on 16-18 April to discuss with US Congress current issues linked to the transfer of data for security purposes such as the case of passenger name records (PNR) in transatlantic flights and the share of information contained in international bank transfers (SWIFT case).
MEPs will also brief the members of the US Congress Subcommittee on international organisations on the work undertaken by Parliament's temporary committee investigating CIA illegal activities in Europe, on Tuesday 17 at 2pm (local time)
The 9-Member delegation is made up of:
- Jonathan Evans (EPP-ED, UK), chair of the delegation
- Giusto Catania (GUE/NGL, IT), vice-chair of the Civil Liberties Committee
- Sarah Ludford (ALDE, UK), Vice-chair of the CIA renditions temporary Committee
- Patrick Gaubert (EPP-ED, FR)
- Martine Roure (PES, FR)
- Frieda Brepoels (EPP-ED, BE)
- Luis Herrero-Tejedor (EPP-ED, ES)
- Claudio Fava (PES, IT)
- Sophie In't Veld (ALDE, NL)
Meetings are confirmed with John Bellinger, (Department of State, legal advisor), as well as Congressmen Jerrold Nadler (House Judiciary Committee), Tom Lantos (chairman, House Committee on Foreign Affairs), Loretta Sanchez (expert on terrorism, House Armed Services Committee) and Shelley Berkley (chairwoman, House Committee on Transatlantic Legislator's Dialogue) with several other meetings planned.
A press conference will round off the three days visit, to be held at 12:30 on Wednesday, 18 April in Washington.

09:00 19:00 Washington Other events New European measures to enhance maritime safety Seminar for journalists organised by DG Information
Maritime safety has traditionally been a priority of the European Parliament, especially since the wake of the Erika and Prestige oil-spill accidents. A new package "Third maritime safety package" has already been discussed and voted in the Transport Committee and most reports will be put to the vote of the plenary in April, in first reading.
Boosting the effectiveness of existing safety measures is the key aim of the European Commission's package. It includes dispositions regarding flag states obligations, inspection regime in EU ports, places for refuge of ships in distress, passenger protection norms, insurance obligations, classification societies and civil liabilities issues.
The rapporteurs of these topics are: Marta Vincenzi (PES, IT), Dominique Vlasto (EPP-ED, FR), Dirk Sterckx (ALDE, BE), Paolo Costa (ALDE, IT), Luis de Grandes Pascual (EPP-ED, ES), Gilles Savary (PES, FR) and Jaromir Kohlicek (GUE/NGL, CZ). 15:00 17:30 Brussels, ASP 1E2 2007-04-19 The President's diary .
